MA: Breakheart Reservation and Wiffleball 1

From: Jude Griffin | Date: 2005-02-14 12:02:19 UTC
A beautiful Sunday here in eastern MA. My bf and I headed north of
Boston to Saugus in pursuit of the Breakheart Reservation and
Wiffleball 1 boxes.

The Breakheart box was a perfect example of why letterboxing is so
wonderful. I have driven in that area several times to visit someone
who lives near Petrie's (flower shop across the street from
reservation entrance) and never even noticed the entrance sign
(traffic can be a little hairy on that section of the parkway). So all
this time I had no idea that, in the middle of what I thought was pure
Route 1 urban blight and gaudy roadside hell, there was this
breathtakingly beautiful reservation. Wow!

The clues to the box took us along a paved path that is open part of
the year to traffic. Happily, not this part of the year, and just some
of the pavement was visible for the first mile (second mile was
completely snow and ice-covered). It was a glorious walk among rolling
hills, a little stream weaving along the way, carving snow sculptures
in its path.

Sadly, however, the box itself was completely waterlogged (placer was
contacted). There were a bunch of footsteps near the tree, but I think
these were related to the sledding trails down the little hillside on
which it resides. We covered the box back up and made more misleading
footprints in the snow near other trees.

We then proceeded to finish the loop, which ended at the little lodge.
There we encountered another form of bliss â€“ a roaring fire, two empty
rocking chairs, and chocolate chip cookies laid out. Ahhhh. Thank you
to Bill & Craig for introducing us to this wonderful place.

After that, we went in pursuit of Wiffleball 1 a few miles down the
parkway. This one had the most adorable clues -- though, I must admit,
as a Bear of Very Little Brain, I *never* would have gotten the clue
about "Wiffleball's Green Monster," but happily my bf is blessed with
more discerning brain cells. This box was wet, but the log was dry in
its bag. Cute log book.

And finally it was down the parkway again to Medford to a small
unprepossessing restaurant in Medford Square where the Boston Globe
has twice proclaimed as the source of the best Sichuan food outside of
China: the Chilli Garden. *yum*
