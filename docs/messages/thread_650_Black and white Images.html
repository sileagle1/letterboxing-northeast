Hi there
Just because you can't draw doesn't mean that you can't turn anything into a stamp.
This includes a photograph in color or b&w. You don't need a fancy computer
program or an art degree, in fact- microsoft word actually allows you to format a
picture now as well. You can just click on the image edit options and it will
transform it into either grayscale or black and white.

Here's a good tutorial from Aisling on how to carve and some of the different
methods. http://www.aisling.net/lb/howto/lb-howto1.htm

Once you've found an image, it is very easy to either transfer it from a printed piece
of paper (laser printer) using an iron or by copying over the lines in pencil and
doing a rubbing. I haven't tried it with rubber, but citra-solve (a type of non-toxic
cleaner) works to transfer ink from paper to medium.

I would suggest just using a google "image" search on the web. You can usually
find black and white images of photos/people/places. As a suggestion, put a text
box around something or a border because even if it isn't your original art, you can
still add your own twist to something to make it unique.

Best of luck!
