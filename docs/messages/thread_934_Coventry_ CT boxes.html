Coventry, CT boxes

From: btoesatbarker | Date: 2004-07-06 21:49:13 UTC
Callie and I did several boxes today in Coventry, one of the Mecca's
in CT for boxes. Anyway, we were able to find most of them, but some
were too elusive for us. We could not find The Mary Lemist Titcomb
box behind the library, the third box of When I was a Girl in the
Martin in the Nathan Hale Cemetery, or the second box of The Women of
the Revolutionary War. If anyone has any info on any of these boxes,
please pass it on. We did find the other boxes in these series plus
the CT State Stamp, which is in pieces (I will let the NeeDeeps
know), Lake Wangumbaug, Pumped Up, Mousing in Laidlaw Park,
Constitution State, Coventry Grammar School, Charter Oak's Great-
Grandson, G.H.R., and Uncle Sam's Nephew. All were in good shape
except the CT stamp. Thanks to all who placed all of these great
boxes for us to find on this beautiful summer day! Hope others who
were also looking had a fruitful day.
Queen B

Sandy,
If you happen to be in Provincetown and go to get the Race Point Lighthouse box please remember to leave yourself plenty of time and water. We looked at the lighthouse and thought that it didn't look that far. We went totally unprepared, no sun lotion, flip-flops and NO WATER!!!! 3 1/2 hours latter I had to ask someone for a ride back to my car. WE DID GET THE STAMP! Unless you are driving please know that it's a good hike and go prepared!!
