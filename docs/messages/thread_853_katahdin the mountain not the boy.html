You know, as much as I'd love to see a letterbox up there, it's
really not in tune with the nature of the park. For a place without
running water at the campsite, no paved roads, no pay phones, no
flush toilets, and pets aren't allowed (even if they stay in the car
the whole time, as I understand it), they take the 'leave nature to
its own course' extremely seriously. I've never asked them about
letterboxing or geocaching at Baxter, but I've got a hunch that they
wouldn't approve. And while I might not have much respect for
national parks at times, I'd be hesitant to plant boxes at Katahdin.

First, it's more fun when you don't have to sneak around to do it,
and second, this is the only place I know where nature really comes
first, and I'd love to see it stay that way. Places like national
parks irritate me because they talk a lot about nature, but sheeze,
then they have roads, and bulldozers, and leaf blowers, and, well, it
seems like they're setting the area for tourists to play, not as a
place to protect nature. Baxter I've got to respect a great deal,
because there, tourists are clearly the second-class citizens. Wish
there were more places like that. *sigh*

But hey, hiding boxes along the way to the park, before you actually
enter it, would be a nice. =)

As somebody who treasures the natural landscape in Maine _AND_ is an avidly-
obsessed-ok-addicted-letterboxer, I would like to add my comment to this thread.

First of all, it is a holy land in the sense that people from Maine are able to use this

also cared very deeply for the land.

Secondly, I don't know the land-use issue/argument, I do know that there is some
concern that the Burts Bees conglomerate and the woman that owns it might turn
the entire North Wioods into the next tacky National Park...

All that aside, Baxter is very very special and deserves to be enjoyed by all and
preserved for by all. When I go to Baxter to hike and enjoy nature, letterboxing is
pretty far from my mind.
