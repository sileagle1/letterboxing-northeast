Suzanne, Jonah, and I joined Sparky Butterfly on a bracingly brisk
hike starting at the back lot of MDC Reservoir #6 (West
Harford/Bloomfield) this afternoon, and went 3-for-3 on boxes in
spite of ice and snow! What helped to make them accessible were stone
caves in two cases, and lots of pine needles/boughs in the other! The
snow at #6 was 3-4 inches of powdery stuff on top of ice on the main
trail - several XC ski tracks noted. Jonah rode in his sled for a
portion of the hike - a good strategy for wee ones. We suspect that
many of the other boxes at #6 (having now found all but 2 that we
know of) would also be accessible, given the conditions these three
were under.

Advice for those still looking to box in the ice and snow (much of
this is old hat for most of you, but this is really our first winter
trying it):

1. When choosing targets, look for clues that explicitly state the
box is hidden "in" something - a stump, a hole in a tree, a cave,
etc - less chance of finding a frozen box or damaging a box if you
have to dig.

2. STAY WARM (or stay inside)!!!!!! We were only out for 90 minutes
at about 18 degrees F, and relatively protected by trees from the
wind; I'm not sure I would have wanted Jonah (let alone me or
Suzanne!) out there more than another 30 minutes, tops. I was sorry I
didn't bring any of those nifty hand-warmer packets sold just about
everywhere (saw them cheap at Bob's Stores in Simsbury today).

3. Bring yellow-dyed water or a dog for hiding your tracks' real
boxing intentions - make yellow snow! (Or, where allowable, use the
real thing - not so good on the shores of a public water source,
though!)

4. If you XC ski or snowshoe - bring 'em! (Just be careful when
counting paces or steps!) Consider sleds, too!

5. If you haven't gone to MDC #6 in West Hartford (as opposed to its
well-populated neighbor on Rte 4), think about it, because there are
a decent number of well-placed, well-clued letterboxes there
(including a HH hostel)!

Stay warm!!!!

Dan (on behalf of the other 2 sleeping members of)
