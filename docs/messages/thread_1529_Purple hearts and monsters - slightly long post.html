Purple hearts and monsters - slightly long post

From: Jim Plouffe | Date: 2004-10-30 01:12:46 UTC
I remember some time back a string of messages about purple hearts for
letterboxers who take mis-steps. Now, what about the vehicles we drive
in? Could they be awarded a purple heart? Okay a story about my
adventures in Mansfield going after more of the monsters. First, a
question to all.... what is the most expense that your letterboxing
adventures cost you?

Well, after going through the Mixed Monster Series at the Lions Memorial
Park, I hopped into my Mits Eclipse. My wife had stayed back in
Massachusetts (about 90 miles) because she had an exam in one of her
classes. Well, as I was exiting the parking area, my front left
ball-joint broke and my front left wheel almost fell all the way off.
WORD TO THE WISE: AAA PLUS IS A GRAND IDEA! Well, as it is, I only
have the basic AAA. All in all, it didn't turn out too bad. I called
my wife. She luckily was done with her exam and she came down to get
me. Well, Murphy struck again. The auto mechanic right there in
Mansfield Center did a great job of fixing replacing my ball joint in
just under 1 1/2 hours. Therefore, my wife drove all the way down there
for no reason. But she did get the Attack of the Tomatoes and four of
the Doctor Who series before it got so dark (my flashlight was left in
my car which was still being fixed) that we had to bush-whack through
the woods to get to the street before we got lost. Hopefully I can
convince the carver of that series to send me the remaining three stamps
as we did go by the stamps at warp speed to get to the street.

Well, the damage was just over $230 for the repairs. Not as bad as I
thought it was going to be when I hit dirt with the front of my car.

One last thing before I bore you all to sleep: Every person I ran into
in Mansfield, be it the tow truck driver, the people at the general
store or just the people I passed on the trails were absolutely friendly
and pleasant. Not only is it beautiful and peaceful down there, but the
friendly people make it a place that I'd go again just for the heck of it.

Hope everyone got more of the monsters than I did,

Blax
