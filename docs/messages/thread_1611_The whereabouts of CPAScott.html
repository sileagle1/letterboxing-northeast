From: cpascott | Date: 2004-11-17 18:46:33 UTC
Hey fellow NE letterboxers...

Some of you may have wondered where I've been lately. I'm still
around, although I've been on a hiatus from letterboxing the last 5 or
so weeks. As I shared with some of you at the Mystery Gathering,
Anjabanja and I are no longer a couple. It's a sad thing but both us
realized that the relationship had gone as far as it could and it was
time to part ways. We've expressed our interest in remaining friends,
and have stayed in touch somewhat, but only time will tell whether
things can successfully transform.

Anja was my companion and best friend for the time we were together
and when I happened upon letterboxing last May she was the first I
told about it. We started participating in this hobby together and
although we didn't use a team name we were very much a team.

When the relationship came to an official end, I lost my motivation to
continue the hobby for a while. This is principally because I am the
type of person who finds significantly more enjoyable in doing things
with someone else thus being able to share experiences rather than do
them alone. Matter-of-fact, I am usually not interested in doing
things by myself. As much as I truly enjoy letterboxing, I could not
see myself enjoying it as much if it were only me on the trails.

I do not intend to abandon this hobby altogether, and may try to
engage in it solo hoping that I'll find it just as fun as I did when I
was hunting boxes with Anja. Hopefully, it will only be a matter of
little time before I find another partner whom I can share this
wonderful hobby with. And, who knows, perhaps Anja and I will get
together occasionally to hunt a few boxes.

I'll be heading back to my boyhood home (the Finger Lakes section of
NYS) this weekend for a long Thanksgiving holiday with my dad, and I
think I'll take the clues to various boxes in the area with me.
Perhaps hunting those boxes will prove that I can enjoy this hobby
solo -- we'll see. When I return to MA, I don't know if I'll return
to actively letterboxing or not.

So, my hiatus from this hobby may last a while, but I assure those of
you to whom I've made your acquaintence over the past five months that
I will return at some point. And, if, in the meantime, any of you who
live near me want another member of your hunting party some weekend
day, send me a message and maybe I'll join you.

I'll be updating my website at some point to remove much of the
journal that is there as well as strike Anja's stats from the page.
Even though we split on amicable terms and will try to remain friends,
I don't feel comfortable maintaining the "Letterboxing Adventures of
Scott and Anja" when only Scott remains. Anja has kept her journal
and stamp, though, so you may see her continue to participate in this
hobby.

The two boxes (Red Horse and Mary's Lamb) that were placed by Anja and
I will remain for letterboxers to hunt and I will maintain them. The
clues will continue to be available on my website. The third box
(Willard Clock) was in the process of being put together when the
relationship stopped; placement of this box has been indefintely
postponed.

I'm not completely sure why I felt compelled to air my story here on
these boards, but perhaps it is because I feel a part of this
community and, as part of the community consider my fellow
letterboxers friends that care enough to wonder "what happened to
CPAScott?" So, here I am to answer that question.

We shall speak again.

Fondest regards,
CPAScott
