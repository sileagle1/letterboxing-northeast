JBean:

Don't know the trail color but you go past the main entrance heading East and take like the second left to the end of the road where there is a trail head. This trail borders the flat horse trail for a while and I think it's along the back of the giant. You'll find a series with birds that were fairly flat, with slow inclines that should work. We did that with Funhog in June in 95 degree weather and the Hog's foot in a cast thingie. At work so I don't have a trail map or box names.

From: NLS | Date: 2005-08-18 15:36:49 UTC
HI Ho JBean
Now you are talking, The Sleeping Giant is a great place for all ages.
If you have never been, and have children in your group, you should by
all means take the Tower Trail up to the tower. There are boxes at the
top as well as along the way. There is no climbing involved, and a
wonderful adventure at the top. I have been hiking children up the
tower trail for over 50 years and every time it was a fun hike with
smiling faces. I am sure with the map you have and the clues you pull
off, you will be able to catch quite a few lettrboxes not far off the
Tower trail on the other color trail. I would suggest you go to the
tower first , get those boxes there and as you start down, pick up the
others you might have energy for.
My two cents.
Have fun
Butterfly
