It is such a tough call...should it be only a word of mouth hobby or
public knowledge.
I learned about letterboxing in May of last year when I good friend of
mine took me to the May Day gathering. If it wasn't for her then I
would never have found out about it. So word of mouth is good!
But the media has already found out about our hobby and published it
so there isn't a whole lot we can do to stop the spread. Like someone
else mentioned it could be bad, if boxes start getting trashed and or
stolen, but it can be good because it could also bring out some GREAT
carvers and placers.
I have been on the hunt before and found a box completly trashed and I
recovered what I could. So I'm sure the vandalism isn't all because of
the media. It will happen to some boxes no matter what you do. We just
need to become more inventive concerning our clues and think "outside
the box"(no pun intended) when we plant them.
I am up for the challenge.
