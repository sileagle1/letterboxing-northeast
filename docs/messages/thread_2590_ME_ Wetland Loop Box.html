From: Phyto | Date: 2005-11-23 01:04:18 UTC
Hi folks,
This is mostly directed to Choi, or others that might know of this
particular box. If you're not familiar with what I'm talking about,
just disregard. I don't usually read this list anymore, let alone
check the "what's new" but I happened to check the list and noticed
that there was a new listing for Wetland Loop.
http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=19385&boxname=Wetland_Loop

Well, that's kinda funny because I originally planted a box in the
SAME location about 2 years ago for the Water District. This was a box
that you need to ask permission for since the Portland Water District
property is private and there is restricted access around Sebago Lake
which is also a drinking water source. I'm quite puzzled to see that
there is a listing for essentially the SAME clue that I wrote...
http://home.gwi.net/~moxie1/Clues/WetlandLoopTrail.html

I'm puzzled and a bit bummed out, why don't people pay attention when
they plant boxes? Maine is not Connecticut folks, we don't need 50
boxes in the same location, on the SAME trail. Thankfully I am still
in good contact with the Education director at the Ecology Center and
will make her aware of this error in judgement.

Sorry, a bit disgruntled...
phyto
x-posted to LBNA

From: Phyto | Date: 2005-11-24 15:45:17 UTC
Thanks Trish,
I actually received an email from the placer today. She is an educator
from the water district who apparently didn't do her homework before
giving away the location for my box. The concept of my clue was to
hint at the location to obtain the next clue which leads you to my
box. What she posted on the web was the next clue (!)???? So, anyway -
to make a long story short, I gave her the lodown.

Looks like she just got back from a workshop on questing, but they
clearly didn't edumacate her on letterboxing and the difference
betwixt the two. Egad, yet another thing to blabber on about, but I
appreciate you stepping in.

Isosceles had a very valid point today. To the untrained boxer, aka-
"newbie", the LBNA site is quite simplistic. If you're willing to go
the mile and scratch beneath the surface, there is another layer to
the onion. The mystery clues, the bonus boxes never published, the
multitudes of different clue writing styles and all the wonderful
eccentricities of boxing that originally attracted me to this hobby. I
sometimes get sad when I think of all the other good stuff people miss.

phyto
