RI Sit Rep - Blade Runner Series

From: Warrior Woman | Date: 2004-10-11 20:47:06 UTC
Just wanted to give you an update on my 7 box Blade Runner Series in
Westerly, RI.

(hangs head in shame)

My name is Warrior Woman. I blatantly admit that I haven't done box
maintenance in this series since it was placed in July 2003.

I received an email indicating that the series was in poor shape. I
even got a sodden log book in the mail.

I went out yesterday (Sunday October 10) to do a box maintenance on
all 7 boxes.

The Good News:

All the boxes were there.

The Bad News:

All the boxes were there lying in plain sight, some with the
containers open.

Also, two landmarks (a junked blue car and a white Wildlife
Restoration sign) were missing.

During the walk down the trails, I found all the parts of the junked
blue car. I hauled 'em all back to where they are "supposed" to be
according to my clues ;-)

Also, on an extreme outlying trail, I found on the ground a discarded
white Wildlife Restoration sign. I taped it to the tree where the
old one had fallen (got ripped?) off.

So, both clues are back in place.

All the boxes now have been cleaned thoroughly, new paper towels and
baggies, new inkpads where needed, and in two case, new log books.

My anal-retentive teenaged daughter was so upset about how the boxes
were found that she demanded the task of rehiding them well.

(skips in glee - I've raised a responsible letterboxer!)

Anyhow - I just wanted to report that this particularly out of the
way 7 box series is now in tip top shape, the clue landmarks have
been restored, and the trees are turning *beautiful* colours in this
area. It's worth the ride.

I came out of the trail with two (count 'em - TWO) FULL black
trashbags - and -

NOT ONE HITCHIKER!

But the satisfaction lies in the fact that I left the entire trail
system better than how I found it, and all the boxes are now
*perfectly* in order.

Warrior Woman
