Nature center directors

From: birder579 | Date: 2004-12-02 13:59:17 UTC
I know and have worked with several nature center directors in the
Connecticut area for many years. All have been supporters of
letterboxing when I explain the hobby to them. One of them is
planning to put out his own series of boxes in his park to get the
activity going there.

A couple of them have mentioned to me that boxes have been placed in
their parks without their permission being asked. They feel this is
impolite at best.

Folks, we can't afford to generate any bad feelings for our hobby.
We depend on the good will and openness of the parks and directors
for our hobby to flourish. They can be our biggest supporters.
Many directors have integrated letterboxing into their center
programs, planted their own, taught it as an activity in their
classes. As is human nature, they will be sharing their experiences
with other directors at other centers. We want them to be sharing
good experiences. This is one of the most important ways for the
good news of letterboxing to be spread.

I know there have been stories of placers who have been called to
dance on the carpet, and not all have been well received. I know it
is risky to talk with "management" or "the authorities". But these
people are directors because they like out of doors activities just
like us. They can be part of our community.

Please, if you have not done so, go to your local park or nature
center director and tell them about letterboxing and the boxes you
have planted. If need be, say that you thought it is harmless and
you didn't know what their reaction would be. We need these people
on our side. If they give a negative response, pull your boxes. We
don't want to be in an area where we are not wanted. There will be
other areas where we will be welcomed.
