Pulled after the flood

From: Bell Lady | Date: 2007-04-26 17:06:14 UTC
Checking on my boxes near the Farmington River after all our rain and
flooding, I have pulled these boxes for maintenance since they were
quite wet:
Amistad (was found floating at the edge of the river)
Aqueduct (had been reported wet by Celtic Roots and Astrii - I
appreciate their First Aid efforts)

I also picked up the logbook of rtrw's Avon box in Fisher Meadow since
it was soaked and had no stamp with it. Dried it out and returned it
to her today.
I was trying to check on my Towpath box there but the water was still
too high for me to reach it.

Amistad and Aqueduct will be returned in the near future but the clues
may be changed.

Bell Lady

From: Hillel | Date: 2007-04-27 15:16:35 UTC
I haven't gotten out boxing in way too long. I have had a lot going on in my life, so before last week it had been since September since I went out. It felt great to finally get back out there after so long. Last week I did the "Raggedy Ann and Andy and the Wishing Well" series in Vernon, CT and yesterday I did the "Rainbow Row" series in Manchester, CT.

In addition I finally got around to planting the "Ship it" series I carved over a year ago. Its in the Nike Recreational Area, the same trail that I used to have "The Great Race" series. Here are the clues for those interested:

http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=30398&boxname=Ship_It

http://www.atlasquest.com/lboxes/showboxinfo.html?gBoxId=38516

Hopefully things will stay calm for a little while so I can get a lot more boxing done this summer. See you guys on the trail
