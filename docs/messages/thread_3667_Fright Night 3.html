Re: Fright Night 3

From: Heidi | Date: 2007-10-30 00:10:45 UTC
Hear, Hear!

There was SO MUCH going on at FN3, it was impossible to see and do
everything! If you couldn't make it, you missed some incredible
creative activities planned by the most ambitious of event
organizers... here's a sampling!

*Walking into the event, being greeted by the Kokopellian himself,
followed by the 7+ foot tall Jack Skellington (Mr. Muddy Paws)!

*Picking up the expertly created color clue brochure created by
GollyGee (who was unfortunately not present on Saturday) and
realizing that there was NO WAY that all those boxes could possibly
be found in one day!

*Seeing a room full of costumed folks who had come from far and wide
to celebrate together, and the mad frenzy of exchanging taking
place. The air got really charged as one after another, the mystery
guests were revealed...is that the Deadly Letterboxer, or was it
David from Team NH? What? Someone was KUKU enough to fly out this
way from Oregon? OMG, is that Nancy Drew (aka Amanda from Seattle)
and Green Tortuga (aka Ryan)? I am sure I missed many others, my
apologies, but the Word Wraith was busy Scrabbling letters to
attempt to defend her title...

*Gamers, take your Stations! Rattle dem Bones with Wildcat
Dave...Viva Lost Vegas with Travelers4...spin the Wheel of
Misfortune with Vanna and Jack (MuddyPaws)...Toss those rings on the
Gravestones with Cooties...and a working Zultan machine?

*Is that Orion and Ron setting up a studio for another Frightful
Video Podcast???

*What are we eating? Is it Kitty Litter or dessert? At least no
flaming lanterns were encountered in the search for the spookiest
culinary dish.

*Time to head out on a road adventure? I'm not ready to leave yet!
Go Go GO!!! Had to detour for Scrapbooking and Coffee, but the
trail of terror continued through the town of Gorham, ME. Were
there boxes at the domicile of the Kokopellian? It was raining so
hard that I missed them! Onward to our final destination...at the
MK family lands where a welcoming Bonfire awaited with fire-roasted
New England Red Hotdogs!

*The Haunted Trail!!! The most incredibly haunted letterboxing
jaunt ever...a most ghoulish marvel of engineering created by Giddy
and the Muddy Paws did not disappoint! Cooties spaced out the
groups so that each group had a maximum spooky experience!

On this warm humid night with a full moon overhead, the ambiance
could not have been better. We started off down the muddy track not
knowing what to expect...glow sticks? Reflective tape? Nope. How
about a tented maze lit by blacklight? Yup. A full-sized crashed
vehicle with a glowing skeleton at the wheel? Yup. A collapsed
crypt with a stamp inside a funky lit skull? Check. Full-sized
scarecrows...being led into the woods by creepy moaning
sounds...encountering Jack Skellington's eerily glowing brain...the
field of lit Jack-o-Lanterns? The most amazing crypt-keeper's
playground...how about encountering a freshly dug grave complete
with flaming lantern post, headstone, and having to dig for the
box? The adventure continues...possible death by falling in a mine
shaft...or death by fright at the antics of the Kokopellian! How
about crossing a bridge over lava? The steam was pretty frightful.
And if all that wasn't enough, last stop...the Cemetery! This was
the creme-de la creme of the ghoulish adventure: gravestones, eerie
colors glowing in the fog...skeletons haunting the graves, spiders,
floating ghouls! Yikes, where's the box? IN THE COFFIN!!! With
the red-glowing skeleton!!!

Did we survive? Barely. This was the most creative letterboxing
effort I have ever seen!

Thank you to all of you CRAZY BOXERS!!! Truly, it was an amazing
day (or 3, depending on how crazy you were).

SapphireBerry (aka Word Wraith)
