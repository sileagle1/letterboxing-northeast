From: gingerbreadjunk | Date: 2006-06-22 03:04:43 UTC
it has come to my attention that the distruction of white sands has
begun. it was never my plan on putting any one is danger when i
planted a box there. if some of you remember, i took time out of my
busy life, to plan a clean up at white sands. the several times i have
gone down there, I have noticed the place is slowly going down hill.
what bothers me the most is, the fact that people form the LB
community as well as pembroke itself, came down to help clean up an
area that "yes" will become trashed again, and for some, may never see
that location ever again. it pisses me off that my time and others
time has now been wasted.

i plan on fighting. i plan on making a difference. i plan on showing
everyone who is boss. white sands is a beautiful place and i hope that
someday, everyone can enjoy the beauty that i have seen once upon a
time.

pardon my frustration, but "@#*&$ &%#@!*"

I hear you loud and clear. I know of other places that are beautiful and
isolated that have become "off limits" because of the hangout syndrome. The
solution is for the site to become more heavily used by legits. If folks find
that they are intimidated or are putting themselves at risk, it makes sense to me
that law enforcement, forestry, and decent folks need to follow your fine
example, maintain the area and increase the traffic. Someone earlier posted that
they are not good looking etc but that younger, more attractive people might
be in jeopardy--that is not the way sick minds work. Looks don't matter and
age doesn't either. Gender might not matter either. If someone is sensing
danger, we all ought to take care of ourselves and then, take care of the
problem.

My family has been going to a waterfall in Maine for 60 years or more and
starting with my father, we plan to leave it better than we found it. We take
trashbags and fill them with BW cans and other unpleasant litter. We have not
prevented the parties, but we leave it beautiful for the next folks 3 or 4
times each summer. Recently, I was pleased to hear that my half-brother's stepson
and his family are also carrying on this tradition. So then it's more like 8
summer cleanup visits. It's like we adopted this one spot. We care about
all the pretty places and we clean as we go, but this waterfall is a special
place. As long as we can get there, we will continue. Wouldn't it be great if
places could be up for adoption by families, clubs and other groups like
letterboxes can be adopted? Last year, the wheeler group that I belong to got a
grant to clean up a piece of wild land that had become a car dump. It was very
satisfying to see the land cleaned up. I wonder if we could petition judges
to assign some of these places as public service alternative sentencing
options? Hmmm. The picnic area, the end of Northstreet, Riverbank Park, mile 73 of
the interstate....

Good luck and be hopeful--stewardship is an aspect of character that
developes from your example.
