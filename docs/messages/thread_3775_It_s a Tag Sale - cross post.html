From: al06360 | Date: 2008-04-08 12:13:28 UTC
I've "carved" 4 stamps so far. I have to use a CNC router since I don't
have the skill to freehand carve even though I'm a cabinet maker. That
said, I have to start with picture, scan it into the computer and
painstakingly redraw it in Adobe Illustrator so it's a format my
machine can use. Then I have to setup the machine, then it runs,
carves the stamp and then I have to use an exacto to get rid of the
tiny bits.

To make a long story short, Each custom stamp would cost about $300 at
our current shop rate which is clearly crazy. The up side is that once
the art is done and the machine is setup, I can produce the 2nd and the
next 1000 for pennies. But I don't see the market for 1000 identical
stamps.

And doing it on the CNC router will never yield the fine details of a hand
carved stamp. Even with the 1/32" bit I use. If it could, then the US Mint
would not produce note and coin plates by hand. They still carve by hand.

I was looking into a system that uses a photographic process similar to silk
screen production.
There was about a $2,000 start up cost for the ultraviolet "oven" and the
related materials. If there was a market it could pay for itself fairly
quickly. The down side to that process was the need to gang up about 10-12
stamps to efficiently use the stock size of the stamp material. That could
cause delays in orders being shipped.

I wonder if there are any YouTube videos about this topic?

Re: Stamping for hire?

From: Wisconsin Hiker | Date: 2008-04-09 01:35:15 UTC
I forwarded your post to another boxer in the Great Lakes region and
here is his reply:
=========================================
I'm not a member of the Northeast board, but Wisconsin Hiker
forwarded your Email to me.

I've talked with her before about possibly selling some of my stamps,
or doing stamps for hire basis. This will give you some idea of my
work, http://hartx6carving.blogspot.com/

I figure I don't want to get in over my head and have order after
order to fill (as that would take the joy out of carving and make it
a chore), but it would be nice to make a little money to put back
into my "Letterboxing kitty".

I figure for my stamps I would have a base price of $5 then $1 dollar
for every hour I worked on the stamp... plus shipping.

All of the "portraits" I have pictured on my site are 2.5" x 1.5" and
take me between 5 - 10 hours to carve. So with a base price of $5 +
$1 per hour one of the portraits would run someone between $15 - $20
per stamp.

This may sound a bit high, but look at what they charge for a rubber
stamp at Michaels. I feel this is a reasonable price.

Oh, and make sure they know that this being a hand carved stamp may
not be *perfect* every stamp I've carved there is something I wish I
did differently, some small infraction . . . well, what they pay for
is what you give them, so long as it is acceptable to you.

Hart x6
