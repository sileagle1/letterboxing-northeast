Hi Northeast boxers:

As some of you may have read on our webpage during the past 6 months,
one of our dreams has been boxing at Dartmoor. I have previously
received quite a few emails of interested parties who would take an
organized trip with us after I posted such.

This is what I know so far:

Mid to late September works best for Sue and I [plus it's our 30th
Anniversary and wanted to take a big trip.] The weather in England in
September is similar to our September weather, not too hot - not too
cold.

Boxing at Dartmoor is much different than from the US. Clues are
based on maps with coordinates and with over 10,000 boxes located
there we can really expand on our F's! We are open to ideas, like
leaving a bunch of letterboxes there. I have thought about an evening
gathering with some local letterboxers at a pub and maybe spending
the next day boxing with the experts - in fact - that might be a good
way to begin the week. Members of the Dartmoor Yahoo talk list have
offered to organize such an event for us if we want. Their advice
would truly prove invaluable.

London's Heathrow Airport is about 2.5 hours away from Dartmoor -
there are 2 other closer airports [Exeter & Plymouth] but I'm not
sure what kind of deals will be available for those more obscure
airports. I will check on that in the coming weeks. We are a member
of Bestfares.com and there is a Bradley Airport to Heathrow special
running through April for $368 round trip. You have to be a member of
this website but I am willing to coordinate that so we can all
achieve the best deal if they extend this offer through the fall. I
would think otherwise $500 per person should be a good budgetary
starting point for airfare {I probably was a travel agent in a past
life!]

I have noticed that if we have 10 or more people there will be a
group discount possibly on airfare and surely on accommodations. Our
plan is to stay 1 week [either 6 or 7 nights.] Spend most of the week
letterboxing [either full or half days] and allowing a day or 2 for
local sightseeing, pubs, museums, pubs, maybe a day in London, and
some pubs!!! Our daily itinerary can be left up to the majority
wishes of the group.

There are 2 major ways we can have accommodations:
1] Bed & Breakfast - private rooms with full English breakfast and
overnight runs approx. $50 - $60 US for the night per person based on
double occupancy. You singles may want to pair up with another
single. That can be planned later. Some B & B's even offer an upgrade
for full 4 to 5 course meals for supper and the option to pack a
bagged lunch that we can take with us while exploring the moors.

2]Self catering - which means you rent a cottage with full kitchen
and bedrooms but WE do the cooking. That would save us quite a bit of
money but do we want to have breakfast ready for us in the AM? I
found a great spot on the internet that was a former horse stable,
made of brick dating to the 1800's. It has been transformed into
quaint "cottages" with full kitchens, etc. There are 2 that will
sleep 2 people, 1 that sleeps 4, another that sleeps 6 and another
that sleeps 8 to 12.

All of the accommodations to which I am referring are located either
in or just outside of Darmoor Park. My concern is to save traveling
time from our homebase to the letterboxes!!

That's about all I have worked up so far. In the next week I plan to
update our webpage with a new subpage for this trip along with some
links to Dartmoor websites, B & B's, etc. This is still in the
planning stages and not quite definite yet, but I thought this would
be a good time to get things started.

Please email me if you are interested. I believe we can do the
complete trip for about $1000 to 1200 per person including airfare
and accommodation and if we choose a B & B, that would include full
breakfast and possibly dinner. Spending money, additional meals of
course would exceed that amount.

If you are interested and the dates work for you, please email me at:
mjpepe1@...

I assume that Bradley Airport in CT is a central location and will
continue to look at airfare from there.

Looking forward to hearing from all of you soon.


Have fun and just get out there and [Darmoor] box!!!


Mark & Sue Pepe

You can talk to a travel agent who specializes in trips to England to
help you with the details but I do know enough to tell you that you
want to fly into one of the London airports, preferably Heathrow. If
you fly out of Boston (which really is not that far, just take one of
the Logan Express buses in) you have your choice of a morning or
evening non-stop to Heathrow on a few different airlines. Flights
there go down to under $300 (including all taxes, etc) from Nov to
March. You will still be paying a high rate if you go in Sep (as
much as twice).

If you decide not to drive then you can take the Heathrow Express
into Paddington Station (think Paddington Bear), which coincidentally
is the station that the trains for that part of England depart from
(the Great Western line).

If you decide to drive, remember that it is on the "wrong" side of
the road and you will be driving a standard car. Automatics are 2 -
3x as expensive to rent. Driving in the remote parts of England is
definitely an experience.
