From: enologistct | Date: 2006-07-11 16:25:43 UTC
OK. I've been pretty quiet up until now, but I feel as though I
really need to say this....

Please DO NOT put the locations of mystery boxes in clues for
traditional letterboxes! It is not fair to the planter of the
mystery box, since the planter either wanted it to be a difficult
mystery, or didn't want too much traffic to the box, as it may
compromise the box.

I've contacted the placer of the trad. letterbox, asking him to
delete the small section of clues pertaining to the mystery box.
The placer didn't need to add that information to make the clues
work, and all it did was compromise another box.

OK... I'm through venting now. BTW..... if I don't hear back from
the planter, can whomever on the LBNA site delete the three or four
words from the clues? Please let me know if this can be done.

Thank you for listening.
Enologist

i would like to add to this. not only should clues to a mystery box not be
mentioned in regular clues they should not be mentioned anywhere that the
placer themselves have not either given permission or listed. recently i was
at a box and in the logbook was a reference to a mystery of mine. the
location was given innocently enough but it was still there, the location
named and the fact that it was a mystery. mysteries should not be labled as
to location in your personal logbook either. you never know who might be
looking at it.
