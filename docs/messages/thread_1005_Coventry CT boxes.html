Well, with the help of Music Woman today, I was able to verify that
two of the boxes that I had searched for two weeks ago are indeed
missing. Number 2 box of The Women of the Revolutionary War is MIA
and The Mary Lemist Titcomb box is also MIA. Music Woman was there to
help me find the number 3 box of When I was a girl in the Martin. I
still had the old clues. She also led me in the correct direction for
Stars and Stripes Forever, which has the incorrect degree reading.
Another group that she helped with is the Watership Downs series. I
could not find the yellow arrow, and was unsure as to which trail to
head out on, but she knew just where to look! I then followed her to
Caprilands and we both got that stamp. I should have asked her to
stay with me when searching for the Red Bird of the Red Bird and
Skungamaug River boxes. I looked all through the pricker bushes
(ouch!!!) and just could not find it, but did find the other one. But
between today and yesterday, I was able to find BenE, Eternal Love,
Kind of Corney, all 8 of the John Wayne Series, Presidential Animals,
Glass Factory, and If Wishes Were Horses. The only one with anything
to report about is Eternal Love: the stamp is in two pieces, but
still useable. I want to thank Music Woaman again for all her help
today! Could not have done it without you!!!
