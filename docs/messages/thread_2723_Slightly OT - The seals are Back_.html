A small update Re: Slightly OT - The seals are Back!

From: trishkri | Date: 2006-03-06 17:05:45 UTC
No letterboxing yesterday but we did make a quick trip to Rome Point
to see the seals. We were lucky enough to run into a very kind
gentleman with a spotting scope who allowed us to get a great view of
the seals. With the naked eye (or cub scout binoculars) we had
estimated that there were about 12 - 16 seals on the rocks. The very
knowledgeable gentleman with the scope said that he had counted 108!
It was amazing to realize how many seals were sunning when he let us
use his scope. Apparently this is one of the best pleces in all the
northeast to see the seals and he has spotted over 130 on a previous
ocassion. They are predominantly harbor seals but sometimes a grey
seal can be seen.

His tips for good seal spotting are to go on a sunny, winter day about
an hour or two before low tide to just after low tide. We got there
about one hour before yesterday.

Trish
