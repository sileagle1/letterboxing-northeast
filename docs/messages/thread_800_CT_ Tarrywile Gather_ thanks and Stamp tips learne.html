CT: Tarrywile Gather, thanks and Stamp tips learned

From: T Prindle | Date: 2004-06-09 12:29:23 UTC
We also wanted to thank Alan and all the other people that made the
gather last weekend such a great success!

We went early and volunteered with the Trail Maintenance, the choices
were blazing, clearing a blow-down, or building a step-stone bridge
over an old pond spillway.... who wouldn't opt for playing in the
water? Actually it mainly consisted of hauling rocks and dirt, but
many hands made for light work and the Park people rewarded us with
a bag lunch and a free T-shirt (which came in handy to change into
after the bridge building!)

At about noon we headed back to the Barn to see if other boxers had
arrived, and met a Koyote on the trail (did anyone else actually make
it to the beacon besides you guys?). As our first event, not
knowing what to expect, we had a great time throughout the afternoon
exchanging stamps and talking with everyone, learning a million new
things.

I'd been transferring my laser-printed images via the acetone method
(nail polish remover) and hadn't tried the other 'heat' (using an
iron) method. I just tried the heat method (on medium heat, keep it
moving over the paper) on my latest transfer on the pink speedball
and it worked awesomely, much less sticky and much better detail than
acetone!! so thank you Mapsurfer for recommending that!

I also tried plexiglass as a backing, which also worked great! Alan
said that's what they use in Dartmoor. Plexiglass is easy to cut to
size by scoring with a mat knife and snapping along the score mark.
I have an old fashioned 'Corner Rounder' to round off the corners.
When the carving was complete, I heat transfered a 'flipped' version
of the image on the back of the pink side and glued plexiglass over
it - worked great, and makes for a nice thin stamp to fit into boxes.

So thanks again for the good times, new friends and great event
stamps (how many subtle donut hints did it take us!?!)

Rush Gatherer and Powwow Dancer

p.s. special thanks to the night boxing brigade behind us that
rescued us at that "right or left at the bridge" delima!
