Hello all,
I showed this post to my husband (Culex), and he wanted to respond to it:
Hi, um just a little correction to the post about ticks. Working with
Lyme disease in Maine, I can say that if folks are finding any ticks
before May in New England, they are probably deer ticks. My lab has
begun seeing some out and about. The deer ticks out now and in the fall
are adults, about the size of an apple seed, and can most certainly
cause Lyme disease. Females are red & black while the males are jet
black to the naked eye. Deer tick nymphs are the ones that are "the size
of the head of a pin," and usually show up June - mid August. The big
ticks found in the summer with white on the back are dog (or wood) ticks
and are the carriers of Rocky Mountain Spotted-Fever, which has
occasionally shown up in Mass and CT. The point about applying Vapo Rub
is a bad idea. That and trying to burn a tick with a match can actually
cause the tick to regurgitate its gut contents back into you before it
pulls out. That might some nasty juices flowing the wrong way! Using a
very fine pair of forceps (tweezers) to gently pull the tick back out is
the best way. Be patient, deer ticks have a barbed beak and yanking
might result in the head and beak staying in your skin while the body
comes away.
Thanks for putting up with a long-winded public health message. My lab's
website is http://zappa.mmcri.mmc.org/lyme/lymehome.html

Happy hunting!
-Culex & Nautilus
