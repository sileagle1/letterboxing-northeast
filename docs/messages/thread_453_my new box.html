If it requires an actual honest-to-goodness hike to get to, it's
very normal for it not to get many visitors. For whatever reason
(lack of time, lack of physical fitness, or even 'power boxers' that
want lots of boxes in as minimal amount of time possible), most
people won't look for a box that's not quick and easy.

I've hidden boxes on hikes that take less than an hour to do, and
they might get one visit per YEAR. Astonishing, really. And for
those boxes I've hidden that take three or four hours to do, they've
gone for two years or more without a visit!

Anyhow, if you want your boxes to get lots of visitors, you'll have
to hide them closer to roads. But I hope you leave it where it is.
There's a severe lack of letterboxes on serious hikes for those few
of us (such as myself) that prefer them to the drive-bys.
Eventually somebody will get out to find it, but it could take
awhile.

-- Ryan

Hi Hikerlou!

I would recommend that you not expect hoards of people to come to your box
right away. If you have such high expectations, you may find yourself
disappointed. Some boxes are heavily visitied (several people a month), others can go a
year or more with out a single visitor, especially tough mystery boxes. When
we sought out one particular mystery, we were the first ones to find it, and it
had been there for a year already!

Sure, there are folks who rush right out to a new box, because they like to
be the first finder, but a lot of us plan our letterboxing trips more casually
than that. It sounds like your box is in a great spot. A half hour is not too
far to go for lots of people. I think you have done exactly what you should
do....let people know it is there and invite them to visit it.

You know how the saying goes..."if you plant it, they will come".

Just remember that southern New England has literally thousands of boxes to
choose from. CSCM and I are particularly focusing on the older ones, so that we
can get them before they are retired or go missing. I know there are some
others doing that too.

So don't worry, they will come...just maybe not as quickly as you hoped.

Cheers!
-Kim (rustypuff)

From: Steve and Heidi | Date: 2004-03-30 21:46:02 UTC
Very good point! Our "Gray Rock Extras" has had minimal visitors in
the past year and a half. Outside of the 'usual suspects', we've seen
maybe 8 or 10 visitors. Compare this to one of our drive-up-easy-
access-to-I-95 boxes and you will see that in about 8 months we've
had 40 or 50 entries into the easier box. We've even had to make a
new logbook b/c the old one filled up in about 2 months.

I'm not sure if its reflective of the state of letterboxing in CT,
however it seems like boxes in the hot spots like Rocky Neck, Haley
Farm, Mansfield, Goodwin, etc get far more attention and there are
far more boxes in those areas. Of course on the flip side these parks
offer some of the more difficult hikes (thinking of Ants Marching in
Goodwin).

Lou - Give it time to settle. Also keep in mind that some people take
pride in putting out boxes that no one CAN find! Chalk it up to a
good box location and the idea that the few people that come get your
stamp image are the lucky ones. We treasure some of the seldom found
dilemmas in this state.

-Steve
