Xpost: Had to call the state police while boxing yesterday....

From: mindizney | Date: 2006-01-25 22:54:57 UTC
OK.... I can safely say that I have NEVER had this happen to me.....

I was going North on Route 201 in North Stonington yesterday, going
around a curve, and have to come to a SCREECHING halt! Why you
ask??????

Well, there was a horse in the road. And it wouldn't move. Since
I'm afraid of horses, I wasn't going to get out of the car. And the
horse was begging to get hit by a car. Every once in a while, the
horse would go over to the side of the road and graze, and when a
car came whizzing by, it would freak out and run BACK INTO the
street. Well, I couldn't let that happen, so I called 911.

The conversation went something like this: "911 What is your
emergency?"

"Well officer, there's a horse in the road."

"A horse in the road."

"Yes, and it grazes on the side of the road, and whenever a car goes
whizzing by, it freaks out and runs back into the road."

"Where's your location?"

"Route 201 in North Stonington. Would you like a house #?"

"No. I'll have to patch you into State Police." (patch happens)

"State Police, what's your emergency."

"There's a horse in the road."

"A Horse in the road?"

"Yes."

"I see that you're on Route 201. Are you on the North Stonington
side or Griswold side?" (I guess they come from different barracks
or something)

"I don't know."

"Did you go by some sharp curves in the road?"

"No."

"OK, we'll send someone out to you. Do you know where the horse
came from?" (Like I even live anywhere near North Stonington)

"No. It was in the middle of the road."

"OK, we'll send someone out."

Well, so I sat there, waiting, and waiting, and waiting, hoping that
this horse didn't get hit in the process. I kept flashing my lights
at oncoming traffic, and my brake lights at traffic coming behind
me. Not that it deterred people from going by at 50 mph and
freaking out the horse again. Thankfully, a lady stopped, who
actually had horses, and was able to keep the horse semi-occupied,
while we waited.

Anyway, the cop got there (who I don't think knew anything about
horses btw), and I'm hoping that he found a way to find the owners
of the horse, since there wasn't a house around that looked like it
had stables or anything.

Another interesting day of letterboxing!

Music Woman
