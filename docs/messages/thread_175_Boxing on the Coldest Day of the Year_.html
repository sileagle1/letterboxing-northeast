Boxing on the Coldest Day of the Year!

From: mindizney@aol.com | Date: 2004-01-16 23:06:00 UTC
Bluebird and I ventured out on the coldest day of the year to go boxing! Are
we nuts or what??? I suppose there was SOME reason why we both had the day
off from school today!

We had a great time finding the following boxes: Special Joy's, The Robin, In
Search of the White Horse of Uffington, In Search of the Exmoor Beast. White
Horse has a totally holy lid and needs a new ink pad. An animal has seemed
to have chewed it and has put holes in the lid.

We then ventured out to Glastonbury to the Audubon Society, only to find that
we couldn't get half of the clue to the third box due to ice. For those of
you who want to get the third box, you have to go to boxes one and two, even if
you have already found them to get the password. Even though the trail to
the second box was impassible, they still wouldn't budge on letting us have the
box. Bird Stamper has trained them well.....GRRRRRRR!!!!!!!!!

We ventured on to get the Town Line, Rapallo Viaduct, and Dead and Dreaming
boxes, which Bluebird was missing. It was nice to get out to Town Line and
Rapallo to check the boxes! All boxes are in fine shape! I'm wondering if I
should use those cheapo boxes more often! They seem to work great!

Hope others got out boxing! Have a boxious three, four or five day weekend
(depending on where you teach in CT!)!!!!!

Music Woman
(Who now has a four day weekend)

From: Just Beth | Date: 2004-01-17 14:10:23 UTC
Well, I didn't go boxing on the coldest day of the year "thus far" but I did
go boxing after a root canal last week.
I happened to be passing by the STanley Quarter Carousel Box in Bristol (at
least within a 15 mile radious of it) and thought before the novacaine wore
off, why the heck not!!
There I was wandering in the woods, mumbling clues out loud and drooling
along the way. Stopping every now and again to dig in the snow at the base
of two trunked trees along the way.
I figured I wouldn't need my pack as it was only one box so I donned my wool
gloves with the fingers cut off(of the gloves that is) I put my letterboxing
neccesities into a zip lock bag and set off. It was quite a windy day and my
long red hair was just flying everywhere and was a tangled mess by the time
I got back to the car.

I know all the cautions about boxing alone however, I was more concerned
about getting picked up by the people in white coats from the local hospital
than I was about anyone messing with a mumbling, drooling, bag lady with
tangled red hair digging wildly in the snow on one of the not coldest days
of the year thus far.

After, I finally found the correct tree and stamped in, I didn't want anyone
to follow my tracks to the box location so I proceeded to make foot prints
around the base of other trees and even stomped a characature of Jay Drew in
the snow, but that can only be seen from an arial view.

From Letterboxing Diva to Bag Lady, gee how quickly we fall.

~Beth
