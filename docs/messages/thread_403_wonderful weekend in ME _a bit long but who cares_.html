wonderful weekend in ME (a bit long but who cares?)

From: solor8008 | Date: 2004-03-22 12:16:54 UTC
Binx and I would like to extend heartfelt thanks to Phyto, Nautilus,
and all others who made the Laudholm gathering such a fun and
informative time! The box stamps were of super quality and it is
always great to see all the familiar faces again!

Despite Binx having this awful cold that seemed to have hit a few
other boxers as well (how you doing Jeremy and Brian?), we had a
great northbound trip, stopping in Kittery for a couple boxes before
arriving too late to the State of Maine to greet our fellow southern
New Englanders. We proceeded on a wild goose chase for a couple of
mystery boxes before giving up and going to "see the lights" in the
area south of Portland. ;)

Federal Jack's was a blast...it's fun to see people from such
diverse backgrounds meeting a table for dinner, all in the name of
our outdoor explorations. Great to see you all again and to meet a
few new faces (welcome Roamin Warrior and Peace Amy from RI!).

Despite the slushy conditions due to an overnight snow-then-rain
storm, we accompanied TeamGreenDragon, RustyPuff/CSCM, Roamin
Warrior and Peace Amy on a nice trek across the heath of Saco.
Thanks for the great boxes Phyto & Isosceles!

We then hot-footed it back south to Wells in time to catch the
Dartmoor presentation by Debbie. Very interesting and I am now
intrigued with the idea of getting to Dartmoor at some point!
Despite some technical difficulties, it was great to hear the scoop
from someone who got to letterbox as an educational sabbatical...how
cool is that? The tick info was handy (thanks Culex), and we can't
wait to create felt pouches for our sig stamps to replace the wads
of dirty paper towels we usually use. I somehow missed the matchbook
demo entirely, much to my dismay (hah! I've always wanted to use
that phrase!).

Laudholm Farm felt like a mini-Connecticut and we boxed our hearts
out well into the afternoon, being the very last car in the parking
lot when we finally bugged out at 5:30. Excellent carvings and great
clues, all! Laudholm Farm itself is a beautiful place and we look
forward to another visit this spring simply to walk around and enjoy
it all.

See you next time round!

Solor
