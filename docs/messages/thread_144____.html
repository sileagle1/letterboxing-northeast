I may be hearty(aka 'Kim of the Antarctic'), but I gotta draw the line
somewhere.

I always describe our New England weather to non-New Englanders in this way:

90 + degrees (with its usual 90% humidity) = Freakin' hot (substitute your
favorite F word here)
80 - 90 degrees = very warm
70 - 80 degrees = nice and warm
60 - 70 degrees = Niiiiiiiiice!
50 - 60 degrees = Perfect T-shirt/sweatshirt and jeans weather - pretty much
ideal for a good hike
40 - 50 degrees = cool
30 - 40 degrees = brisk
20 - 30 degrees = chilly
10 - 20 degrees = cold
under 10 degrees = Freakin' cold (again...your favorite F word here) or as we
New Englanders like to say:" Bittah cold"

That about captures it... :-)
-Kim (Rustypuff)
