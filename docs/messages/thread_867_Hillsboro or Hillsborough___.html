From: cpascott | Date: 2004-06-25 01:15:34 UTC
Pretty much all "boroughs" can be abbreviated as "boro". "Borough"
is the more formal.

In Connecticut:
Marlborough

In Maine:
Scarborough

In Massachusetts:
Boxborough
Foxborough
Lanesborough
Marlborough
Middleborough
New Marlborough
North Attleborough
Northborough
Southborough
Tyngsborough
Westborough

I'm sure Rhode Island, New Hampshire and Vermont are similar. Both
spellings can be used interchangably.

CPAScott

'Boro vs 'Borough. This is an old argument that comes up here in NE. When I was doing som research in Northborough, MA, the historical society woman said the towns like to keep the old spellings but that the powers that be don't like to pay for the extra letters when printing signs. So, it comes down to money and pure laziness! I 'vote' for 'borough.

'Borough' comes from the older 'Burgh' or 'Burg', which started out meaning a well-protected hill, then a fortified settlement on a hill, then eventually to a town then to a district in a town. (You SCA people should know a herald when you hear one go on and on about placenames and onosmatics).
Now ask me where 'Mid-Atlantic' and 'Mid-West' comes from. :)
