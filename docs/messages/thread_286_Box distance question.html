Hi all,

I know that there are no rules, per se, but I was wondering if you
could let me know if the following is okay or breaking LB etiquette:

Is it okay to plant a box that's in the same (large) park as 3 other
boxes, but is really 2 miles away from the last box? I know it's not
cool to place boxes in close proximity to one another, but I don't
know if 2 miles is adequate space or not.

Thanks!

Fleur de Lis

Re: Box distance question

From: Steve and Heidi | Date: 2004-02-29 22:09:28 UTC
We've seen boxes literally within 2 feet of one another in certain
state parks here in CT and beleive it or not -- some folks never even
notice! 2 Miles is entirely more than plenty of room. Keep in mind
that some folks read through all the clues and do several series' at
once. Others of us like to do a series at a time and don't mind
covering the same ground going in different directions. Mystery
boxes etc should not be counted in your "count" of close-bys, becuase
they are not technically "public" knowledge. Thats how we can have
place like Avery Land Trust where there's somewhere more than 8 boxes
in 75 square acres (actual # higher, however some secrets are made to
be kept!).

Just our 2 cents....
-Steve, Heidi and Madras
