only in connecticut!!!

From: Phyto | Date: 2004-06-18 22:01:50 UTC
Phyto traveled almost 8 hours to arrive in Torrington, Connecticut safe and
sound. Much to phyto's dismay when phyto arrived at the Warner Theater
box office there was no box in sight. After many emails back and forth to the
theater director, phyto was sure that the box would be present.

Phyto was in luck! After making a small fuss, phyto's box was returned. To
phyto's delight, The Engraver, Corinna, Wanda & Pete and some other folks
had managed to snag the box before it went away!

Moving on...phyto decided to box with FOOL (fraternal order of the lizard)
and we found "OUR SECRET" safe and sound. Phyto and FOOL left "The
order of the red hand hitchhiker" in that spot waiting for someone to NOT
bring that up to maine (it was at the maine event last year!!!!)

Finally, the fool's folly was ours as we stopped at "The Embrace". What a
wonderful stamp - echoes of Matisse - really loved that spot... phyto and fool
were surprised by fellow letterboxers. It was the first time that Phyto has been
surprised on the trail. Woohoo! That will be a trail tale to be sure.

Thanks you crazy connecticut boxers!

From: lb_isosceles | Date: 2004-06-18 22:42:52 UTC
Meanwhile, Isosceles was left to his own devices in
Maine. Needing to get his mind off of other things,
he made a pilgrimage to a nearby letterboxing paradise,
where he discovered a much sought after yet seldom
discovered letterbox. This find was as sweet as honey,
and had been undisturbed for more than a year!
Apparently the fire from the breath of the last
finders had made the trail more difficult to follow,
although it had left a strange green tint on many of
the nearby rocks =)

-Isosceles

We've considered this, actually I have even carved 2 stamps for a
local pond. Unfortunately our current housing situation keeps me
from using my kayak much and has squelched my initial drive to do
so.

In a nutshell, we live in a condomunium and my kayak is hanging up
high in my parents garage. So access is limited and a royal pain to
wrestle the boat down, etc. We'll be moving the end of next month
and the kayak's coming with us.... sooooo look for a few aqua boxes
by the end of this summer in SE CT. Heidi and I will be literally
next to the Thames River in Ledyard, CT and once some exploring is
completed (and some wall painting and floor sanding in the new
house...) , we'll be back to planting!

It is truly a fun idea!

-Steve (Heidi, and Madras)

to come kayak in Connecticut. We've not done these boxes, but have
paddled that part of the Conn River and its very very nice!
