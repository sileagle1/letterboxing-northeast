F100 AT LAST!

From: monotropa | Date: 2003-12-29 22:24:02 UTC
Finally, after five years of letterboxing, I've hit F100. I hid my first boxes in 1998 and found my first one in 1999. There weren't very
many to find the first few years, but my how things have changed!!!

I hit F100 today at the Journey Into Imagination series in Shenipsit State Forest in CT. Thanks for the recommendation, Kstar! (Amy
and Jay, I would love to do your MA boxes soon, too.) Unfortunately, there was a lot of shooting going on the whole time I was in the
forest, so I split after finding the first six or so boxes. The guns were getting on my nerves. Just after I found the Lorax and Goodnight
Moon boxes and returned to the main trail, I saw a dead deer lying right on the side of the road. Being a tree hugging, vegetarian kind
of person, I felt grossed out and uncomfortable.

Is there a better (i.e., non-hunting) time of year to go there? I wouldn't mind going back but not if I have to worry about becoming the
first letterboxer to be killed in action.

After that I drove to the Ashley Reservation in Holyoke, MA, where I found one of the letterboxes there. Lovely place. The frozen
reservoir was so beautiful in the bright afternoon sun. I will go back!

Thanks to everyone who's contributed to this great hobby. Good letterboxing to all in 2004!

Bonnie
