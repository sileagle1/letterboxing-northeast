Sun Puzzle letterboxes in Arnold Arboretum (MA)

From: ms_wanda_maximoff | Date: 2004-12-10 14:38:41 UTC
Team Freckles was able to squeeze in some boxing fun afterschool
yesterday. We went in pursuit of the two-part Sun Puzzle letterbox
in Jamaica Plain's Arnold Arboretum.

I was hoping the dark overcast skies and chill wet weather would
discourage most folks from venturing there, so we could look for
boxes without spending a lot of time dashing away from hiding spots
and inventing involved conversations about dendrology to cover up
our true mission. :)

We found the first Sun Puzzle box, nice hiding spot, wonderful
stamp. Could use a new outer Ziploc (Murphy's Law contributed to me
not having one on me) -- I will replace it next week when we go back.

The second box we could not find, though we are positive we had the
right tree. There is a chance we missed it though, because the very
open and public and well-travelled nature of where it was/is hidden,
made thorough searching impossible.

We started off after the Mad Chef letterbox, but it was too dark, so
off we went to a baklava-making party, dreaming of the box that got
away. :)

Library Letterbox

From: john@johnsblog.com | Date: 2004-12-10 21:54:22 UTC
I am very excited! Recently, I meet with the circulation
director for our local library and pitched a book
letterbox (letterbook). She loved it. I worked up a stamp
design, carved the stamp and listed the contents that I
would supply, including wet wipes and handouts on
letterboxing. She wrote me to say that the director signed
off on the plan and they had decided that they would take
a large reference book that was about to be retired and
hollow out the pages. It already has its own Dewey code
and we've agreed on some simple treasure hunting clues to
find it.

Now I just have to wait for them to hollow out the book
and I will post the clues. It will be in the reference
section of Memorial Hall Library in Andover, MA.
