From: rospa10 | Date: 2006-10-15 01:55:57 UTC
There are some indoor boxes around New England. You could shop, have a
cup of coffee or even a meal while boxing. Re. outdoor boxes: snow
shoes, x.c. skis and small hand held garden-type spades (mine is made
of bright orange plastic) do come in handy. If you do dig out a winter
box, just make lots of cofusing tracks around the l.b. site...walk
backwards, walk in circles, you get the idea--keep box hidden. Truly
dedicated boxers join fly south for the winter. Truly, truly dedicated
boxers migrate with the monarche butterflies to Mexico.
Enjoy!

I used to go away to the Virgin Islands in February, but last year I
couldn't swing it so I started the "Desperately Seeking Sun" event for
my fellow northern boxers. Here in Central New York we get a LOT of
snow and a LOT of gray days in the winter. This was a feeble attempt
to have a party with other winter-bound boxers.

The day of the event it was BITTER cold....15 below zero with the
windchill...and snow was blowing everywhere. I had no idea if anyone
would even show up, but to my surprise they came from everywhere! We
had nearly 60 people for that event and it was a LOT of fun. Great hot
food and steaming cups of coffee, tea and hot chocolate. I learned
that northern boxers are HARD CORE boxers and will box in nearly any
weather. I still remember Jiggs walking back into the lodge with
icicles hanging from his coat...

There was plenty to do outside AND inside, so you could take your
choice of collecting exchanges and personal travelers around the
roaring fireplace or brave the elements to get the boxes sent from
talented carvers all over the country. It was so much darn fun, we are
doing it again! Already we have nearly 50 people signed up and it
isn't even November!

Check out the pictures of your fellow Snow Boxers, and if you would
like to join us, please do. There is also an after party called
Desperately Seeking Suds. :) This year we have an entire lodge to
ourselves and the trails are flat..making it easy for snowshoes,
crosscountry skis or hiking. There may even be ice fishing boxes
involved...depending on the ice thickness! As always, there are games
for the kids and a KIDS ONLY letterbox as well as a challenge for the
adults as well. The famous Lock Wench Raffle Baskets will be back and
everyone who attends gets a door prize. We'd love to have you!

LW

From: Phyto | Date: 2006-10-15 15:04:32 UTC
Hi Kelly
We live in Maine, though it is not as far north as you can get - we
are in the Central Highlands region, we still receive a fair amount of
winter. I'd say our boxing continues all year round. We enjoy this
time of year the most with less tourists and crisp fall days. The
boxing until the end of November is great in Maine - and New England
in general IMHO.

If the weather prevents us from searching for boxes, we use the indoor
time as an opportunity to brainstorm box ideas for the upcoming spring
and summer season. It's a great time to curl up with a hot beverage
and carve some stamps, create clues and spend time repairing other
boxes from the last season. There is no shortage of letterboxing to be
done.

There are some indoor boxes to be found as well.
Good luck!
phyto


P.S. - yes to the shovel, I have a bright orange plastic one that I
have been known to use in extruding a snow-covered box from time to
time ;)
