New Haven CT boxes retired

From: sadie_russ | Date: 2007-09-25 02:59:54 UTC
With some sadness today we officially retired our "Above It All"
series at East Rock Park.
The main reason was the uneasiness we ourselves were feeling in the
parking lots there.
It was a crystal clear day with unlimited visibility. Two hawks were
soaring on updrafts overhead, and fittingly, a Cessna kept circling
the Rock as we pulled the boxes.
Thanks to all who were "brave" enough to come to our urban park to
box. Special apologies to "System and SAB" who recently wrote in the
logbooks that they were coming back with a compass to find box #2.

S&R

Sorry to see them go, but I understand your decision, Sadie and
Russ. In this case it's not what's best for the letterbox, but the
letterboxer - the area's not safe any more. I have a box of my own
in the same park and haven't been to check on it in a year, simply
because I don't feel comfortable walking around there, even with my
dogs. You've got me thinking that it's probably time to retire or
relocate it - how can I expect others to go there, if I don't feel
safe there myself....? That responsibility can't be pushed over onto
the LbNA Waiver of Responsibility!

I've also "adopted" a letterbox from the LbNA "basement of unclaimed
boxes" that's located there in East Rock Park, "Revival of the
Alchemy Series - Mercury". It has been there since June 2002 and has
fared well the entire time. I am reluctant to move it, since it's
not my own, and certainly wouldn't retire it, but again feel that for
the safety of those who come in search of it, it should be in a more
secure location. Must give this some serious thought!
