What a Weekend! - Long Post

From: Brian, Ryan & Lori | Date: 2004-03-15 15:54:09 UTC
This weekend, TGD managed to find 23 boxes!!!
Yup - that's right - we spent a day in CT (heh, heh, heh)

We drove 2 and half hours on Saturday to Redding, Ct.
Our objective was to meet Alan and his hiking class at
Hungtington State Park at noon. We arrived about 10:30
and went to Putnam Park and found 3 of Alan's boxes.
This was a wonderful park steeped in history of Revolutionary
Soldiers. As we approached the location for the third box,
we saw a small group of people walking down the hill. Of course,
it was Alan and his hiking class (we were not surprised but we
were very psyched to see our old friend again!) After a quick trip
to snag another box in a different location at Putnam park (What
was the name of that box, Alan, the one with the Apple stamp in it)
We drove over to Huntington State park and ate our lunch while our
hands were so cold we couldn't even feel them. Eventually, we were
joined by the Columbian Bugs and headed out for our long trek through
the woods to find the Peanuts gang. After a while, we "bumped into"
Just Beth, who found us "discretely" stamping into one of the
Huntington State Park boxes and along came Phynstar with Northstar.
All of us plugged along, climbing hills and sometimes huffing and
puffing - cold hands, and shortage of water. We had a lot of laughs,
while Alan stood scratching his head (I won't go into that) but it
was funny! I think that at least two of Alan's students, Debbie and
Dennis, will join our ranks as "official letterboxers". By 6 pm,
with the sun setting and my hands as numb as my brain, our total
count for the day was 16 finds. Huntington State Park is a beautiful
parcel of land with lots of glacial erratics and old foundations.
Many wonderful features with lots of boxing potential. I'm glad we
made the trip out there finally. Thanks Alan! It took us five hours
to get home while we sat in a traffic jam on I-95 in Providence due
to a five car accident.

On Sunday, we took it easy by strolling along Mt Hope Farm in
Bristol, RI. Beautiful place, where we met Quin the famous greyhound.
(you have to find the first letterbox of the series to know why Quin
is famous). The boxes are hidden in wonderful locations with special
qualities. Then, after realizing that there is no place to eat on
Route 1 near Foxboro Stadium - we found a Happy Turtle who is indeed
very happy and in good condition. Then we climbed a hill to find out
that Jay and Amy are very much in love and that their box is well
taken care of!

Soooo - what did you do this weekend??????
