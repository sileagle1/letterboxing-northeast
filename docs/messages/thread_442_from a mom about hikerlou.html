Re: from a mom about hikerlou

From: Brian, Ryan & Lori | Date: 2004-03-29 17:43:03 UTC
>when our children do things incorrectly we guide them in the right
>direction. we never even tried in this case. how is the child going
to >get educated if we the "adults" don't help them.

Since when is it my job to "guide" anybody else's children? My 11
year old son Ryan is also a member of the list but he doesn't post
because I've asked him not to. Who is running that household?

I was as worried that the whole thing could have been some kind of
undercover police sting operation and that they had just randomly
picked our group and let on they were 13 and would be waiting for
whomever showed up. I didn't want that connected to me in any way. We
even attempted to get Bluebird on the phone to confirm that she knew
him, and hers wasn't some name they picked from the members list to
make the whole thing more convincing.

>i knew he was going to come back under another name and he did. this
>child has chosen a hobby that is kind and loving. why can't we be
>that way to him?

I also expected he would come back under a new name and I am glad to
see he did so openly. We emailed him and I also tried to get in touch
with him thru IM but he did not respond. I felt I had to do something
then and not later and did what I did because it was my only choice.
I knew no lasting harm would come of me stopping his messages till I
could sort things out.And looking back now after having time to think
things thru I admit I acted a little rashly. But at the time I didn't
have the luxury of being able to look back on things.I'd much rather
have come in today and received a nasty note from him than come in to
read of an abduction.I thought I was being "kind and loving" as best
I could considering the circumstances.


>Hikerlou's posts were all polite and it sounds like he listened to
>the cautions he received. From his response, it sounds to me like
his >parents do the right things in terms of internet supervision.

I half expected to get an email from his parents or for them to post
themselves to let us all know things were on the "Up and Up". If they
were monitoring his posts then why didn't they step in when they read
our warnings about children on the internet? If I read Jay's post
written to my child about how he should set up his talklist to
protect himself, I would have absolutely got in touch with him.If not
for more advise, then at least to thank him for the guidance he would
have given my child. As for listening to the cautions he received, a
little late don't you think, he had already posted were he would be,
the cautions should have come from his parents before he was ever
allowed on the computer. Kim, are you telling me you would have no
problem having your 13 year old posting were they would be, alone and
in the woods?

>If he was banned without
>opportunity to give his side and comment, personally, I think that
>was an error.

Like I said I didn't have the "luxury of time" and made a snap
decision. Since no lasting harm would be done, I don't think this was
an error on my part. Doing nothing could have been an error that
would haunt me the rest of my life. To me this was something that
went way beyond letterboxing or this talklist.

Brian
TeamGreenDragon

From: Just Beth | Date: 2004-03-29 19:25:48 UTC
Brian,

I for one completely support your decision to put a stop to Hikerlou's posts
until this matter could be sorted out in an appropriate manner. As a fellow
letterboxer I understand all of our desire to encourage a young letterboxer
and make him feel welcomed by visiting his box. HOWEVER, as a parent I would
be furious to think a group of adults allowed and encouraged my child to put
himself in an incredibly vulnerable and potentially dangerous position
witout intervening. If you think I am over emphasizing this watch the news
tonight and count how many children are kidnapped and murdered every day in
this country. Yes, it even happens to nice letterboxing families too.

How many times after a tragedy do you hear people say if only I knew, if
only I paid more attention, if only if only.....the problem is you never
really know if you made the right choice or not. However, I think was a good
time to react first and ask questions later.

I'm sure Hikerlou will realize it was certainly done with his best interest
at heart and his parents should be grateful that Brian was only trying to
look out for their son.

It was a tough decision and you will feel the flames lapping at your
backside but FROM A MOM, I'd rather have my sons feelings hurt than to have
something tragic happen.

Hang in there Brian, it's a tough job but someones got to do it and I for
one know you did right thing.



~Beth
