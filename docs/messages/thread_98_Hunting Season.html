Righto Phyto, in fact the color is specified by maine law.

HUNTER ORANGE means a daylight fluorescent orange color with a
dominant wave length between 595 and 605 nanometers, excitation
purity not less than 85% and luminance factor of not less than 40%.

The strange part is that only HUNTERS are required to wear this or
any bright clothing. In Maine, if you are not carrying a firearm, you
are not bound by these laws. (I don't think hunting for letterboxes
counts)

However, I would not be caught dead (no pun intended) wearing
anything else during the maine hunting season. It only takes one
hunter with a "shoot first, ask questions later" philosophy to ruin
your whole day. That particular color of orange is not only highly
visible during broad daylight, but also in the twilight hours, when
hunters hunting for their last day of the season are really itching
to shoot a deer, moose, bear, or whatever they can strap to the hood
of their car and haul back to New Jersey. Even if you plan on only
being out during the broad daylight hours, you never know when you
might get delayed until evening, and that bright red jacket of yours
may begin to look awfully brown...

Better safe than sorry.

-Isosceles

From: devoile | Date: 2003-12-31 03:24:16 UTC
Orange is only mandatory if you're a hunter in the state of CT, as in
Maine and most other states. Hikers are not required to wear any
identifiable color at all. Of course we all know this would be
irresponsible. As long as you wear any bright color, and stay close
to the trails, you should be fine. Every season, regardless of
location, has it's inherent risks. Hiking in and of itself carries
with it a certain amount of risk. There is always something to be
afraid of. ( I recall a post from a letterboxer trying to work
through her overwhelming fear of being attacked ) Use your common
sense and try not to allow these things to frighten you away from
doing what you enjoy. Life is too short. "D"
