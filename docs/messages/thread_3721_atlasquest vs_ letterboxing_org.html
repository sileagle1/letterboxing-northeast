Re: [LbNE] atlasquest vs. letterboxing.org

From: themerrypranksters@comcast.net | Date: 2008-01-15 22:13:37 UTC
We use both, but mainly AQ. We post all our boxes on both sites.
You will find some boxes available on only one of the sites, so it
helps to cross reference them both when searching an area.
We find AQ to be better for communicating with others. E-mailing
is made easy and the message posting boards are a lot of fun.
Gatherings are listed in an nice orderly fashion and there seems to be
an unlimited wealth of shared knowledge if you have any questions
or problems. Blog sites and links are available for all sorts of
supplies like carving mediums, tools, patches, T shirts etc. Also lots
of good tutorial info, statistics and glossary pages.

In a nut shell, we find LBNA to be more basic and "traditional".
AQ has may more playful features and is more "modern".
Both are good for finding clues and logging in boxes. It's really
just a matter of personal preference!

The Merry Pranksters

AQ searches are not limited to city based searches. While some boxes
are assigned to arbitrary points within a city because specific
locations are not yet (or never will be) listed, many boxes have
location data that allows for some useful search methods and results.
The ability to search for boxes within a given radius from where I am,
or where I plan to be, can be very useful. I would contend that this is
a more useful method of "area search" than county boarders, which can be
very arbitrary. For example, on AQ a 25 mile radius search from my home
is all one page and I can sort by distance if I so choose. On LbNA I
have to review two seperate regions, find the towns that are within that
approximate distance and not a hundred miles away, and compile my own
list. Near home, that is not a big deal. When travelling, it can
require more research and effort. I like that research, so I do it, but
if I am in a hurry, I go to the AQ search first.

This interview by Mark Pepe give some insight into the roots of AQ:

http://ryancarpenter.blogspot.com/

More about the evolution of AQ can be found on AQ itself, here:

http://www.atlasquest.com/aboutus/

I personally see AQ as a continuing attempt at building a robust tool to
assist with the practice of this hobby. I am new, but I do recognize
that LbNA has a deeper connection to the 'feel' of the letterboxing
experience, if you will. The AQ tools, while powerful, are occasionally
not as user friendly as they might be, and the design is sometimes
downright quirky. It seems that some appreciate and love that
quirkiness, while others don't. One great thing about AQ is that it is
highly customizable to meet your needs, but you have to be kind of geeky
to use and appreciate some of those options.

Sunnyside Seeker
