Cedar Hill Cemetery Hartford, Connecticut

From: GrampysGurl@aol.com | Date: 2004-10-16 21:04:31 UTC
Roo and I headed out there today and I for one quite enjoyed myself. We
couldn't find the first box, I suspect it is missing but we found the other 3,
all are in desperate need of new log books... I will try to get out there in the
next couple weeks as I would love to explore the cemetery more without the
thought of the "hunt" on my mind. The Horace Wells Stamp was being guarded by
2 female deer that we were able to get quite close on and sat there looking
at each other for a good 10 minutes :o) The J P Morgan Stamp was guarded by a
lunching Red Tailed Hawk and on the way out we saw a large egret of some kind
as well as mallard ducks and a few Canada Geese.... We also so a bunny and
many different kinds of birds, I do believe I saw my first juncos of the year
and I also ran into a few Carolina wrens while digging out the Crying Woman
Letterbox (I plan on going back to find the stone that the stamp is based on)
as well as my favorites the chickadees. I know for myself those are the
things I'd like to see on a trip. Thank you to the placer and thank you for
inviting me to such a wonderful place to visit. Off to Mile of Ledges tomorrow.
Only a few weeks and about 15 stamps into this and I am hooked, DH laughed when
I said I was going to try this through the winter, I hate the cold but I
think my love of this will actually outweigh my hatred of cold lol.
