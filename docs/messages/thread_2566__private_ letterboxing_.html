"private" letterboxing?

From: bobsandy98 | Date: 2005-11-14 12:44:54 UTC
I know this posting is a little late involving the controversy of
keeping letterboxing restricted-and I will admit that we never planted
one of our own boxes,so I don't know how I would feel if our boxes got
trashed. But reading that letterboxers want to go "underground" with
their clues & basically restricting who has access to the clues is very
sad. The hobby has grown because of the media-and like everything else
that the media reports on, there is a flurry of interest & then it goes
away. People eventually are going to get tired of hiking in the woods
for the sole purpose of trashing a rubbermaid container. Letterboxing
(& geocaching)has introduced so many people to out-of-the way trails &
areas that no one would have known about. We have discovered so many
trails that we never knew existed & it is a great great hobby. Now that
winter is approaching there is a good chance that a lot of boxes won't
be disturbed in the snow & by next spring this will be forgotten by the
few who would like to vandalize the boxes. Only a thought-but felt I
needed to say it.
