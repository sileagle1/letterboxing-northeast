Advice sought

From: J. BRIDGMAN | Date: 2006-05-27 04:16:52 UTC
I have been contacted by the Avon Land Trust to give some
letterboxing information for their newsletter. Seems that someone
came across letterboxing.org, looked up a box on one of their
holdings (which happened to be one of mine) and was impressed with
the number of visitors and the quality and variety of the stamps.
This person saw this as a way to attract visitors to other land trust
holdings and asked for my help. They would like to have a summer
activity for children of hunting letterboxes on land trust
properties, rewarding those who find all available with a t-shirt on
Avon Day in September. The advice I need is what to tell them for
the newsletter. What should I NOT do or say, especially. Can I let
them photograph the contents of one of my boxes and publish some of
the stamp images in the newsletter? They asked if they could do
that.

Would some of you be willing to place some boxes on Avon Land Trust
property? I'm not sure how many they are anticipated nor what areas
they are considering, but I have already learned the location of one
area that I had not been able to find before. I will be having a
phone conversation with them on Tuesday so will have more information
after that. I'm working on new boxes but I'm sure they would like a
variety of placers. Will keep you informed as I learn more.

Bell Lady

From: birder579 | Date: 2006-05-27 22:42:29 UTC
Hi Bell Lady
That is fantastic news. I'm glad they recognized the benefits of
having boxes on their properties. An introdiction to letterboxing
and pointers to the web sites are appropriate for their newsletter.

As far as images, just your own stamp images or those where you have
permission from the artist/carver should be published. Also do not
publish the real or trail name of any other letterboxer, without
permission from them. Those have been the problems in the past. A
photo of the box and contents would be fine, without any stamp
images showing.

I could plant a box where one is needed. Contact me off line.
The Bird Stamper
