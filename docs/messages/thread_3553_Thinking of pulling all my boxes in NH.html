Re: [LbNE] Re: Thinking of pulling all my boxes in NH

From: Chuck and Laura Lubelczyk | Date: 2007-07-11 00:59:47 UTC
I agree with others that you should keep your boxes out and not let
negative comments spoil your enjoyment of the hobby. However, consider
that the boxer who sent you the note may have thought of they were
helping or providing constructive criticism. I have received many notes
about clues over the years (and sent a few). If someone sent me a note
about a misspelling or word added/left out I would shrug and thank them
and probably make the change. Mistakes happen and lots of people make
simple typos. I also appreciate when people let me know about
problems/suggestions with clues. I reserve the right to do what I want
and write my clues how I want and ignore any suggested changes, however,
if 3 people write and express confusion about an aspect of my clues
maybe I'll change it to be clearer (or maybe not if it was intentionally
left vague). My point is that sometimes the original intent and the tone
is not always clear in an e-mail. I would first assume that they think
they are being helpful rather than criticizing the lovely letterbox
gifts that you have left for them. Happy continued boxing and planting!
(from the usually quiet on the boards) Nautilus

Re: Thinking of pulling all my boxes in NH

From: Lisa (Taplin) Murray | Date: 2007-07-17 00:05:45 UTC
I get hoards of email from people who complain about my clues and I either ignore them
or I write back a taughtly kind letter basically saying that finding a box is not a guarantee.
Of course I am thinking lots of OTHER things like: get a life, maybe the problem is YOU not
my clues, get an editing job etc etc.

I wouldn't pull boxes over a few complaints. Just ignore this person! I also don't give hints
to my boxes, in general. I just don't respond to emails that ask for hints. I feel like finding
a box is a challenge and is not an instant satidfaction hobby. I know some people see
boxing that way and want to pump up their F-count, but I don't and I don't play that way.
Decide how you want to play, what boxing means to you and then be involved that way.
DOn't worry about what other people think. They have thousands of boxes to choose from
if theycan't seem to figure out your clues. These person is choosing to be bothered by his
or her inability to figure out you clues. Oh well! I think it is very rude to critique another
person's clues or give cluewriting tips! Don't pull your boxes! Leave them there for the
clever people to find, I say!

Lisa
