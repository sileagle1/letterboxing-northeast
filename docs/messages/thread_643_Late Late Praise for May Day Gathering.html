Well, the account of our adventures:

Rule #1: While letterboxing, make sure to seek out the correct
driving directions.

Do NOT, and I repeat DO NOT USE MAPQUEST.COM to find any sort of
driving directions. This could cause you to drive for two hours,
instead of driving for what could have been a half an hour...*grumble*

Rule #2: Make sure your rain poncho is thicker than a plastic bag, or
else you will get hot & sweaty, and tear it off, and become drenched
from the rainy weather in the forest.

Rule #3: Make sure to know which side of the pavilion to start the
April Showers series on. Or else risk walking for what seems like
forever, deeper and deeper into the woods and farther and farther
away from any sort of landmarks.

Rule #4: When you have a personal traveler, be prepared for people to
flock near you to earn the stamp. And make sure you do not have
doubts of your ink pad running out.

Rule #5: Do not trust Butterfly when she says she's leaving and must
get your personal traveler right away. Hahaha...

Rule #6: When someone wins in the raffle three, or four times, make
sure to make them feel guilty enough to not accept anything else
after getting a handbook, a walking stick, etc. And make sure that
you find out that you can buy more tickets (unfortunately we did not
know this...)

Rule #7: Despite all the minor misfortunes, take a deep breath and
keep letterboxing!

Thanks to MommaFox, Bald Eagle, Teach and Preach, and The Maplines
for making yesterday's event great!

Thanks to everybody who found out about my personal traveler - I'm so
glad everybody liked the first stamp I carved (hey I'm trying to be
humble here)...I think I should try and make a list of everybody who
got my P.T.

I'm extremely sorry that for some of the people I met, it was a one
way exchange!! I was glad to get many exchanges - but to those who I
encountered when my stamp wasn't with me (Pine Cone, Pine Tree, Cat
Lover/Gardener/Teacher, Mystique...and anybody else I may have
missed!)...may we meet again!

My P.T. will be available on the trail and at events with me...so
I'll probably post it again near a gathering date in the future...

...also I heard talk of a gathering on the horizon of early June? Is
that the one people have been chatting about?

Buddy
