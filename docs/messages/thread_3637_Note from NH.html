From: Mary Ellen Martel | Date: 2007-09-22 13:12:25 UTC
At the risk of angering someone ~ isn't planting "farther off the path" exactly the sort of thing that gives letterboxing a bad name with our park officials? I have lived right next to a National Park for over 35 and I have seen the destruction that has been caused by millions of people going off the marked park trails. Erosion has become a huge problem because vegetation has been destroyed. Even trees have been damaged by people wearing down the soil around their roots. Places that used to be little green paths through the woods are now wide strips of barren, packed dirt that is as hard as concrete. And I won't even go into the cost to the local towns when ambulance crews have to rescue people who thought they knew a "better" way and have been seriously injured falling down cliffs. Rather than planting "farther off the path" and encouraging people to break park rules (around here the rules state very clearly that people are to stay on the marked trails), why not find
places to hide boxes that don't run the risk of pushing people to break rules. And if a place like that can't be found, then perhaps the park isn't the place to plant a box. Many of our parks are in very fragile condition. As public lands shrink, more people are using the parks and the negative impact is huge. Let's not let letterboxing add to that.
Just my opinion.
Memlili

From: fleurchild | Date: 2007-09-24 16:24:01 UTC
Please, please do not put boxes off trail on mountains! By doing so,
you encourage people to create new "trails" that cause erosion and
cause plants and trees (some endangered and protected) to suffer damage
and even die. Erosion also contributes to mud slides, change in
weather, and horrible trail conditions.

Also, above tree line is the alpine zone where plants can sustain
incredible damage just by being walked on once. Those plants are trying
to survive in that harsh environment and don't need boxers tramping
over them to get to a box.

I agree with the others. If there isn't a suitable spot right next to
the trail, you should be placing the box on that trail no matter how
cool or "perfect" the spot might be for your stamp.
