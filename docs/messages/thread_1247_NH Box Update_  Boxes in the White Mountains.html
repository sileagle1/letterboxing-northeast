NH Box Update: Boxes in the White Mountains

From: cpascott | Date: 2004-09-07 14:10:03 UTC
Hi all! Just returned from a nice three-day weekend in the White
Mountains of New Hampshire! Although our primary purpose over the
weekend was not letterboxing, we didn't manage to successfully hunt
nine boxes while we were there -- and picked up a hitchhiker along
the way!

All of our finds are now listed on our website, but the write-ups
aren't yet complete. They'll be there in a couple of days.

The boxes we hunted were all in or near Franconia Notch and Crawford
Notch State Parks.

The following boxes were all found to be intact and in good shape:
* Clark's Trading Post (Lincoln)
* Artist's Bluff (Franconia Notch)
* Little Dragonfly (Franconia Notch)
* A Snail Trail (Franconia Notch)
* Our First Vacation (Crawford Notch)
* Franky's (Crawford Notch)
* Pemi Trail (Franconia Notch)

The following boxes were found, but had problems:
* Tigers at Twilight (Crawford Notch) -- this box was extremely
wet. The logbook is completely ruined, as is the stamp pad. The
stamp was fine.
* Arethusa Falls (Crawford Notch) -- everything about this box was
fine, except perhaps it's hiding place. It could be seen from the
trail, and judging from the number of people who had written in the
logbook "found this box while answering nature's call", an alternate
location for this box might be wise. We attempted to better obscure
it's hiding place after we stamped in. If the placer reads this,
however, please don't retire the box -- hiking to these falls were
one of the highlights of our trip!

Another box (The Basin Letterbox by Amanda from Seattle) exists in
Franconia Notch, but we were not aware of it as the clues were
listed on the LbNA website under the "Mystery Boxes" section even
though the box nor its clues have any mystery element. We've
informed the placer.

We're eager to go back to hunt the boxes in the eastern section of
the White Mountains next -- Conway, North Conway, Glen, etc.!

Our trip enabled us to cross the F50 line ... and moving us closer
to our goal of 100 finds. We'll certainly get there before the year
is over!
