Hey'all! Since the LbNa board is down, and they've probably had
enough of this on there, anyway:

Yesterday we found the Smith's Castle letterbox in Wickford, RI.
Being an enormous box, it's kinda hard to hide, but it's hidden
now... better than when I spotted it.

The other box we found, Grandma's Letterbox, wasn't hidden at all. I
spotted it from 40 feet away. It was just as bad as the photo from
Silent Doug and Clueless that appeared in Mark & Sue's blog.

Actually it was worse. The lid wasn't fastened, and the bag
containing the stamp and ink pad were full water. At least the
logbook had been placed right on top of the bundle of bags (no, it
wasn't bagged), so it was relatively dry. Whether the last finder
was a true boxer or not, I don't know. There was no stamp-in on the
last used page, just handwriting. It was a child, probably under 10,
judging by the handwriting.

So when I replaced the box, after drying it and supplying fresh
baggies, I used a small rock to dig out some loose dirt from under
the big rock and give the box more room under the big rock. Then I
arranged as many rocks as I could in front of the hidey hole. There
wasn't much else I could do, as this area is maintained to some
degree, and it appears that any plant debris is removed or blown
away.

Just a case in point to illustrate the re-hiding problems we all
encounter from time to time.
