From: uneksia | Date: 2006-08-13 22:22:22 UTC
i am an avid mystery solver. many that i work on i might get to actually go
find some day. question i have is how many letterboxers out there actively
seek mysteries? seems that there are more and more easy boxes with hand held
clues to walk you directly to the spot.

what made me think of this is i have 2 mysteries out there for over a year
now. both are pretty easy, just a tiny bit of thinking required. one has
only been found 4 times and the other, which is based on the first ones
clues, has not been found at all, not even by wanda and pete. now i don't
mind that they have not been found. they will sit there patiently waiting
and rewarding those that do take the time. but, it did make me wonder if the
art of mysteries is going to be eventually lost to the easy no brainers.

smile
uneksia

From: Chris | Date: 2006-08-13 23:03:18 UTC
Originally we would find the local boxes throughout towns in new
london county (connecticut). Our 3rd day in letterboxing we got our
first connecticut mystery. we only got 4 mysteries or so our first
year. the last few months (especially since LBNA came back online)
i've been printing out many mysteries to solve and find. some
people enjoy challenges and we are some of those people. it all
depends on what you're looking for. as newbies we wanted to get the
feel of letterboxing. now we're more experienced we want the
challenge.

chris

I love the mysteries. I have found that I tend to plant mysteries more than
anything...I think only one of my boxes is a straight forward box...all the
rest are mysteries.

I love when folks finally figure it out and I get a happy email telling me
so...or when someone comes up at a gathering and asks me if they are 'hot'
or 'cold'.

I think I value a mystery 'find' more than a straight forward 'find' too.
After you have deciphered clues or spent time researching an answer, it
means more.
