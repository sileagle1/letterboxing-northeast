NY: Ithaca's Plantations box is gone

From: TurtleMcQ | Date: 2005-05-13 01:44:07 UTC
I happened to walk by the location of this great box (the second I ever
found) last week and saw that it had been exposed due to an enormous tree
falling on and breaking the rock formation it was hidden in. The
landscapers at Cornell are very industrious and I knew the box would be in
jeopardy if left there while the tree was being removed, so I brought it
home and contacted the owners. I'm very sad to report that they've decided
to retire the box for good and I'll be giving it (and its three very full
logbooks) back to them on Sunday.

For those of you who weren't lucky enough to visit it, Plantations was
planted by TRIO in September 2001 and was one of the oldest surviving boxes
in NY (and hidden on a gorgeous little creekside path, to boot). Just in
case anyone had already printed out clues in anticipation of looking for it
while in Ithaca for the Finger Lakes gathering on June 11th, be forewarned
that the clues have been removed from LBNA and the box is gone.

It will be missed...

(a most dejected) TurtleMcQ
