From: Steve and Heidi | Date: 2004-04-14 16:00:33 UTC
Mark & all other letterboxers-
Excellent job on the 2nd draft! I definately think we needed to
qualify these box listings. When we travel long distance we like to
stop once in a while and a short walk or drive-by is a good idea.
Perhaps a 1/2 hour lunch break hiking is in order once in a while
too. Our concern was simple: You could very easily get caught up
letterboxing and veer way off your travelling game plan!

For those who will be suggesting boxes, here's a good rule of thumb.
Driveby's rule when travelling, but also consider places of
significance to that specific area. I.E. in South Eastern CT you
would probably suggest Captain's Walk, Fort Griswold, and Fort
Trumbull as must-haves for the I-95 traveller. These boxes have
meaning to this are and make for a fine representation of our neck of
the woods. They can all be done, including getting off and back on
the highway in a matter of 20 minutes.

We'll be travelling to S.C. for 4th of July week and look forward to
finding some boxes and learning about the areas we travel through!
Thanks again for all the hard work : )

-Steve, Heidi, and Madras
