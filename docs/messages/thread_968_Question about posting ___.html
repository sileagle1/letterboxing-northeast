From: cpascott | Date: 2004-07-13 14:18:00 UTC
Hi all!

I just redesigned the website I set up to track my and Anjabanja's
letterboxing experiences and statistics. Instead of simply listing
the boxes we've found, I decided to write a short description of our
quest to find it and, where applicable, provide some useful
information about the places we travelled in the process.
Essentially, this becomes a journal of our letterboxing adventures.

I'm in the process of adding some commentary to some of the boxes for
which I had not previously written anything. I also wanted to
include a couple of pictures with each entry. No pictures of the
find or its location, of course, nor any pictures that would
compromise the integrity of the clues will be posted, but rather some
nice shots of the scenery to compliment our story.

While it's nice for us to have and to look back on, I thought others
might be interested in hearing our tales as well. I know some people
here post their stories and I was thinking of doing the same (or at
least providing the link to the site where you can read them). As I
said, they are all pretty short.

But, because this is not a forum but an e-mail list, I thought I'd
ask the group's opinion and/or permission first. I don't receive e-
mail from these groups, but rather check the website for new
messages, but I could appreciate that people may not enjoy me
populating their e-mail boxes with the stories of my finds.

So, how about it?

As an example take a look here:
http://home.comcast.net/~scott.e.hall/finds/Boston_Tea_Party.html

Thanks!

CPAScott

We think you did a fine job on that website! The only caution I will
say is that you may want to list mystery boxes seperate and not put a
date with them. Sometimes listing them in order with the other finds
may be just enough of a hint to clue [ no pun intended ] someone in
on where the mystery is. Example - If you see I spent all day at
Rocky Neck State Park and there's the "ABCDEF Mystery" listed in the
middle, well, enough said?

Other than that, go for it! The enthusiasm is terrific and if people
don't want to read it, they don;t have to click on it!

-Steve and Heidi in CT


PS - Your best bet is to just list your website in the "Links"
section of this group.

Exactly! This all kind of goes back to a large part of the debate on
the LBNA list regarding the database. I'm making a big assumption
CPAScott that you were not a letterboxer prior to that huge
debate,mess,flame,thingamadgiggy. Essentially there was an idea to
make a database in that section of letterbox-usa to track last visits
and overall box status.

A lot of folks said it took the mystery out of finding boxes. A lot
had Phyto's opinion about not wanting to see any more info about the
clues than the placer had given. Several of us requested that our
clues not be commented on too. This wound up becoming a huge debate
and caused many hurt feelings to the point that someone even
vandalised the database and deleted a lot of stuff.

That backround being given, I think your idea of keeping a log and
journal on your website is perfectly fine. You will likely not see us
keeping that same log on our site for a few reasons: 1) We're lazy
and its not that important to us currently. 2) Don't want to risk
accidentally spoiling it 3) We're not big into writing. Most of the
clue writing we do is hard enough, you'll see my clues as technically
correct but I'm not at all gifted as a creative writer (i.e.
describing a hike on paper).

I'm gonna clip this short as I have no idea where these ramblings are
going (case in point re: my creative writing skills).

-Steve (and Heidi & Madras) in CT
