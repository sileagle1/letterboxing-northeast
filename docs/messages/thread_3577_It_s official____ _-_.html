It's official.... :-(

From: Pam | Date: 2007-07-22 21:26:29 UTC
I went down to Bluff Point and Harkness today. As far as I can tell, there are NO alive traditional boxes at Harkness State Park. However, the "non-traditional" boxes seem to have been spared of this horrible event.

Only 5 out of 24 boxes made it out alive from Bluff Point. Three were previously missing, and the rest were maliciously stolen from their hiding spots at Bluff Point. Two of the boxes made it out alive because I accidentally screwed up the clues on the letterboxing.org site (they were correct on Atlasquest). One made it out of sheer luck, since it was in its rightful place. One was found under a stone in the stone wall after someone dismantled it looking for the box. The fifth box was pulled earlier and hadn't had a chance to get back out again.
The five still alive are:
Don't Mess With Me
Pirates of the Caribbean
Peg Leg Polly
More Pirates of the New World
Scurvy Dog

Three of the boxes, to this day, I couldn't find the hiding places for (boxes 1, 3, and Dubloons) since I didn't plant those boxes. As with Harkness, non-traditional boxes seem to have been spared for the time being. We are working on trying to find out why someone would do this to entire series, and will be getting the remaining five boxes back to their owners, since we are now retiring the series.

I WOULD STRONGLY SUGGEST ARCHIVING BOXES ON LETTERBOXING.ORG UNTIL THIS SEEMS TO BLOW OVER.
I'M JUST SO MAD!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!

Music Woman

I work with surveillance equipment on a daily basis. The sad fact of
the matter, despite what we see on TV, is that photo documentation in
cases such as this is often of limited value. A camera may capture
tons of images, but unless someone knows the people in the photos,
identification can be next to impossible. Additionally, images would
have no value unless they captured someone walking away from the area
with box in hand without any successive images of the same person(s)
putting the box back in place. There is also the matter of placing
recording equipment on property other than one's own. I can't imagine
that DEP or anyone else in the state would approve of such a plan. I
have been the victim of theft and understand the violation one
experiences when property is take, but no matter how noble the cause,
law enforcement agencies take a dim view of vigilantism.

While it might be a good idea to contact the box owner to obtain
clues, I can foresee problems for the box hunter. I like to make the
most of the time I have for letterboxing, and to that end, I often
print out clues for a number of boxes in a given area and make a day
of it. I have no problem sending requests for clues to box owners in
advance, but I would have to depend on them to read and respond to the
messages in a timely fashion. If an owner is away or otherwise
unavailable to read his or her mail, it could be frustrating for the
letterboxer. Once in a while I get the boxing bug on the spur of the
moment, and waiting for clues could put a damper on that particular
process. Also, as someone noted previously, I could have clues and not
try to find the box for weeks. If a box winds up missing a day or two
after I obtain the clues from the placer, I don't want to be accused
or blacklisted simply because I'm the last person who was given the
information.

As to mail addresses, providers are bound by privacy laws, and can't
give out information about an account holder just because someone asks
for it. I wouldn't mind having to log in at a site in order to gain
access to clues. It doesn't provide any real security, but it might
deter the would-be poacher if he or she has to go a step beyond simply
visiting a site in order to view information.

The bottom line seems to be that there is no simple solution. My sense
is that whoever is responsible is some jerk who will tire of this
activity, hopefully sooner rather than later, and move on. I also
wonder - and this isn't meant to open a can of worms - if the
perpetrator could be a disgruntled significant other of an
enthusiastic letterboxer ("You're going letterboxing AGAIN?") who
decided to express his or her dismay by hijacking boxes in the area
where his or her partner searches for boxes. I know, it sounds a bit
sick, but stuff like this does happen.
