excited first timers in CT !!

From: T Prindle | Date: 2004-05-10 01:47:43 UTC
There was a cryptic reference to 'letterboxes' at our favorite place
to hike in Mansfield, CT in our local free paper we received
Saturday, and we had to go see what this was about, checked out a
website and headed straight out. We had SO much fun! Thanks to
Chuck and Molly for a great first letterbox with a elephant token
souvenir! Like excited newbies we were ill prepared with store-
bought rubber stamps of ol' and an ink pad barely moist, a log that
was humbly' more than a pocket calendar.... Alas we found the Horton
box and our life has changed !! (*smile, well, it was enriched).
Today, for mother's day, we took my mom with my sister and her family
on a letterbox hunt in my mom's childhood stomping grounds in Canaan
at the Beckley Furnace, and for the first time the whole family was
actually excited to do something as a group.

Well... I'm am rambling, just found the yahoo group for the NE
letterboxers, and wanted to share our initial excitement and thanks
for this great family activity! We will be sure to improve on our
signature stamps and log book!!

Tara P. and Brad P. in CT

Dear P-Family,

Thank you all for the warm welcome! In my spare moments in looking

familiar place! Brad and I are in Mansfield, and it will keep us
happily busy in our own backyard alone, indeed!

are waiting until we are better equipt!) down the road today and got
some pink speedy-pad to work on ... now waiting for the ispiration
for trail names and a signature stamp to carve for the P. family.
Maybe something to do with Brad's Indian/Ojibwe heritage or our
mutual love of 'nature' crafts.

What do other couples do? Do you have one stamp between the two of
you? or do you each have your own stamp?

Again thanks for the welcome
Tara P. and Brad P.
