Neat Mini Sewing Machine (logbooks, stamp covers, etc.)

From: Steve and Heidi | Date: 2004-01-13 23:01:01 UTC
For those of you who have never seen one, we found a neat little mini-
sewing machine under our Christmas tree this year. It is made by
ProvoCraft and called the Sew Crafty Mini. Stands about 6 or 7 inches
high and offers a single stitch pattern. It uses 4 AA batteries and
really zips through both fabric and paper. We've been experimenting
with it for logbook binding. Managed to make some neat rough copies
of books with it, and also used those fancy ruffle edged scissors.

I think the mini sewer runs about $14 at most craft stores (I think
this one came from AC Moore).

-Steve, Heidi, Madras
