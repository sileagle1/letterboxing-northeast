Before the weekend of 12 May is a good time to check up on your box
and if it isnt well hidden, bring it in. The following is from an
email I recieved at work today:

On Saturday, May 12, 2007, there will be a statewide cleanup effort to
get parks and beaches across Massachusetts ready for summer.
Volunteers are needed to help spruce up park facilities, clean
coastlines, maintain trails, plant flowers, and more. This effort is
being led by the office of Governor Deval Patrick, the Office of
Energy and Environmental Affairs, and the Department of Conservation
and Recreation (DCR).


[We are] getting involved! Through stewardship, we can help to improve
the quality of life in our neighboring communities. We are organizing
a cleanup challenge and are asking you to lend a hand to help clean up
Callahan State Park in Framingham. Both the DCR and the Town of
Framingham are our clients. Let's show them we care!

Callahan State Park in Framingham is a 820-acre day use area with
seven miles of marked trails and is used for activities that include
fishing, hiking, horseback riding and cross-country skiing. Within the
park are nearly 100 acres of open fields, 70 acres of which are
currently under an agricultural lease. [We] will be assisting in
cleanup activities through the park. I encourage all of you to
participate!

Children of all ages are welcome so we encourage employees to bring
their families! This is also a great way for all of us to have some
fun while getting to know other people in the company. I plan to be
there and hope to see many of you there as well.

Happy trails,
