From: Chuck and Laura Lubelczyk | Date: 2003-12-08 00:05:12 UTC
Phyto and all-
First let me say how pleased I am to see this list and how excited I am
to hear a winter gathering is in the works. I've managed to meet a few
of you, but there are still many more I have not met yet (but hope to soon).
As far as winter gathering places go, I love the building on top of Mt.
A, but the road is a bear in winter and they close it off sometimes due
to weather conditions. I'd especially rule it out if we would have to
pay a rental fee.
I've got another idea for a spot you're familiar with and love and that
I might just have a bit of pull at <grin>. Think Laudholm Farm in the
Mather Auditorium where we could have it for free. We don't have a
fireplace, but have got plenty of tables and chairs, audio-visual
equipment if we wanted it, access to a kitchen, and lots of historic
charm. There's also 7 miles of trails perfect for snowshoeing and cross
country skiing with a few of Phyto's beautifully carved boxes planted
there. It's in southernmost Maine and just a quick jaunt off the
highway, so easy to get to. Maine is pretty far north for a lot of folks
who might be interested in coming though.
Nautilus
