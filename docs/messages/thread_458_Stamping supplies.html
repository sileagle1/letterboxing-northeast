Re: [LbNE] Stamping supplies

From: Fisher & family | Date: 2004-04-01 16:30:43 UTC
Hi. We made our personal stamps with the small white erasers, (2.5 x 1). And I made a stamp for a box using the larger white eraser, (1.5x3). They were carved with an xacto knife and wood cutting tools. Not masterpieces but we love them.

I didn't catch what area you are from in CT but Kathy Johns in Storrs is a very fun spot to check out. And the Lily Pad in downtown Willimantic has some supplies (white erasers, log books...)

From what I've gathered, depending on what carving medium you use you may have to mount it on a piece of wood.

Have fun, Fisher

From: dave & diane | Date: 2004-04-02 04:11:59 UTC
I've done moderately well with the cheapo erasers. The only problem I ran into was the size of the canvas to work on. I've also got the high-speed pink stuff, but for smaller stamps I'm just as happy keeping the costs of my hobbies down with a good ol' Dollar Store rectangle. If they degrade or something, then I'll have another reason to carve more stamps! Win-win!

I have always bought all my stamping supplies from Stampeaz at
www.stampeaz.com. She (the owner) has great stuff & great prices.
