From: lb_isosceles | Date: 2004-11-30 17:37:43 UTC
Hi Moonstone,

If you have access to a photocopier, you could make a copy of your
printed image, and use the photocopy as exhibit A for the ironing
process.

Ironing is really the way to go, though, since you get a super sharp
image without any mess or residual brain damage from inhaling fumes
from <insert solvent of choice>.

One caveat, even with a toner based image, ironing does not seem to
work on certain carving blocks, most notably the crumbly cream
colored stuff. No matter, just use Mastercarve or the pink stuff and
life will be good.

Oh, and don't let the iron get too hot or linger too long, or the
carving block can undo itself and end up half stuck to the paper.
That can really detract from your overall carving experience.

-Isosceles
