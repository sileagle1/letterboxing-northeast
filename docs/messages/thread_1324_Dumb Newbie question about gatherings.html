Hi Kerri!

As far as Clues for the Monster Mash - Chuck & Molly will be posting
the clues October 1st on their website http://www.chuckstraub.com/

I think because this is such a HUGE gathering, with over 100 1-month-
only letterboxes (well, some letterboxes may be replanted in other
areas but that's besides the point)...I highly doubt that someone
will be printing out 100-300 sheets of clues, making copies, and
having them on hand for anybody to take is a bit farfetched -- but
some clues may be printed out and made ready at the gathering for
letterboxers - I'm not sure if that will happen or not. There's too
many boxes for one person to do in one day, not to mention the fact
that there are other contests at the gathering pavilion...as well as
the overwhelming amount of people attending...

*takes a breath of air*

Anywho, I would just print out a portion of boxes so you have clues
with you - I don't think clues will be available at the event (some
letterboxes might have clues to them, like day-only ones or
whatever), but it's best to take out insurance and have clues to
however many boxes you'd like to search for - or any certain ones
that spark your interest (may I suggest the 'Carrie' letterbox by me
& Pigtographer? hee hee, just kidding)...

Pigtographer and I have been to 6 or 7 gatherings/mini gatherings
(some that had clues available, some where you had to bring your
own), and I think our plan of action is to split up the day - I'm
under the assumption that Chuck and his cohosts will have all the
boxes planted before the clues are online and before the gathering
starts. Pigtographer and I will probably print up 10-25 clues for
different boxes (multiple box series' are always fun) and box before
the gathering (early Saturday morning) and maybe a little bit into
the gathering - then the latter half we will most likely meeting new
and old letterboxers alike. Everybody has their own agenda - and
people will be in and out of the gathering all day, so just do what's
best for you and your troupe of letterboxers and enjoy the gathering!

Being me, I'm probably rambling on, so to finish it off: look through
the clues on Friday, October 1st, find some that you and your
relatives might be interested in and print them out prior to the
gathering the next day. Make sure not to forget anything you might
need, and enjoy the experience that is a gathering, not to mention
that if there are very few or no clues available at the gathering -
you will have clues on hand...and you won't be like Pigtographer -
(this is not, I repeat, not a mockery, because Pigtographer herself
say this alllll the time...) you won't be "clueless" in more than one
way. ;-)
