We Lost Everything

From: von der Insel | Date: 2010-01-23 22:04:56 UTC
Today, Dave and I went out Letterboxing after about a 3 month hiatus.
We thought we'd find a couple of boxes and then go to the movies.
Great Plan!
Unfortunately, after our last letterbox - Forgotten Families # 9 & 10 (great cemetery, by the way!) in West Warwick, RI,
we forgot our letterboxing bag on the trunk and drove off down Church St. towards Cowesett Ave.
We think between St. Mary's Church and the next Cemetery (#8) the bag came off the car.
We noticed we didn't have the bag when we got to Route 2.
We retraced our "steps" and drove back to the cemetery on Church St., but found nothing but our bottle of Technu in the street.
We asked at the Church if anyone found the bag, but no luck. We will leave our contact information with the rectory this evening.

The bag is a navy blue fanny pack with two patches on the front pocket - AQ and LBMA.
Inside the bag there is our stamps! (including personal travelers), our logbook!, a zippered bag of ink pens and pads, Dave's lensatic compass, and my favorite compass with reverse azimuth capability. There is also a newly-found Hitchhiker!

Needless to say, we lost interest in the movie (maybe tomorrow).

We can re-find the letterboxes in the logbook, we can re-carve the tower and the mobius braid personal stamps - don't know if we can find the sheep stamp - she's been with us from the very beginning - May 10, 2003!
So, if you're from (or in) the West Warwick, RI area and find our bag we will be totally indebted to you --
as reward - the HH is yours and a fresh new block of pink stuff.

So, think good thoughts that our bag will find it's way home to us; fortunately there were only about 33 stamp images in this logbook.
On the drive home, Dave and I thought that maybe the finder would wonder what this letterboxing stuff is all about and discover a new hobby!

Bye for now - got some re-carving to do!
Diane
the von der Insels

From: Teresa Gallagher | Date: 2010-01-25 05:30:13 UTC
Were you in a particular park managed by some group or town? If the bag fell
off the trunk near the parking lot someone might turn it in. I had a car
key turned in to me once (I work for a town) during the fall when all the
leaves were falling and I posted it 'found' on our trails website. The
owner was shocked to see the notice a couple weeks later. If there is a sign
kiosk a note might be posted there; if it's a town park it might be turned
into the Parks or Conservation Departments; if it's a Land Trust someone in
that organization might know about it.



Good luck. I lost my camera boxing last fall after I set it down on the
pavement getting into my car. Just left it there in the parking lot.



-Tess
