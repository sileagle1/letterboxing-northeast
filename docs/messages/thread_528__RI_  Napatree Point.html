(RI) Napatree Point

From: mary_rhode_island | Date: 2004-04-13 20:27:21 UTC
If your box is there, it's alive and well. Ditto for the
lighthouse. There was a reference to a nearby geocache in one of
the logbooks, but we didn't find it.

It was somewhat wet and wild out there yesterday, but I would prefer
prickers and naked mystery plants (vines and others) to fields of
poison ivy in full foliage. That place could use a machete. ;-)
The beach was wide open, meaning no plover lines. Those must go up
during a defined nesting season. No bugs yet. Lots of waterfoul,
including loons. There were a few dogs running free with their
owners, but none ventured up to the dunes, and none were out of
control. The signs say dogs are to be kept on leash at all times.
We saw no osprey.

For boxers who have not been out on this strip of beach: This is a
better time to go than mid-to-late summer for ease of access. Some
people even prefer the winter. Besides a minmum amount of people and
dogs, and poison ivy, there was lots of parking available, and we
drove down the lane ("walkers welcome") to the lighthouse without
incident. If we go back to the point in warmer weather, it will be
for the beach and water's edge. Sunsets there are really nice, too,
and worth the trip. I wouldn't recommend this area for night boxing
due mostly to unsafe footing, especially inland through the
shrubbery, etc., so plan to catch the sunset after boxing instead of
before. ;-) The lighthouse should be done in dry daylight, too,
and even then be aware that the dry loose rocks are tricky and not
for the weak ankled.

Mary (RI)
