CT State Parks - new policies coming

From: donutz716 | Date: 2008-07-02 16:15:19 UTC
Hi All!
 
I went to the DEP in Marlborough today to find information about an unmarked trail off the Airline Trail.  During the conversation, I was told that the DEP is currently coming up with new policies on planting letterboxes in the State Parks.  I can only tell you what I was told - when the policies are completed the information will be posted to the DEP website - sometime this year.  They are considering that all boxes require a permit.  Any boxes without permits would be removed.  That's all I know, but thought I should disseminate the info to the group. 
 
Enjoy!
donutz716

Re: CT State Parks - new policies coming

From: birder579a | Date: 2008-07-04 14:47:20 UTC
Recently I had the pleasure of speaking with Diane Joy, who was
promoted to head of the state parks division of the DEP this spring.
Previously she was in charge of the No Child Left Inside program, and
is a big supporter of letterboxing. Anything to get people out of
the house and into the woods is high on their agenda. I am hopeful
of a good outcome.

The Forestry division is seperate from Parks. When I emailed with
the previous director, Don Smith, he said he was worried that the
state may be liable to a planter if their operations destroyed a
box. Such things as bulldozer road clearing, harvesting of timber by
timber companies, or prescribed burning all could wreck a box.

As far as stone walls go, I had the opportunity to speak with the
stone wall man of Connecticut, Dr Robert Thorson. He said about
letterboxing, that he doesn't mind stone walls being used. The
abomonation is pulling the stones up and crating them off to be sold
elsewhere. "New England stone walls" can be bought in midwestern
landscaping stores.
The Bird Stamper

From: Ellen Dill | Date: 2008-07-04 15:51:09 UTC
I have always loved the look of NE stone walls.  Then I saw a show on PBS last weekend about deer ticks.  At this time of year the larvae of deer ticks infest mice in great numbers, leading to the continuous growth in the number of deer ticks.  The biggest area of their proliferation?  New England.  Why?  The NE stone walls are like "Mice Luxury condos" so our mouse population is so high that the deer tick problem is spiraling!   My love of those pretty walls is dwindling . . .
