This series was planted by Irish Tinker alomost a year ago. As with
virtually all her letterboxes, the historical background info. is
fascinating to read. Stamps are a distinct pleasure also. All boxes
are intact (I did some minimal drying out and placing paper towels
around stamps. There is a overly wet ink pad at box # 3 so I used
one of mine.
I had the trail to myself, just an occasional bird call. Note:
muddy does not even begin to describe the trail--streams cross it, so
be sure to wear waterproof boots.
Now for another alert: Sheesh!! Sorry, but feel I must comment.
Walking back from the last box, after the Y and before the Blarney
Castle l.b. I saw 2 used syringes on the trail. (Cars have driven in
on the trail even though it is forbidden.) I did not have the
capabilities to safely pick up and dispose of these items. If any of
you are going out there and are willing to use a plastic bag as a
glove to place these items in a hard sided container of cardboard or
plastic to dispose of at home that would be wonderful.
