From: birder579 | Date: 2004-12-03 20:15:54 UTC
The hairy roots are a dead (oops) giveaway. I had a friend say to
me "don't be a dope and grab the hairy rope". I follow her advice
always. In the fall it does get white berries. They are a favorite
of several kinds of wild animals. They are not affected by the
oils. In the winter the berries turn more of a gray color.
The Bird Stamper
