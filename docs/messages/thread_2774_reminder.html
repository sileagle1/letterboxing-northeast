Hay letterboxers, please take the time to cover the letterbox when you
are done stamping. Make sure the log book is safe inside the baggie and
closed tight. Yesterday Lobsta and I stopped to check on Thomas
Carrier as we were going by. What a good thing. The boxers that were
there on March 20th left the box in plain sight, the cover not on tight
and worse, the log book on top of the baggie and not even inside . It
was very sad as there was even water in the box. We fixed it up but
were wishing we knew where this boxer hurried off to next and might
have done the same thing.
I am sending a note to them , they are not new boxers.
Sad, Butterfly .

Re: [LbNE] reminder

From: uneksia | Date: 2006-03-22 13:47:00 UTC
along with the reminders to hide well is a reminder to the placers. winter
and spring take a toll on boxes. what was well hidden in the fall might not
be so in the spring. not always is it a finders fault that the box is not
well hidden. take note of what you are covering your boxes with. a handful
of leaves and pinecones is not going to be there come spring. under that log
might seem like a good place, but the spring rains have a tendency to sweep
away the leaves you had covering it. often times we tend to cover the
letterbox with the same materials that the placer has used, i think this is
particularly true of newbies who are leery of changing anything. so, rehide
well and plant well!
smile
uneksia

Just to reinforce this point...

When I went to Devil's Hopyard a couple of weeks ago, I found Hopbox without
its lid on at all. It had been left completely open, and plenty of water had
gotten inside it and subsequently froze. The logbook was destroyed. Now that
it's dry, you can read it, but it's not useable. I had to put a new one in.
Judging by the looks of it, it was a new boxing group to find it (no stamps,
little kid handwriting saying something like "This is fun!") but I wish I
could get in touch with them and say, "Look what happens when you're not careful!"
