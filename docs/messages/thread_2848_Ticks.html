From: Chuck and Laura Lubelczyk | Date: 2006-04-20 23:44:01 UTC
As someone who works with ticks (and mosquitoes) I can say that they are
really out there and have been for a few months. Essential oils don't
really work. To my knowledge, there is no scientific evidence to back
this up. However, a compound commonly sold at outdoor supply stores (LL
Bean, EMS, even WalMart) called permethrin is quite effective at keeping
ticks off of clothing. Permethrin is only applied to clothing, not skin,
and will last for several wash cycles. It breaks down in contact with
skin oils but doesn't do anything detrimental. You can still use DEET
though on your skin, but keep it at 20%. Using the 100% doesn't give you
any extra protection.
A good website with info is the CDC Lyme site
www.cdc.gov

-Culex

From: Phyto | Date: 2006-04-21 00:46:51 UTC
Short of curtailing your outdoor activities, you can do as Culex
suggested and in addition, you can check yourself afterwards
thoroughly - and wash your clothes right away. It has been my
experience that the wooded areas of Southern Maine have a much higher
(deer) tick population in proportion to other areas. To date, I have
not noticed a significant tick issue in Northern Maine, 'cept the time
that I was walking a local trail in Bangor near the animal shelter
alongside a river. Man, watch out for that!
