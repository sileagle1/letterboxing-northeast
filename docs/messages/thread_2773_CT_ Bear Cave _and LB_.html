--- In LbNE@yahoogroups.com, Pam <mindizney@...> wrote:

> Do I dare ask what peepers are???????

Peepers are a true sign of spring in the vernal pool ecosystem. It's
the spring peeper (a frog) that is the first to begin its mating call.
The ubiquitous "peep" is usually heard in the early spring evenings
around marshy/wetland areas or even in your backyard as these frogs
are tolerant of the light frost evenings when temperatures dip down.

Here's a great description of spring peepers:
http://www.montshire.org/minute/mm030428.html
