NW CT Sunday boxing report: some soggy letterboxes!

From: mindizney@aol.com | Date: 2004-09-20 10:00:36 UTC
Yesterday, PSU fan and I went out in search of some letterboxes in the NW
corner of CT, starting in New Hartford. We started out at the Nepaug series and
DEP boxes in Nepaug State Forest. Box 1 of the Nepaug series has a cracked
top on both the box and the inkpad, and I'm surprised to see that no water was
in the box, especially after Ivan came through yesterday. It does need a new
box though, and I'll e-mail the planter later on today.

We then went over to Endless Inkspots and after huffing up the hill (I was
huffing, Peter wasn't....hmmmm), we found it with no problems and no water
inside.

Brodie Park was another story. We were able to find the box in short order,
however, it was totally soaked inside, both inside the box and inside the
stamp and ink pad ziplock. We dried out things as best as we could, thankful that
the log, in its separate ziplock was not harmed.

We then headed out to my favorite falls in NW CT and had a picnic near the
falls. We couldn't picnic on the rocks by the falls since they were wet and
practically underwater! The falls were so impregnated with water due to Ivan
that they overflowed the banks of that section of the river. We decided to
picnic near the gorge and after a nap in the crisp afternoon air, found Waterfalls
12 six steps from where we picnicked! Thankfully, the second river in the
area had not overflowed its banks and the box was fine!

After we got so cold that we had to pack up for the day, we tried for the
Crawdad (again, since I tried with Butterfly and Bluebird earlier this year) in
Winsted, however, even on the second try, I still couldn't find it!
Unfortunately, Peter wasn't able to help since he's extremely allergic to poison ivy!
He stood on a large rock away from the forest floor and tried to direct me, but
no luck. Hmmmmmm is it missing???????
Thanks to those who plant for a great day!
