Letterboxing on the 1st day

From: Northstar | Date: 2004-01-02 14:52:05 UTC
What a way to ring in the new year- a day of letterboxing! I decided
to visit HOP BROOK LAKE in Middlebury, CT. U.S. Presidents series #1
& #2 are both in good condition. Aside from finding more than a few
of them to be uncovered or in plain sight, it was nothing a little
creative re-hiding couldn't fix. I also added a few new baggies where
needed. Aaron's birthday box is a different story. The logbook was
soaked, and the moisture is causing the metal rings to rust and
corrode the book. I've witnessed first-hand what this can result in
if left unchecked.(i.e., a completely destroyed book)If someone can
get out there to retrieve the book soon, all it needs is a week of
drying to save it. I searched and searched for "MY HEART" but was not
able to locate this box. Was I looking in the wrong area? I hope so.
Perhaps someone can make it out there to check on it soon and let me
know. "RED SKY AT NIGHT" is in good condition. Neat logbook, RUBADUC!
Lastly, I traveled down the road to NAUGATUCK where I set off to
find "CEMETARY SEARCH 1" at the suggestion of a fellow letterboxer.
The box was in good condition, and I got a H.H.! Add that to the
other H.H. I found earlier in the day, and my box total for the
day=15! Just 15 more boxes to my 1st 100! Hmmm....where to next? --
NORTHSTAR
