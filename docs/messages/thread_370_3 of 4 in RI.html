3 of 4 in RI

From: Don | Date: 2004-03-15 00:13:43 UTC
HI
Today I trekked out with my wife Sheila and Grateful Dawg to visit a
couple of sites. The first area was to Sins of the Flesh at Fort
Barton, Tiverton, RI. The hiking was great; we found a great
waterfall running though a small gorge and great views of the bay
from the fort. We couldn't find the 1st letterbox but the other two
are fine. We'll have to try again another time for no. 1.

We then traveled over to Bristol and the Read Letterbox. Which had
several books for trade and a nice view of the bay. After that a
visit to Aden's for a Guinness and a early St Patrick's meal of corn
beef and cabbage to finish off the weekend.

Trail Turtle

A Boxing Day...

From: buddyandpigtographer | Date: 2004-03-15 01:07:03 UTC
Well, I guess we wern't the only ones out boxing today...

After finding Mushroom & Broken Heart of the Favorite Ornaments
Series the day before the POE Gathering, Pigtographer and I headed
back to Burlingame State Park to finish out the series.

We finally found the Cuckoo Clock! As well as the rest of the
series, except for 'Stocking'...we knew we had the right place
according to the clues, but no Stocking Stuffer for us.

Halfway through the series we stopped to get Sadie's &
Russ's 'Camping At Burlingame' LB.

Even though it was rather cold in the woods today - the ticks are out
and a movin'! I found a deer tick crawling up the leg of my jeans
and hastily flicked it off. (I found it when we found the Gingerbread
Man - so I'm thinking it hitched on when I was looking for Pickle.)

After having lunch, we drove to Jamestown to look for the N.E.S.S.
Series - it seems that not many people have been out to look for
those! Those were a lot of fun and a very creative idea. We found
all three with ease.

On our way back, we made a pit stop at the von der Insels' Rhode
Island Directions Mystery LB - Pigtographer hadn't found it, but I
found it last summer. Anyways, no one had visited that box since
November 2003, so we cleaned it up - wrapped the stamp in a new paper
towel and put it in a new plastic baggie - there was a lot of
condensation within the LB. Wiped out the container as well, and the
logbook. We rehid it carefully!

We had a great time today and picked up some great stamps - thanks to
everyone who continues to plant LBs!!! (We really liked the
letterbook idea - maybe we could extend it to another library...)

On January 1, 2004, I went in search of this series. At the time, it
had been noted that both "Christmas Tree" and "Stocking" were MIA.

I found Mushroom, Cuckoo Clock, Broken Heart, Pickle, Gingerbread
Man, Kindergarten Keepsakes, and then half heartedly went into the
Christmas Tree area, knowing that several were missing. That area
was so intriguing (to me) to poke about that I ended up off trail
nearly a half mile away deeper into the woods. Much to my surprise,
sitting out in the open, getting a little sun, was the Christmas Tree
letterbox that had gone missing in June 2003.

I rushed back to the correct area, put Christmas Tree back into its
place (after stamping in, of course!), then put in an earnest search
for the rest of the series. I found Gold Star and Candy Cane and
then got completely stumped by Stocking. I searched for Stocking
where it belonged and also where it didn't belong (who says
lightening doesn't strike twice?). By this time, the light was just
beginning to start to fade, so I had to rush out, and never got the
chance to search for the rest of the ornaments. It's nice to know
that, with the exception of Stocking, the rest of the ornaments *are*
there so that when I go back, I'll focus on the "known found" ones
first and *then* use my psychic power (bwahahaha) to find Stocking.

"Aaaahhhhllll be bahhhhhck"

Warrior Woman
