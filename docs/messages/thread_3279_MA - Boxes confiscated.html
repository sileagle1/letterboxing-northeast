From: Warrior Woman | Date: 2006-10-16 14:41:55 UTC
After being out in the wild since 2001-2004 to present, every one of my
letterboxes were confiscated by (I assume) Park Rangers in Hopkinton
State Park, Hopkinton, MA. The boxes that are now gone and are marked
as retired are:

Marathon
The Great Flame War (3)
Piece of Cake

Others who have planted in Hopkinton State Park (both sides) may wish
to check on their boxes as well. There are at least two other series
and a few singles.

who will not be replacing these boxes

> WW, any chance that they would give them back to you?

I am wrestling with this two sided sword.

I went out and confirmed that yes, indeed, they are gone - VERY
NEATLY taken away. My contact information was inside every single
box, but I have not been contacted by the park officials.

As we all know, many of us who plant boxes adopt the "it is easier to
ask for forgiveness than to ask permission" when it comes to local
and state parks.

If I do nothing, I have lost the boxes, the stamps, and most
importantly, the logbooks.

If I go to the park officials, and identify myself as the planter and
could I please have the boxes back (by now they are surely trash), I
risk being fined for whatever violation I committed.

Sigh.

I am just happy that these boxes had a long and happy life, and that
maybe some of you got a chuckle about "The Great Flame War" series.

Warrior Woman

Re: MA - Boxes confiscated

From: cpascott | Date: 2006-10-17 19:33:23 UTC
This is very concerning and worth further investigation to see if, in
fact, the park rangers at Hopkinton did take the boxes.

Obviously, there are many boxes in State Parks and Forests here in MA
that sit without issue. To the best of my knowledge, no policy
exists which prohibits Geocaching or letterboxing on DCR property,
although some permit policies were being drafted.

As I was intending on holding a letterboxing gathering at Hopkinton
next summer, I'll call them and inquire more specifically. Perhaps I
can obtain the information necessary to confirm whether the DCR is
intending on following up with its permit policy or restrict the
hobby altogether. A real shame either way -- DCR properties are
pretty significant tracts of land around here!

CPAScott
