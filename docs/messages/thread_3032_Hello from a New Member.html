Hello from a New Member

From: queenmab_82 | Date: 2006-06-26 17:25:05 UTC
*waves to all*

I just started letterboxing this spring, so I'm very new to the hobby
(I think I'm up to a whopping 13 found). Most of the time I'm bumming
around UNH, pretending to work on my MS thesis when I'm instead
perusing letterbox clues and wishing I were anywhere but at work.

This past weekend, I camped with a bunch of friends up at Moose Brook
State Park, and got the (brand-new: I was the first!) letterbox at
Crystal Cascades on my way up Mt. Washington on Saturday. It took me
6 hours, but I made it to the top (my first attempt on this mountain,
plus I'm woefully out of shape) via Tuckerman Ravine and back down
over the Lion's Head, taking another 3 hours to get back down. My
friends all hike a lot faster than I do, so I was pretty much alone
from the base of the ravine headwall up to the summit and the entire
way back down.

I am so very very sore today!

~mab.

From: queenmab_82 | Date: 2006-06-27 13:53:42 UTC
I was thinking that myself, on my trip back down. It was starting to
get late into the afternoon (I hit the summit at 3:40 and started back
down at 3:50!) and I was hurrying because I didn't want to still be on
the mountain when it started to get dark - but I didn't want to hurry
to much in case I tripped and injured myself! As I came out the
bottom, I met up with them, "just" on their way back up to start
looking for me.

I've done other hikes by myself - but they have all been much smaller,
as when I did Mt. Major to snag the Compass Rose letterbox :) So I do
have some confidence in my abilities to take care of myself - but it's
a pretty big difference between Mt. Major and Mt. Washington, both in
size and in the number of people you're likely to meet on the trail!

~Mab
