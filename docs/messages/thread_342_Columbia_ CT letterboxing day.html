Columbia, CT letterboxing day

From: rospa10 | Date: 2004-03-08 03:51:39 UTC
I got a late start to the letterboxing day (3:00) but certainly was
outside my home before the hunt began (paying bills, making a grocery
shopping list..just plain enjoying the sun). I headed to Columbia and
ladned at Mono Pond Park where I'd never been before. Found
NeeDeep's "Indiana stamp" (my 6th find of the series) with a
hitchhiker alongside! Discovered "Mom's Birthday" l.b.on way back to
car and thought it most sweet and touching.
Then I tackled "Sweet Innocents" a 2 box series by my friends
Columbian Bugs and Paws across Columbia. The trail is a soup, I must
say. I waded through a swamp, not a vernal pond, to get to box # 1.
Container dry inside, just replaced wet outside baggie. The trail is
nice, just be sure to wear above ankle boots in spring time. Box # 2
was gone! I found the spot and saw hiding sticks. Hopefully it was
pulled for repairs.
My last l.b. find was Laurie's (Columbian Bug Lady) Crank-Lebanon
Letterbox series #4. On my way back to car,met a young man eagerly
preparing to send a transister (don't know the proper name..) plane
into flight.
It was a peaceful yet productive weekend for me.
Catlover
