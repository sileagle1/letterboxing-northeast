Soaked boxes

From: Phyto | Date: 2003-12-31 00:26:13 UTC
Here's a short story and some information that might be helpful to
other boxers. I recovered a box today that I had already moved once
before in it's short lifespan of only 2 months. It became obvious as
the leaves dropped and the rain began pouring that the downward sloped
hillside of this location was due for some saturation. Needless to say
the rooted stump where this box was originally located became an empty
void for rain to fill.

Thus, I moved this box slightly higher and used a more effective rock
cover. Thinking that this would help I blindly left the box. As most
snow has vanished and subsequent melting and runoff has occured I came
back today and noticed that this particular spot must be built on
glacial marine mud as it is sickly wet and not apt to dry until June!!

The moral of this story is: check out the box during different
seasons. As most trail maintenance people would agree also - check it
out during the rain - even if you're not apt to plant a box during the
rain, make note of where the rain runs off so that you can note to
yourself where NOT to put a box - ie: in spots where rain and
meltwater will collect.

I've learned my lesson with this. One final note is that when placing
a box in a location that you're unsure of perhaps anchor the box and
use flat rocks for drainage underneath as well as on top. What's below
the box seems to be just as important as what's on top covering it in
my experience.
