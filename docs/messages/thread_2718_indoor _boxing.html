Re: indoor 'boxing

From: Rick in Boca | Date: 2006-03-07 15:42:01 UTC
I had a wonderful weekend in Mystic with my new girlfriend. It was a
little windy for tourism, but it didn't matter. Let me put it this
way, we didn't see many of the sights [Rick blushes].

We drove up to Providence to visit the Culinary Arts Museum and
Archive at Johnson & Wales University but when we arrived we found it
closed due to spring break. Even though we called and spoke to them
last week about our plans. Maybe at the last minute they decided to
have spring break...

But like a boy scout, I was prepared.

So for Plan B I introduced her to letterboxing. This is a little like
explaining to somebody that your odd religion isn't really a cult
after all. I had carved her a personal stamp to get her started and
she really liked it.

Because we had all day for this and I wanted to get her started on
something where she could get the idea without having to go on a long
hike, we did The Trolley Series. What a fun series! And it made us
really come to love Providence. We found five of the seven boxes so
she was able to experience my kind of letterboxing: searching and
searching and searching for a box before giving up.

At Water Place Tunnel all the snow from the parking lot is now plowed
on top of the bushes where the box should be hidden.

Thanks to all of you who graciously sent invitations to box and
exchange. We'd love to come back up.
