ANNA, plus a bit of good news.

From: weathergleams | Date: 2004-09-18 16:05:03 UTC
The clue for our Maine mystery box, ANNA, has been very slighly, but
necessarily, revised. Just a heads up in case you printed the old
version.

With all the recent troubles with boxes being poorly rehidden, we
thought we'd pass along some good news. Our very first box was
placed in California, and we don't get to check on it very often,
and it's always nice to hear from a boxer who finds it. The other
day we had such a report, and the sender mentioned that the log
showed several accidental finders. This is very strange, as the box
cannot be seen by passers by, requires an unusual exercise by the
seeker and the removal of a substantial piece of cover! Properly
rehidden, it's invisible, and was when this last finder found it.
Clearly, somebody along the way must have slipped up big time in
rehiding the box. But here's the cool part: Every single one of
the accidental finders left a nice note, and some even tried to play
along by stamping the box's own stamp into the book. A few were
really intrigued by letterboxing and may have become converts!
Admittedly, we were very, very lucky, as this could easily have had
an unhappy ending. Fortunately, somebody finally made the box
invisible again. It's nice to be reminded every now and then that
the "right sort" are still out there!
