15.September=Archery Deer Hunting in CT

From: connfederate | Date: 2006-09-14 15:18:31 UTC
Just an FYI -- on tomorrow, Friday the 15th Sept. the Archery Deer
Hunting Season opens in Conn. Please check the CT DEP website (look
at the hunting abstracts) for particulars about all CT hunting season
dates. (NOTA BENE: not all hunters must wear orange, in certain
situations/seasons they may wear camo).

I recommend anyone going into the woods during any hunting season
should wear blaze orange visible from all sides: hat and
vest/sweatshirt; at the very least a blaze orange hat.
If you see a vehicle parked near where you intend to box, be aware
that a hunter or two could be in the area.

I intend this post to be informational, not political. Please do not
take this post as a reason or excuse to curse or promote any activity.

Be aware, be safe and get out and box!
Connfederate

Most archers are dedicated ethical hunters. Hunting deer with a bow
requires the hunter to get up close and personal, excellent stalking
skills or a good tree stand are a must. Most shots taken with a bow
are less than 40 yards so there's little chance of mistaking the
target. As connfederate says blaze orange is always a good idea but I
worry a lot more about the shotgun, rifle hunters than the archers
(and a whole lot more about the poachers).
