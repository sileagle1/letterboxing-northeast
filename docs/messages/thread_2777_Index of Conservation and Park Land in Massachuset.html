Hi all ...

I'm starting a little project to catalog all of the conservation
and/or park land in the Commonwealth of Massachusetts. My intent in
undertaking this project is to identify all of the usable, public
access land where letterboxes could be placed. The directory would
serve as a sort of idea list for placers as well as a listing of neat
and interesting places one might enjoy the outdoors, whether it be a
public park, forest, sanctuary, preserve, or other such conservation land.

I envision a database that includes the following information:

Name of the Conservation Area or Park
Location (Town)
Managing Authority
Acreage
Whether the land is publicly or privately owned
Whether the land is open to public use
Whether the land has established trails
Other information pertinent to letterboxers (such as restrictions)

Identifying all lands owned by either the federal or state government
is easy enough, as the majority of this land is public-use parks (e.g.
Great Meadows National Wildlife Refuge operated by the U.S. Fish and
Wildlife Service or Borderland State Park run by the Department of
Conservation and Recreation). What is challenging is finding,
identifying and listing all of the conservation and park land owned by
one of Massachusetts 300+ towns or countless Land Trusts, Conservation
Commissions, and other foundations and societies that own or manage
reserved land.

A preliminary review of association associations (that is, assocations
to which other associations belong) such as the Land Trust Alliance or
Massachusetts Land Trust Coalition has yield a listing of 877 parcels
of land so far -- and I'm certain that there are probably double that
many once the list is complete.

Given that large number, developing a complete listing is a daunting
project, but one I still wish to accomplish. So, I seeking help. If a
couple of us are willing to do a little research to build the
database, and then I can find a partner who'll help me develop a
database page within my website, I think the end result would be
rather useful. Who knows, if the project goes well, perhaps we can
expand it to the other New England states.

If interested in helping, contact me at seh-letterbox@....

Thanks!
