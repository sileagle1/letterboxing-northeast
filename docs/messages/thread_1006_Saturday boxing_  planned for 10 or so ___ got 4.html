Saturday boxing: planned for 10 or so ... got 4

From: cpascott | Date: 2004-07-19 19:23:18 UTC
Hi all ... I had some errands to do on Saturday, one of which
required me to be in Pawtucket, RI, so Anjabanja and I thought we'd
hit some letterboxes around the area.

I thought we might go after a couple that were in MA along the way to
RI, but the morning errands took longer than expected and I needed to
get to Pawtucket by a certain time.

We started by hunting the Small City letterbox in Central Falls.
Could not find it. Placer thinks it might be missing, but not
confirmed yet.

We did pick up the Scuba Steve and Fox in the Box letterboxes in
Pawtucket. Per the clues, the Scuba Steve box is hidden behind some
trees, but not very well hidden. The logbook had been destroyed due
to wetness. I can't imagine this box will survive long given it's
placement. We e-mailed the placer to let them know the situation
with the logbook.

We then headed to Lincoln Woods to get the Sports of Lincoln Woods
series, but I wasn't dressed for the hot weather, opting instead to
believe the weathermen who predicted the weather to be about 15
degrees below what it actually was! Rather than trounce around the
woods, we opted to come back another time. Same goes for the
Butterfly Farm boxes.

Instead, we headed over to Max's Minute (found!) and on to East
Providence where we hunted for the East Bay Bike Path Letterbox by
Bill & Craig and the East Bay Bike Path series II by donna. We found
the first with no trouble, but could not locate any of the three
boxes in the series. The placer confirmed that two of them were
recently found, but we may have had old clues. As we printed what
was on the LbNA website, we asked them for the updated ones and may
try again the next time we are there.

So, even though we hunted 8 actual boxes, we ended up with only 4.
That's OK, there's plenty of days left!

If interested you can read of our adventures for these boxes at our
website, http://home.comcast.net/~scott.e.hall/F.html. These finds
are boxes #16 - 19 on the "Published Boxes Found" list. There's some
brief commentary about the Slater Memorial Park, Charles I.D. Looff,
the East Bay Bike Path, the Squantum Association, and Brown
University as well. This time, we've added a few pictures. Enjoy!
