MMM thanks and results

From: cpascott | Date: 2004-10-04 17:50:29 UTC
Wow! Anjabanja and I have only been to one other gathering since
our start of letterbox in late June (we list 3 on our site due to
the Cat and the Canary traveling event stamp), but all we can say
about MMM is ... WOW!

We're not really huge on the whole exchange thing, although we very
much like to meet and chat with other letterboxers, so despite the
very large number of letterboxers there, we picked up only 31
exchanges between the two of us.

However, we enjoyed meeting every one and well as a number of
letterboxers whom we did not officially exchange with. Special
hellos to Northstar and Scarab! It was also nice to reconnect with
some of the letterboxers we've met previously, especially Team Green
Dragon and The Dream Catchers.

We were hoping that we'd hit 100 finds over the weekend. We came
into Mansfield with 82 finds (78 boxes and 4 PTs). Well, I have
finally finished our tally and we are amazed that we added some 59
boxes (including 2 permanent boxes and 1 HH) and 2 PTs bringing my F
count to a whopping 142 and Anjabanja's to 141! Our very first
letterbox find was June 19 ... only 15 weeks ago. We were averaging
between 5 and 6 boxes per week up to now ... and now are averaging
over 1 box found per day! Of course, these kinds of statistics
don't really mean all that much when you compare the very close
proximity of boxes in Mansfield, and how the MMM boxes were placed
compared to, say, the boxes we hunted in NH's White Mountains.
Regardless, we're excited to see our F-count jump and have now set
our sights on 250 as our next big target.

As I'm rambling on, let me stop to thank anybody who was involved in
the organization or operation of this event ... most specifically
the host, Chuck & Molly. We didn't have any real time to talk with
Chuck, but we pass along our gratitude anyway!

We started our quest Friday night around 6pm and hunted two boxes
that night ... we ran into a couple of other letterboxers, stumbled
upon a permanent box, and looked forward to Saturday.

We found 10 boxes before the meeting, got a couple of PTs at the
gathering, and hit our 100 mark with "Carrie", whom we hunted with
Warrior Woman. 34 boxes and 2 PTs later, we called it a night.
Although we've rountinely hunted boxes right up until dusk, this
night we met up with Scarab and a group from D.C. and hunted well
into the darkness -- picking up the boxes in the Munsters series and
the Bad Knees series as a result. Before we headed back to camp,
however, Anjabanja and I trekked near the old cemetery in the pitch
dark to pick up two more -- Attack of the 50 Foot Woman and Mummy
Dearest. That was rather a spooky adventure indeed!

We had an early start Sunday and nabbed 25 boxes before heading back
home. The biggest adventure this day was hunting the Dawnlanders
box and having to cross the river with the aid of a staff generously
left by another hiker or letterboxer. Somebody ought to build a
bridge ... my right foot is still wet!

We're hoping to come back to pick up more of the temporary boxes
this month and will be back in the spring or summer 2005 to start
hunting some of the hundreds of permanent boxes in the town as
well. Anjabanja's eager to show me around UConn, her alma mater, as
well (we didn't allow much time for anything other than letterboxing
this weekend).

It's gonna be some time before we get our journal-web site up to
date for all those boxes, but we're working on it. If you're
interested, you can see our stories and pictures at
home.comcast.net/~scott.e.hall/. I'm hoping to at least have the
list updated and attach pictures by tomorrow.

Anyway, time to stop rambling! Looking forward to the next
gathering which is not too far away (if you know the details!).

CPAScott
