As moderators of the big list, we've been having some discussions
with other moderators and Randy Hall (LBNA list owner) about the
general trends of that list and the trends of membership (i.e.
preceived, actual, and future trends). My comment the other day to
Randy himself was that what we (Heidi and I) see happening is that
general letterbox chatter, tips, etc seems to be prevalent on the
letterbox-usa list....while regionally specific posts of new boxes,
visits, local gathers etc seem to be occupying much of the space on
these regional talk lists.

No, Its not a bad thing at all...IMO why does someone in Oregon care
about a new box in CT? To be honest we went no mail on Lbna some
time ago and we simply take care of our moderator emails and sift
through the posts as we have time (or see replies, complaints, etc.).
We kept LBNE on "daily digest" and have found much more interest in
the local happenings, stories, news, sit-reps, etc. I guess it boils
down to who your target audience is. Something like Mark's post of
the CT County boxes is not a bad thing to cross post because people
from other areas might like to try it...but the locals also see the
post here on LBNE.

Remember that there are no rules. If you see something posted that
you already read elsewhere, feel free to skip it.
