My logbook also serves as a sort of diary so I would not freely hand
it around a gathering, sharing the images of stamps I've tracked
down. I have selectively shared it with people, showing all-time
favorite images for example.
How do others feel about logbooks and sharing them and the images
therein? Just curious. As an aside, the stories people share (in
person or on message board) are a fascinating part of letterboxing
for me.
I still maintain that a lovely carved event stamp should not be
relegated to a drawer, never to be seen "live and in person" again. I
also believe letterboxing does not have hard and fast rules in these
grey areas. Therefore, the final disposition of the event stamps
should be left to the discretion of the carver.
