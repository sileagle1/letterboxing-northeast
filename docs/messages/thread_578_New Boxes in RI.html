New Boxes in RI

From: wandaandpete | Date: 2004-04-23 21:57:18 UTC
Well, I have to say I've been a bit under the weather lately
(probably got bronchitis while boxing last week in the pouring
rain). So, instead of going further afield for hiking or letterboxing
adventures as originally planned, I've been staying close to
home and doing some more local planting. The other day I went
out with Laurette (who recently reached PFX 500 in New Mexico
 Congrats!) to put in a new series of 3 "Hungry Rocks" as an
add-on to "Dad's Birthday Box" in Exeter, RI. We also did an on-
the-spot replacement of "One More Little Feather" while we were
out that way! Then, the next day, since I was out doing my
annual "arbutus sniffing walk" at Kimball Audubon Sanctuary, I
though I might as well put out the "Trailing Arbutus" box in a new
location this time just over the border in Burlingame, as had
been suggested by the Audubon refuge chairman. Hope those
of you who didn't have a chance to get that one before can now
find it in its new temporary home.

I'm also working on several other series that should be coming
out soon, as I'm making an effort to try to get to P100 before
reaching PFX 5000! That should be happening sometime within
the next month or so, but probably not in CT  I just did an
update of my box counts for all the individual states we've visited,
so far, and found that my F count for CT alone is now over 2000!
That still represents well under half the total number of boxes
that I've found including 30 other states, but  WOWEE CT!!!
Even at the rate they're still coming out over in CT, I don't think
there could even be enough left there for me to find to get me to
PFX 5000 right now. I guess that means we'll have to be
planning a trip to ME, PA or somewhere else sometime soon.
Thanks again to all you planters everywhere! Glad to see this
hobby of ours growing by leaps and bounds!

Wanda from RI
