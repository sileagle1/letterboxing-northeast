From: Ellen Simpson | Date: 2005-09-18 18:56:07 UTC
It's kind of a pain, but you can take a bike onto the T and commuter trains.
Of course there's specific times but most conductors don't mind if you have
to bring a bike on. Once you have a bike, man, now you can go anywhere.

Salem, MA is on the same line to Newburyport (all Essex County). It's a 20
minute ride from Boston. I've placed a few in Salem which are actually
pretty easy to find and not too bad on bike (in fact I biked all of them
when I was finding a place for them) and will be placing some more, and the
Marblehead Bike Path has one which is close to one of mine.

Than there's Lynn, a few stops before Salem.

AND OH, than there's the Alewife Station one which is at the end of the Red
Line. From there you can take the Minuteman Bike Path to Arlington and
Lexington. There's several there. Of course I'm not 100% sure how close some
of them are to the bike path. The bike path takes you right into downtown
Arlington and Lexington.

Okay. I'll stop.

Ejyo

PS I can't even imagine having an addictive, time consuming, hobby like
Letterboxing while in school.
