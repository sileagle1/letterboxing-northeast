Re: DCR properties?

From: cpascott | Date: 2006-04-19 20:51:45 UTC
There are plenty of boxes in DCR properties, including State Parks
and State Forests.

To the best of my knowledge, there is no official restricition on
placing boxes on DCR property. There are lots of open spaces,
including DCR lands, that do not yet have any letterboxes. You
should have no issues planting at Elm Bank currently.

However, as the popularity of this hobby and others, such as
Geocaching, grows, it is increasingly likely that organizations such
as the DCR will issue regulations. In fact, the DCR has already
established an intent to require permits to place geocaches,
letterboxes and the like on it's watershed properties (such as the
Quabbin Reservoir lands). Theres is a group called the "DCR
Geocaching Policy Group" that was supposedly working on this policy
with implementation scheduled for 2006. I have no information,
however, as to whether that policy has, in fact, been developed or
implemented. This intiative, however, sits within the DCR's
Division of Watershed Supply Protection, and does not appear to
cross over to either the Division of State Parks and Recreation or
the Division of Urban Parks and Recreation.

Regardless, it appears that any such policy will only apply to
watershed properties and that geocaches and letterboxes will be
allowed with a permit. Elm Bank Reservation is not a watershed
property AFAIK (nor is it within the jurisdiction of the Division of
Watershed Supply Protection), so again, you should be fine placing a
box there.
