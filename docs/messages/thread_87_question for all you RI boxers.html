question for all you RI boxers

From: Janet | Date: 2003-12-28 23:57:52 UTC
I just finished two full days of letterboxing in Rhode Island with
Music Woman. I was surprised at how many boxes had little activity
over the past year. I went to one new box in North Kingstown that I
believe has been out there for a month and no one had been there
yet. I went and did Wanda's winter Holiday series (very, very nice
walk in Arcadia; cute stamps too). This series is seasonal and has
been out since early December and is only out until early January.
No one had been there yet either. This series has 8 letterboxes in a
fairly short distance. Can't understand why no one had been out
there yet.

You could say that these recent boxes haven't been visited because of
the bad weather and the busy Holidays. I can understand that. But we
also went to some older boxes that only 3 or 4 people had been to all
year. This surprised me. These were not mystery boxes or hard to
find boxes.

I was wondering if other people had noticed low visitations to their
boxes in Rhode Island. I also wonder if anyone had any ideas why.
Just curious.

Moonstone

From: kiwi-1 | Date: 2003-12-29 17:41:08 UTC
Janet,
I have also noticed many boxes here in Connecticut that have not had many visitors. I have come to the conclusion that there are now so many boxes in this area, that people just can't get to them all. I know I have an enormous pile of clues printed out to boxes I would love to visit. There is only so much time however, so people can only whittle away at the huge number of boxes slowly. Way back when, there weren't quite so many boxes to choose from, many people were just waiting for new boxes to find, so when the clues came up they flocked to the box right away. I'm not really sure what to do about that. I guess that's why I tried hiding my series for a limited time, that way by the time people forgot about them, they would be removed anyway. I think a little promotion from recent finders certainly helps. That way boxers know they should find the box in good condition, and recent finders can try to entice them with a few details of how wonderful the hike was or something.
Melissa
