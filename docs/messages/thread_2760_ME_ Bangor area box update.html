ME: Bangor area box update

From: Phyto | Date: 2006-03-19 00:42:00 UTC
Phyto and Isosceles went on a box maintenance mission today.
The following box(es)/series are alive or require attention:

Thomas Hill Standpipe - missing, last visited on 2/11/06, this was one
of my first Bangor plants, and I can only guess that it wasn't
re-hidden very well. This will be replanted - SOON!

Sklar Park - in great shape. Added some new plastic zip bags. Last
visitors in November.

Stella - great shape. Large box size for hitchhiker additions ;)

Dancing Bubs - also, in good shape, added some plastic bags.

Prentiss Woods - some ice crusties, but to be expected. Added some new
plastic bags, and will be downsizing the boxes to small lock'n'lock
containers in a week or so. The boxes are too large for the locations
and the logbooks are nearly full enough to justify a transition. Last
visitors to this location in march of this year!

Arbor Way - only 1 box is still active in this location - Black bear.
It is alive and well with the last visitor in November.

Horse Feathers - these boxes were bone dry and in great shape - not
very many visitors...

Easter Hunt '04 - a box I placed for a friend. In great shape in a
location that is a joy to find during the right season. Bring a picnic
in good weather and enjoy the butterfly garden nearby.

and this brings me to Rocky and Bullwinkle. This darn Moose is still
on the loose, and to date, not that many finders have accomplished
this fantastic series of Isosceles'. I highly suggest the search for
it is well worth the effort. These are still in tact though I might
wait for mudseason to subside.

The Standpipe will be replaced soon.
Letterboxing in Maine to go offline for good as well.
Look for updates and as always, email us for questions, comments and
if you'd like to be on our mailing list.

phyto-out
