Freiburg's Schlossberg

From: Dave and Diane | Date: 2007-12-15 20:35:44 UTC
So there we were, in the Black Forest, climbing this huge 'hill'
above the old town of Freiburg. Of course, we are going after a
letterbox knowing darned well that our compass is sitting in a
backpack in our dining room in Rhode Island. Dang.

We get up the hill, and we're higher than the highest cathedral
steeple in the city. Then we need to climb up this 100-foot steel
tower swaying in the high winds. Why? Because there's clues up
there!

After we get the clues, we trek along the ridgeline, through the
ruins of old fortifications. Truly neat. We count out the
appropriate paces and count milestones and approximate degrees and
such based on the Sun and that every traditional European cathedral
is laid out east-west. So guess what? We found the box! Hoorah!

Our first Old World box! We're so proud. So two weeks in Germany
and Austria, only one find, and we're so happy about it? Well I tell
you - Nybbler put this box out (a very nice one!), and it was one of
the shortest walks there was of all the clues for Germany. And this
was something of a shlep, but also soooo much of the other areas were
snow-covered.

Dave & Diane
