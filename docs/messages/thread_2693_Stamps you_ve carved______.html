From: Phyto | Date: 2006-02-11 17:45:52 UTC
--- In LbNE@yahoogroups.com, "Gretchen Caldwell" <boston.rott@...> wrote:
> I'm working on carving my first two stamps, for my first two plants.
When you carve stamps for your plants, do you stamp them in your
logbook, or somewhere else?


This is a great question that might also generate some conversation on
the newboxers list (hint, hint Mark!) but, I'm glad you brought it up
nontheless. When I first started carving, I didn't think ahead about
the images and where to put them not to mention what would happen if
the box/stamp went missing. I have learned my lesson ;)

Since I carve all the stamps in the boxes I plant, I usually stamp the
image multiple times. I am not as meticulous as some folks (kudos to
TeamGreenDragon for their archiving) nor as willing to put the images
out for public property, but I do from time to time scan in the
logbook so that I have a record of the stamp image in case it 1) goes
missing - and I'd like to recarve a similar design or 2) I want to
distribute an electronic copy for the purpose of a finder stamp in
said instance when stamp is missing.

On our clue pages, the boxes and stamps that are missing and/or no
longer active have posted images of the stamps. I'm reconsidering this
approach as our webpage is very inactive and nobody pays much
attention to boxes in Maine anymore, but anyhoo - I'd recommend that
you print yourself a clean copy of the image as often as you can
remember. Now that I've carved over 100 stamps, it has become
difficult to keep track of them and I often wish that I had begun
saving the images from the start.

I'm sure you'll find it enjoyable to look back on your images in the
same way that going through an old logbook brings back memories. For
some examples of how letterboxers have used their electronic images
you can see Springchick's webpage and the DrewClan's for some teasers
of all the fantastic carvings that they're done over the years!

Enjoy planting,
Phyto (Maine)
