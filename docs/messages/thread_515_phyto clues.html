> I'm not out to structure anyone else's good time, but that's
how I find clues easiest.


I think of the LBNA site like a shopping mall.

From a finder's perspective, it is like one stop shopping for a bunch
of clues in a particular area. However, the product available at the
mall may tend to lack the diversity you might find, say, in a
downtown shop where the items are hand crafted. Also, since the mall
attracts the majority of the finders, finder's collections tend to
look very much the same. I would not argue that shopping at the mall
is not easier - but some things are worth a bit more effort. In fact
it seems that it is the increased effort that can make the prize more
valuable and sought after. (In Maine, instances of this phenomenon
are abundant)

For placers, the mall lets you reach a larger group of customers, and
many will partake of your products. Placers who find the mall
distasteful, or perhaps want to offer their products from a more
traditional location may miss out on many sales. Some placers do not
want the high traffic that results from mall visibility, and consider
the loss of a few sales an equitable trade off for the homogeneous
world of the mall.

As far as a majority of placers choosing to leave the mall and open
up shop downtown, or across the tracks, or even out in the country, I
doubt that that will happen anytime soon. But I think it would be a
mistake to move all of the shops into the mall.

Hats off to Phyto for opting to do it a different way!

-Isosceles
