Re: [LbNE] April Spring Drive

From: MayEve | Date: 2005-05-01 14:07:11 UTC
I am smiling still thinking of yesterday, and wanted to thank everone who came out in the less-than-perfect weather to make the day perfect in every way! It shouldn't have surprised me to see the boxing caravan of 14 cars maneuver in and out of a tiny cemetery with fine skill, OR the lack of hesitation in creating a new "bridge" over Roaring Brook when we found the old one MIA, OR the enthusiasm and spirit that never wavered throughout the soggy day! Boxers ARE amazing!

A really big THANK YOU to RTRW for warmly wecoming us into her home mid-day to snack, refresh, regroup, exchange and mingle; and to Mark and Sue Pepe who "covered" the Drive so nicely on their website and whose encouragement made the day; and to Music Woman for her wise input and fine organizational skills; and to Donutz and Corinna and those who tirelessly inked up at the stops; and to Pine Tree for letting us use her Walkie Talkies (even though I didn't know how to use them!); and to Flutterby for helping me re-hide boxes!! AND to my favorite brother Wild Rover for being the heart and soul of the Drive and Krusty Krab for being the inspiration.

Letterboxing in itself is a wonderful thing, but it's the people you meet along the way that leave you with much more than a stamp in your logbook! Thank You ALL







MayEve

Re: April Spring Drive

From: wildrover351 | Date: 2005-05-02 04:31:22 UTC
I, too, wanted to thank everyone as well. Mark, without your
committment to getting the info out to everybody the event could not
possibly have been such a success. RTRW, I can't thank you (and
Scott) enough for your wonderful hospitality, and for the awe-
inspiring Event Stamp and the remarkable Horace Wells & Isreal
Putnam stamps. Music Woman & Donutz, as I knew would be the case
your help with organizing everything and keeping the stamping
efficient was invaluable. MayEve, it was so much fun doing this
whole event with you that I just can't wait to do it again, and the
lessons & skills you taught me will make me a better boxer.

I also want to thank EVERYBODY who attended and made the event so
special. Krusty Krab thought she was going boxing with Daddy &
Auntie to do the House Of Mouse Series, and lo and behold 30+ people
turned out to box with her for her birthday !!! From the outset I
knew it would be a success, as Music Woman led us into the Roaring
Brook Nature Preserve, and Paul from the Maplines proceeded to build
a walkway across the brook where the bridge had washed out. Friends
helping friends, teamwork, having fun, enjoying each other's
company... I just want to thank you all. The event was very, very
special to me, and you all made it so. ---Wild Rover
