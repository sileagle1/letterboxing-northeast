ME: Portland boxes update

From: Phyto | Date: 2004-08-08 02:19:08 UTC
Phyto & Isosceles did some boxing.

Munjoy Hill Observatory has been revived and is back in place with a nice new log
consisting of the re-constructed old logbook pages (that were quite damp) and
additional pages to hold new visitors. I have also added postcards to the Observatory,
Baxter Woods Series, and Box #15 of the East End Series.

Unfortunately due to the celebrations of the Center for Cultural Exchange at the
corner of State and Congress, we only checked on 1/2 of the Portland Series. I can
confirm that the following are gone: Boxes #4, 7, and 8. The others are alive and well.
The "science" box - (number escapes me) is also mia - but I just can't remember the
number. Didn't have the clue set with me, so well, it is quite a challenging find.

Bug Light Glyph sadly is gone.

Fort Allen Park is alive - though it is not covered so well - my bad.

Key to the City is doing very well.

Still haven't gotten out to the Riverton Trolley Park...and finally, CBP's "Casco Bay" box
really eluded us - we're just not sure on which bench to begin our pacing out on the
east end.

Finally, a total of 3 hitchhikers were deposited in requisite boxes....Windairen's
"Compass", and Isoscele's "Fermi" and "Rutherford" Physics in a Can hitchhikers.

Have fun out there!
Phyto

Re: [LbNE] Re: ME: Portland boxes update

From: uneksia | Date: 2004-08-10 16:57:28 UTC
well, what a trip to portland yesterday. not being a city girl it was quite
an interesting experience. having parked the car we started our adventure.
after walking forever and a day it dawned on us that we were at the location
for the clues to the last box. after a bit of sitting and contemplating this
situation we decided the course of action would be to do the series
backwards.

#15 found
#14 found (and yes brian it was a long reach) unfortunately there was not
stamp in it. there was a little note saying had to remove to stamp in a safe
location and would return it on august 10th. bummer.
#13 found
#12 already reported missing so we didn't look
#11 found
#10 could not find
#9 we could see the circle but there was a movie going on and so had to
just keep on walking. what the heck did they have to pick that time to watch
a movie for!
#8 already reported missing so we didn't look
#7 already reported missing so we didn't look
#6 found by this time it was getting dark so was easy to get and put back.
we were ready for the break.
#5 could not find. think i found the pointed rock but there was no stamp
near it. by this time it was really dark and i was not into groping around
with a policeman sitting across the street watching us.
#4 already reported missing so we didn't look
#3 found
#2 found the monument. found the rock in the roots but could not find the
stamp. could still be there as it was pitch dark by this time but i really
don't think it was.
#1 that hill is dark at night so we could not even see the stump. nice thing
is tho we discovered a short cut back to our car.

the short cut now is a whole different story. we discovered at box #1 that
our car was parked on the other side of the bay. we have no idea how to get
back there without backtracking. figuring a kid living on that side is not
going to walk the blocks it takes to get to a normal path to reach the beach
area there had to be a short cut. there was. we found it.

all in all a lot of fun. we will be back. more prepared next time, with a
city map and not leaving our wallets in the car. (boy were we starving). i
can hike 10 miles in the mountains find a box and hike back. these urban
boxes are hard! thanx for the challenge and adventure.

smile
uneksia
