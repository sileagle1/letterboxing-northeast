Well, now that Willard Clock has finally been planted (clues are
posted at
http://home.comcast.net/~scott.e.hall/clues/Willard_Clock.html), it's
time to plant another!

I've long wanted to plant a box on the Boston Harbor Islands (there is
one box there currently, but you need a boat to get to it), but have
waiting pending some research into whether the NPS manages the islands.

They do not. They are part of the 13-member partnership made up of
various local, state, and federal agencies that individiually own all
of the Boston Harbor Islands and collectively manage them. The NPS
does not own any of them.

Thus, although it is not perfectly clear whether the NPS' restriction
on letterboxes would apply to a park they do not own and only
co-manage under a unique set of by-laws, it is my assertion that it
does not.

So, on to the details.

Scarab (of the Doubtful Guests) has graciously offered to carve the
stamp for this box. Titled "The Lady in Black" the letterbox honors
the legend of the ghost that wanders the island and it's principal
building, Fort Warren. If you're curious, read more at
http://home.comcast.net/~scott.e.hall/clues/Marys_Lamb.html. Thanks
Scarab!

This box will be placed on Georges Island when I travel there as part
of a business outing August 26. Stay tuned!
