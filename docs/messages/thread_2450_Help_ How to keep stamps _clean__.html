From: YahYah | Date: 2005-10-06 22:27:02 UTC
Would anyone have any tips on how to keep stamps clean? I'd like to
use a variety of colors when I stamp at different locations, but it
seems the darker colors really hang on to my stamp, making switching to
lighter stamping colors kind of impossible, as the darker color bleeds
into the lighter stamp pad color.

Any tricks? Can I soak the stamp in soap and water? or will that ruin
the integrity of the stamp?

Right now I have a store bought rubber stamp, it is not something I
created myself.

Any tips or tricks on how to keep the stamp clean would be appreciated.

Thank you
YahYah

Re: Help? How to keep stamps "clean"?

From: azobox | Date: 2005-10-08 15:10:08 UTC
Among the many suggestions frequently posted on this topic are a
couple that stand out.
First, it's generally agreed that any of the various 'baby wipes' are
discouraged because the scent may be attractive to critters.
Second, since hand-carved stamps may be more fragile than
store-boughts, any scrubbing should be avoided lest the stamp be
damaged during the cleaning.
I've seen lots of boxers just use damp paper towel -- just 'stamp'
repeatedly until the ink residue is gone.

Good luck,

azobox
