From: Phyto | Date: 2004-04-04 02:24:02 UTC
Phyto and isosceles had an early spring venture into letterboxing land. We first
enjoyed a jaunt through the Wyeth world and admired Zorach's artwork. Thank you
Casco Bay Painter for a wonderful adventure.

Then we attempted Martian Maggot's whaleback shell midden in Damariscotta,
however the kind DRA volunteer (Damariscotta River Associaton) was maintaining a
large portion of the landscape and we decided it would be better to leave well
enough alone on this matter. We'll come back for this box when it's warmer. They
plan on pruning the apple trees in the historic orchard and harvesting this fall. This
was very interesting as it hasn't been done in 30 or so years!

After this short walk through the meadow, we felt like singing and enjoyed
AmyMisha's mystery. First finders on that one - and a lovely one at that! After this
attempt we couldn't leave without a wildflower walk and too bad we had to leave
our black cat moxie at home for "Chat Noir"...

Finally a crazy heart drove us to madness as we stamped in at our last spot along
the way home. I've got to say that 4 new letterboxes in one day surely puts my
maine letterboxing over the top! Thanks to all the new planters.
-phyto
