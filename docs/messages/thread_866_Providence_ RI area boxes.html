Re: [LbNE] Providence, RI area boxes

From: dave & diane | Date: 2004-06-26 03:58:23 UTC
The boxes on Jamestown (Newport county) called Canonicut Cannon and Island Sanctuary are easy and pleasant. They can be mosquitoful at dusk, but the Cannons takes her by WWII observation areas of the bay (from the island, it's a nice vantage) and the Sanctuary observation platforms puts one up close to the egrets. On the road past Canonicut Cannons is Beavertail Lighthouse. It's a great place to soak in a real New Englandy rockshore, but I'd disrecommend the Beavertail Light or Loss of a Friend letterboxes for your Mum - an incredible view awaits *after* a strenuous walk of some distance, and the poison ivy is in full force.
Wickford, Washington County, is very pleasant. The Faces of Wickford gives a lovely stroll in a sheltered harbor town (though the last of the four is missing). It's not too touristy, even though it's the Witches of Eastwick village.
