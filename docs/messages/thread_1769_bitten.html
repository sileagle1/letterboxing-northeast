So, I read about letterboxing in the AAA magazine earlier this month, and
thought it sounded like fun. I am a reporter for a small weekly paper - I
checked the towns we cover, and there are boxes here! I talked to my editor,
who agreed it was a go. Last Saturday, we (my 3 daughters and me, plus my
editor) printed up some clues and headed out. Today we ran a feature article
on letterboxing, complete with a bit of my account of our trek to find a box
plus an interview with the planters of a couple of the boxes and photos of
my daughters, icy fingered and rosy cheeked, after getting a stamp in our
book. We are up to five stamps now, including one hitch hiker, and are ready
to plant a couple in our home town. We were hoping to go on Saturday, but it
looks like snow again, and after freezing last weekend, I think we'll stick
a little closer to the woodstove until its a bit warmer out.
Stamps we found in good shape - Oliver Mill, Trout under Eagle Bridge (both
of them,) Partridge in a Pine Tree, and the North compass hitch hiker.

What fun!
