From: Kimberly Calcagno | Date: 2006-12-13 03:24:30 UTC
Hi!

I just wanted to give you the heads up.Audubon Rhode Island who owns and
manages Caratunk (even though it is in MA), doesn't permit letterboxing or
geocaching. I am one of the refuge managers for them (I manage the
Smithfield and North Smithfield refuges), and I have been trying to persuade
them (being a letterboxer myself and one of several 'boxers on our staff) to
allow letterboxing, but I haven't quite changed their minds yet. They see
letterboxes as a bit of trespassing and a bit of litter. They haven't quite
gotten the point that it is a great opportunity to get people to visit our
refuges and a great family activity. I have managed to get them to agree to
the idea of the refuge managers or our education department placing Quests
or Audubon-sponsored boxes. We have not yet sold them on the idea of
allowing others to place boxes.



Mike, the refuge manager at Caratunk, is a great guy, but he will adhere to
the policy and remove the box and contact the placer if he finds out about
it.



So..my recommendation and request to everyone out there seeking this
box..please don't attract attention to yourself and if you see that a trail
is being formed to the box, please let JAMO know so they can move or remove
the box. With your help, we might just convince them yet.



Thanks!! Keep on boxin'!

-Kim (Rustypuff)
