I too have found Geocachers to be extremely helpful!!

The New England Geocachers seem to be a great bunch. Two of them allowed me to place logbooks in their caches, for letterboxers.

Turned out I had some Geocaching waypoints near at least 3 of my boxes (these waypoints were white plastic rings with coordinates). I adopted the cache, so no problem coordinating maintianance for me either.

I don't like to drive in the winter, so I have been doing nearby Geocaches since 12/27/05. No GPSr is required; at the cache listing, click on "topozone" and write your own letterbox-style clues. Done that way, they are harder than most letterboxes.

enjoy the hunt!
