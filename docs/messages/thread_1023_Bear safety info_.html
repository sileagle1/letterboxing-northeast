[LbNE] Bear safety info.

From: Erin Boucher | Date: 2004-07-23 15:52:19 UTC
There are bears in CT but the chances are that you will never come across
one. If, by some outside chance, a bear is anywhere near you, it will most
likely smell or see you first and run away. Also, boxing alone and/or at
night is risky for a number of reasons; unpredicted weather changes, injury,
bears, psychotic escaped convicts, etc. If you do venture out alone, bring a
cell phone. Anyway, back to the bears... For the record, bear attacks in
North America are very rare. On average, they only occur about 15 times per
year and only 3 of them will result in death. The vast majority of these
attacks are in Canada and the western U.S. so you have little to worry
about. But, if you are still concerned, here are a few suggestions:
Avoid strong-smelling items; especially perfume. Bug spray is ok.
Keep any food in air-tight containers
Carry pepper spray (this is also useful for those pesky escaped convicts)
If a bear approaches you, spray the pepper spray at its eyes, nose, and
mouth. Once the bear is blinded by the spray, leave the area as quickly as
possible by backing up slowly NOT running. Running will trigger the bear's
chase instinct.
Also, DO NOT apply the pepper spray to your gear as the strong smell may
actually attract bears.
If you find yourself under attack with no opportunity to use pepper spray,
try to appear bigger than you really are by waving your arms, backpack, etc.
Fight back, make lots of noise, and try to appear dominant and the bear will
back down. DO NOT play dead.

You really have very little to be concerned about in CT as far as bears go
but it's always good to be prepared!

Happy and safe boxing!
Mystique

From: Tom LeClerc W1TJL | Date: 2004-07-23 16:57:16 UTC
All the below is correct but EVERYONE plese be aware that cell phones are iffy
at best. Many areas have no signal or quite poor signal. Don't depend on them
and allow yourself to take chances just because you think you have easy
communications or rescue at hand. I teach hunter safety courses and this is a
point we constantly have to make - people think they work everywhere. They
don't.

Don't worry about bears. They want to encounter you a LOT less than you want
an encounter with them.

Tom of Tom & Suz
