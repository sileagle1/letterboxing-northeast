Hi all,

I've noticed a disturbing new trend in list habits...crossposting
the same posts to the big list and this one. What do all of you
think about this? It seems to me that most folks here read both
lists anyway, hence there is no need to post twice.

Right now it's a minor percentage of the posts but I'm worried about
developing bad habits that, as the list gets larger and more
established, will make wading through all the posts a chore.

Thoughts? Moderators, what do YOU think?

Solor

Re: crossposting

From: rscarpen | Date: 2004-06-17 22:58:38 UTC
> I've noticed a disturbing new trend in list habits...crossposting
> the same posts to the big list and this one.

Not really a disturbing 'new' trend really--it's always been around,
though fortunately, it's not as bad as when the board was first
created. Back in those days, one could make a good case that since
not everyone knew about this board, you had to post both here and the
main talk list.

> Moderators, what do YOU think?

My thoughts are expressed on the Posting Guidelines page I created at
http://www.geocities.com/rscarpen/Lb/Docs/Posting/

What to do about infractions is a little harder. I typically let it
slide at first in the hopes that problem will just 'go away'. You
laugh, but it really works sometimes! =) Many of the cross-posters
are new to this board and don't realize that pretty much EVERYONE in
the northeast are members of this board, so they post to the main
board in the hopes of catching those that aren't. After a while on
the board, they realize posting a dulplicate message to the main
board is overkill and stop on their own.

And the occasional post such as your own also help nip that cross-
posting habit in the bud. You've already embarrassed Phyto into
submission. ;o) Probably several others too! (Don't worry, Phyto,
we won't hold it against it you!) But the occasional gentle reminder
never hurts.

If I notice a person becoming a cross-posting menece with no signs of
abating, then I'll e-mail them personally that it's not really
appropriate and that usually solves the problem. This board I don't
watch very closely, however, and there's probably a lot of cross
posting I don't notice.

I've never really discussed the issue with Brian, but I know he
frowns on cross-posting as well. Whether he has a 'method' for
handling it--I don't know. We both try to take a 'hands-off'
approach to moderation. I wish I could say it was for some noble
purpose, but for me it basically amounts to being too lazy to take
a 'hands-on' approach to moderation. =)

Hope this answers some of your questions!

-- Ryan
