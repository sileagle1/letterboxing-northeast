From: Warrior Woman | Date: 2004-07-12 23:19:47 UTC
Since I respect both of you, and both of your opinions, let me cast
my fly in the ointment.

I have adopted four boxes, some with the original placer's blessing,
some not. Why? Because the clues are hyperlinks to another site
that is no longer active. Two fine examples are Point Judith and
Just Bass in Narragansett RI. MadMary did give me her blessing to
adopt the boxes, but the webmasters at LbNA preferred that I keep the
hyperlink clues intact instead of re-entering the clues as text. The
only connection to me was via "Contact the Placer" which would
redirect the email to me.

So I kept the hyperlinks active as instructed.

Now the hyperlinks don't work, I get emails from "seekers"
asking "what happened to the clues?" and I am now working with Wes to
figure out how to "fix" it so that I can edit the clues and remove
the hyperlink and re-enter the clues as text.

The same thing happened with Bonsai and Piece of Cake.

Fortunately, I have the text on my hard drive of all my placed and
adopted boxes, just in case this sort of thing should happen.

Caretaking is one thing (and I'm meticulous about that). Just plain
old access to the clues in the first place is another. If I come
across a letterbox that I can easily maintain that fits both
categories, I am quick to contact the placer (if I can) to get
permission to adopt the box, and if I can't get ahold of the placer,
and the clues go "lost in cyberspace", I usually have a hard copy of
the clues handy and will adopt it.

Of course, I am quick to "give it back" if the original placer does
eventually show up (ie Grey Reservation in Sudbury, previously
adopted and since given back to its birth parent).

And sometimes I have a fondness for particular letterboxes/placers
and will "babysit" a box or two (several of the Magic Tree House
Series, Four Faces of Wickford, etc.).

Just trying to be helpful!

From: birder579 | Date: 2004-07-13 02:19:14 UTC
I know that this is a touchy subject, but...

It has happened, it is happening, and it will happen that people
will plant boxes and post clues, then loose the interest or ability
to continue to maintain them. Then the web site will gather invalid
clues that direct us to empty locations. This is frustrating for
those of us who are hoping for a reasonable chance to succeed in
following the clues and find a plastic treasure box.

A measure of how much we value something is the degree to which we
keep it clean. Just ask the dust bunnies under my bed. I feel
there needs to be some proceedure that will deal with unmaintained
clues.

Having them adopted by an active boxer would be best if the box is
found in the location. If a person who found the box before, or a
certain number of experienced boxers, attempt to find it and can't,
and the owner of the clues is unresponsive to reports of it being
missing, the clues must suffer the ultimate fate.

I don't like the imposistion of (ugh) rules, but that is the way to
value the remaining clues that are valid and kept up to date by
active boxers.
The Bird Stamper

Re: Boxes that have since been orphaned

From: cpascott | Date: 2004-07-13 14:04:49 UTC
We touched on this topic on the main board a few times. Basically,
it all comes down to personal responsibility. If you plant a box but
then cannot care for it, attempt to find a caretaker or adopter.
Depending on what your involvement with the box will be, update your
clues to keep them current. If you aren't willing to do this, then
ask the adopter to do so -- and provide them the details. If someone
reports your box as missing, confirm or deny it. Double check in the
field to see if it is, in fact, missing. If you can't get out to
check, have a caretaker check or, at a minimum, list the box
as "potetnially missing" in your clues. If the box is gone, pull the
clues or indicate as such. If you decide to leave the hobby, pull
your boxes and clues, or find adopters for them.

It's all up to YOU the placer. We can institute rules, but they
can't be enforced. The best we can do is to provide guidelines and
encourage placers to follow them. Let's hope that's enough.

CPAScott
