A Boxing day's ramblings

From: Northstar | Date: 2004-08-25 19:11:34 UTC
Off it was to Rocky Neck State Park yesterday to get to some much
needed boxing! After dropping off the non-letterboxing chaff, I set
out to find the elusive Red Dragon. I was successful this time
around. I then went in search of the "Three Seashells by the
Seashore" boxes. All were found with no problem, however- box #3 was
missing it's stamp and inkpad. I then headed out of the park to
visit "Book Barn" and "Relax and Read". What an awesome bookstore!!
I spent some time walking the grounds and inspecting the various
sections. Heading back to Rocky Neck, I sought out "Two Cats"and
found them with no trouble. Back inside the park I scooped up boxes
1,2&3 of "Marshland~Woodland~Water" before taking a swim. After
leaving the park for the day, I decided I was not finished. I picked
up "The Travelling Man"+ the "Running Roads" h.h. and also "A Fishy
Tale. While driving home, I found I was still not satisfied, and
thusly, set about to find "The Wedding Rock" and "The Secret
Garden", both in excellent condition. "The Secret Garden" made my
500th find to date as I find myself zeroing in on my first year
anniversary of this great hobby. If not for letterboxing, there are
things I might never have seen and places I might never have been.
Most of all, there are the people who make letterboxing what it is.
Without these personalities, the finds would be that much more
empty. Thank you to everyone who joined me in the first part of this
journey, I hope to see you all again in the next part. ---
Northstar
