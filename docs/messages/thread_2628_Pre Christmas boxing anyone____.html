Winter Gear: was Pre Christmas..

From: defygravity2001 | Date: 2005-12-23 14:41:04 UTC
Without Letterboxing in the winter....I'd have some serious cabin
fever. Somethings I find help: Yaktraks and shoeshoes.

It's hard to find serious women's outdoor clothing. I found some -30
boots from LLBean (generally the women's equivalent of mens boots are
rated about 50 degrees warmer). The rest of my gear is mostly from
ski shops. You can rent snowshoes from EMS for a weekend or so to
see if you like them, then if you decide to buy, they'll credit the
price of the rental towards your purchase (or they did when I bought
mine).

Yaktraks are awesome little coils that stretch over your soles, not
very expensive, but are like tirechains for your feet. They work
great at keeping you off your behind on slick icy trails. Great
holiday present or stocking stuffer.

The problem I haven't solved yet: I have a camelpak-style hydration
pack. The water in the tube will sometimes freeze. I have to pretty
much sip constantly to prevent it (and answering nature's call in -10
windchills is just OUT of the question). I'm going to look for some
thin insulation at Home Depot....but any ideas would be appreciated.

Merry Merry!
~ Aili
