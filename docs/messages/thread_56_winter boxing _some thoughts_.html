winter boxing (some thoughts)

From: Phyto | Date: 2003-12-14 20:47:46 UTC
Alright folks - be honest here - how many of you went boxing this
weekend? I know that it's a fair number of you in CT at least, but for
folks up this way in Maine I made a few observations when I was out today.

1) ROCKS
Yes, rocks - not so much the snow tracks to the box- forget that,
you're already openly going after that box to begin with - at that
point, who cares about the tracks?

I'm talking about rocks that cover the spot - they're frozen to the
ground. They are truly hard to dislodge from their frozen cubby to
extract a box from. Has anyone else experienced this terrible tragedy?

and

2) STUMPS
Need a bit of R&D on this one, but how about a sawed off stump that
has an additonal 1-2 inch thick layer sawed off and then hinged? yes,
the hinge might be more obvious but it would make for a great natural
cover and hiding spot inside. You could potentially use black coated
hinges so that they would be less obvious. One would need the
necessary tools to procure a sliced off section of stump and
additionally you might have to hollow out the stump in the middle. ok-
perhaps this is too much work...

Any thoughts on these items?
Happy winter boxing,
phyto

I went out yesterday letterboxing briefly (I live in RI.) The rocks
were only slightly frozen with no snow on them. I was able to pry

over my log book (the finger bled a lot ... yuk!) but managed to
keep the blood off Laurette's beautiful new log book (I hope.) ....
moral of the story ... don't use your fingers to loosen frozen
rocks ... or if you do, keep your fingers out from between the rocks.
