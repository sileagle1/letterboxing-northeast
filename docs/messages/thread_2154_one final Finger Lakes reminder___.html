2005 Finger Lakes Gathering - Ithaca, NY
Saturday June 11th, 11 am - 3 p.m.

You want waterfalls? You want lakes? You want gorges that go on for miles?
You've got 'em in the Finger Lakes, plus plenty of breathtaking views and
all of the fun of the second sorta-annual Finger Lakes gathering.

Bring your tastiest contribution to the potluck plus your clues for the
many boxes in and around Ithaca, and join us at 11 a.m. at this historic
location, formerly the "Hollywood of the East," at the southern tip of
Cayuga Lake. You may want to brush up on your cinematic history if you hope
to find the right park, the same one where the Wharton Brothers made their
headquarters early last century.

Turn left when you enter the park, and be sure to observe the 10 mph speed
limit. We'll be at the smaller of the two pavilions - just look for either
of the two sets of revolving horses (one large and musical, the other small
and human-powered). There's even a playground very close at hand -
including fun of the most unexpected sorts - for attendees both young and
young at heart. So sorry, but four-legged friends are NOT allowed at this
gathering place.

This lovely park has only one box listed in residence, but rumor has it
that a certain missing arboreal giant will be replaced in time for the
gathering. Expect that other newcomers may show as well. Plenty of other
boxes may be found in nearby locations, including Farm City Day, Farmer's
Market, Downstream Deliverance (soon to be retired - get it now!), Monster
Tamer, Taughannock Falls, Taughannock Falls Overlook, all of the boxes at
Six Mile Creek, Guardian Angel, Sackett's Bridge, Red, White and True,
Snoop Doggy, June Monsoon and a host of others (including one very special
Tompkins County mystery box that you must not miss, and another NY mystery
box that may be closer than you think).

Come mingle with letterboxers from near and far as we enjoy each other's
company from 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. Don't forget to bring something to share for
the potluck, and extra clues for any boxes you'll be planting. (We expect
at least 65 attendees, so please bring plenty of copies.) You'll probably
want lots of bug spray, too, and maybe an umbrella if rain is forecast.

We ask that HHs not be exchanged or left out on tables for others to stamp
into at the gathering. The spectacular I Love NY HH Hostel is only a 25
minute drive away, and HHs are always welcome there. You're also welcome,
of course, to leave HHs in local boxes or to exchange them with other
letterboxers on the trail.

For lodging info, visit http://www.visitithaca.com/ Be aware that lodging
will be scarce due to Cornell's Reunion weekend.

Looking forward to seeing many of you on Saturday!

TurtleMcQ and MamaBear
