Self congrats and a self-admonition

From: Diana Newton Wood, MD | Date: 2006-07-04 00:05:19 UTC
Hi,

I just wanted to talk about my boxing adventure today, in Leominster.

I was really close to 100 boxes, and I had told myself I would get them
this vacation, and then it was July 3rd, and I picked a series that
included 14.
Now, I should have known better than to take my 9 and 6 year old (almost
7...) on a 14 box hike. I had no map, and so I was really reliant on the
clues.
We got about 1/2 of the way before the first clue before my youngest,
started objecting, but I made him press on, as I didn't know how to pick
of the clues on the way back from where I was..
plus I really wanted to get those boxes. I promised ice cream, museum
(it was closed), and just pushed him along. The older one was tired, but
quiet.
We finally got to what I thought was the halfway point, (It turned out
to be much farther along than I thought). We had been at it for 4 hours!!
I had brought only one bottle of water, that I refilled in a brook, and
no food. I was lucky I had started out early.

Finally, I realized we were much farther along, and the kids kept
walking, while I darted to the boxes, stamped in quickly and caught up
with them, but on the second to last box,
my youngest came screaming back to me at the top of his lungs! He had
seen a *head* and he was terrified. I walked with him, and found a
doll's head on the side of the trail. Someone had
polked two holes in the forehead, and there was matted hair on it. I
could easily see how a young child could mistake this for a real head.
We looked at it together and put it in a bag at the bottom of my pack.
I thought someone had put it there to scare someone, but my husband
thinks it just looks abandonned.

Anyway, we made it out without further incident, but I am chastising
myself, for bad planning, and for wanting the boxes more than I cared to
make it a safe and appropriate trip for my kids.
We did stop at Friendly's and then two drive bys before we got back on
the road, so the last memories will be good ones.

Oh, and I got my hundred :)
But I learned a lesson!

Diana
DEF Dale End Farm
