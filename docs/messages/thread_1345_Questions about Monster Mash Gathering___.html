Re: Questions about Monster Mash Gathering...

From: Warrior Woman | Date: 2004-09-29 12:25:51 UTC
Dear Barbara, Mom of the Brush Bunch,

First of all - RELAX !!!

At gatherings such as these, there are children, dogs, big kids,
little kids, kids who sit on rocks ... (okay, I'm getting carried
away)

Gatherings are FUN !! Children LOVE them !! I've brought my own
three children to gatherings and they have a ball !! Maybe they want
to do stamp exchanges, maybe they don't (often, they are willing to
stamp *anyone's* logbook, which helps folks up their X count, but
don't necessarily whip out their own log book), sometimes they want
to look for boxes, sometimes they don't.

Everyone comes with their own agenda. Some folks are deadly serious
about exchanging with every other boxer at the event, deadly serious
about capturing every box, deadly serious about keeping a list of
every single boxer's personal traveler clues - or not !!

Some folks (like me) have been to so many letterbox gatherings that I
view this gathering as a sort of Halloween King Richard's Fair - I'm
there to have fun, to socialize, to hunt for boxes on Friday,
Saturday morning, and Sunday, to gladly offer an exchange should
anyone want to, and to enjoy the gathering's schedule of events.
With this many people attending, I'd go *nuts* trying to keep track
of all of the little nuances and play that occur at smaller
gatherings. To me, this is Mardi Gras, for adults and children alike!

As for your children - it's all about running around like mad, having
fun with a bunch of truly fun and happy adults, making friends with
other children, visiting with every dog there, and eating the entire
dessert table before we get to it (and my thighs thank them).

Can it be overwhelming? Sure. But a lot of us are parents
ourselves, and when those "times" occur (and they do), a break away
from the event, go off and box one or two boxes UNRUSHED, will often
help to ease the day. Or just go for a walk, or stop at a library -
whatever it takes. We've all been there, and we are all eager to
help.

Are there any rules that say "You must arrive at the start time of
the gathering and must remain until the finale under penalty
of ???"? Absolutely not. I've hosted letterboxing events where
folks show up an hour early, folks have stayed the whole time, folks
have stayed for a half hour, folks have arrived just when I am
cleaning up.

So - RELAX.

So - ENJOY.

So - HAVE FUN.

This public service announcement has been brought to you by no other
than the warm, wacky, and wheeeeeeee !! karma of Warrior Woman
