From: Phyto | Date: 2005-06-04 23:02:00 UTC
Took in the fruit of the vine while being at peace with nature today.
The Shalom winery was an interesting spot. This is a MOFGA (Maine
Organic Farmers and Gardeners Association) certified organic vineyard.
They sold us organic eggs and we even had a chance to meet the hens
and roosters up close and personal. Phyto especially enjoyed the
compost pile. Do check out this box as a nice detour from the crowded
bustle of the miracle mile.

Then we took another less traveled path and quietly enjoyed
Sangfroid's "Old Cemetery". Fantastic carve. Thank you for such a
wonderful box. A stop at the local Co-op for some tea and then onward
we pressed.

The skies threatened so alas, the trip to further isles had to be
postponed. Bangor boxes called so homestar we went yet what is this?
To our alarm, it has totally been harmed! The box was nowhere in
sight, just the empty remains of a once happy nest. Farewell dear
homestar :(

No finders at St. Franics. This is a hard one!

******pleast note*******
The following Phyto boxes are not currently active:

The Chocolate Church in Bath - pulled for repairs
Skyline - drenched by the past two months of soaking rains.
Spring Point - equally drenched. The stamp is fine. Needs a new log.

To all those who have contacted me recently regarding requests for
various sundry clues, email is the best bet to reach me - and the best
mode of receiving clues will be your postal box. I'll need your
mailing address. I'm happy to mail clues, but I do not like them
passed electronically unless under special circumstances.
