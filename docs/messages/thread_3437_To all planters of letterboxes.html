I know that some of you just throw your boxes out there and well.. they belong to the world now Good Luck!
But, box maintenance will win you many friends and kudos.
In a related note....
Dave was out at _Acting the Goa_t this weekend for the view and brought home a very soggy logbook. It appears no one has been to visit in almost a year and the logbook was pretty full. We have dried it out and will be returning it to the owner. Dave didn't have any supplies with him (afterall he was only there for the view!) so he left a few slips of paper in the now dried out box. So, if you're planning on visiting this box before the planter can get there - go prepared with paper to leave your stamp impression.

vonderinsel wrote:

> But, box maintenance will win you many friends and kudos.

I went out and did my box maintenance in MA this past week, and over
this winter, a total of seven of my boxes in MA went missing or were so
bad I retired them. I updated the clues on both LBNA and AQ.

I only had to retire one in RI (Point Judith), but the others have
fared pretty well.

You never quite know what you'll find when you do box maintenance ... I
try to do mine at least once a year and maybe even every six months if
it's a high traffic area.

> In a related note....
> Dave was out at _Acting the Goat_ this weekend for the view and
brought home a very soggy logbook.

Oh, dear! That box was on my list of "to do" boxes for next weekend,
along with Alice in Wonderland. Trying to do all the Mt. Tom boxes at
once.

I've been out on the trails quite a bit lately, and it does seem like
box maintenance isn't a big priority. I try to carry extra baggies,
paper towels, etc., when I go out, but there's only so much you can do,
especially when they are not your boxes.

I'm on my spring planting binge again - five down and six more to go!

No! letterboxes being pulled

From: christi tilton | Date: 2007-04-15 00:33:46 UTC
Hello,
I wanted to let people know that I plan on pulling the No! series in Wethersfield toward the end of this week. I've had to recarve a number of the stamps since I planted them and had to replace the containers and rehide some. I've finally come to the conclusion that the area is to busy (bikers, hikers, etc) and the town (or some group) is constantly clearing the woods. I've replanted a couple of boxes due to a tree or part of a tree being cut down.

It is my hope that later in the spring, once I've recarved the missing stamps, that I'll find a new place to plant them.

Thanks to all the people who have emailed me and kept me updated on the boxes condition.

Christi
Wethersfield
