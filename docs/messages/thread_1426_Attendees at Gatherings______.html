From: Steve and Heidi | Date: 2004-10-08 00:19:04 UTC
So we're looking for an honest opinion on this one. We'd love to hear
honest comments from the attendees at the MMM (especially the
organizers). And this is meant to be an open discussion and not a
flame war.....

How many people is too many at a gathering?? Was 350 too much to
handle?

We ask this just as creative dialog for many of the newbies that may
consider hosting a gathering etc. We were part of a very special day
in Niantic a few weeks back which was essentially a small, intimate
gathering of about 15 people and it was a marvelous day.

Flip side of the coin; our first LB experience was a Kids Day 2002
and we were overwhelmed with 50 or so exchanges. It was hectic, we
were overwhelmed. Our 2nd gathering was Molly/Ginger and there were
more people, less confusion and more comfort.

So we don't really mean anything negative at all, just looking for
honest opinions and helpful dialog.....

And thanks to those who undertook that massive task last weekend!
-Steve, Heidi and Madras

Re: Attendees at Gatherings......

From: edelweiss | Date: 2004-10-10 00:02:57 UTC
This Mansfield Monster Mash event was terrific. I had a great time.
You guys missed a doozy of an affair. Chuck and company did a mind-
boggling job of running this thing. The boxes came in from all over
the US, by multitudes of fantastic carvers. All the clues were
painfully exact. The hikes have been amazing. I wished I lived
close enough to Mansfield to make it a regular thing.

Before the event, I was nervous about being overwhelmed by the sheer
volume of exchanges, so my plan was to focus on boxing. In
retrospect, I wish I had arrived earlier (perhaps the night before)
so that I could have boxed first and then spent the better part of
the day exchanging. As it was, I barely did any exchanging, but I
did find over 20 boxes.

However I did meet many cool letterboxers even if we didn't exchange
stamp impressions. There were people from all over the place. I
like to actually put a name to a face and have at least a little
conversation with the people I exchange with. Hasty exchanges, in
which people run up to me, ask to exchange, swap sigs, then run off
to the next person leave me feeling unfulfilled. So I was very slow,
but needed to be for my own self-preservation.

All in all, I would say that the size of the gathering is
unimportant. At the MMM, there were alot of opportunities to
socialize. As with most parties, some people work the crowd like
politicians, some hole up in a corner with their friends and some are
the wallflowers. There's room for everyone. Know yourself and enjoy
it your way.

Laurette

PS- Huge thanks to Chuck, Chickabirdie and all the rest who worked so
hard and made this day go down in letterboxing history.
