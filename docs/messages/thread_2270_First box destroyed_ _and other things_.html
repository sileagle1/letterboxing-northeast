First box destroyed! (and other things)

From: cpascott | Date: 2005-08-01 15:05:00 UTC
<sigh>

I am finally dealing with the loss of a letterbox. Mary's Lamb, my
great little mystery box and one of the two first boxes planted by me
and my former GF, has been destroyed.

Well, maybe not. It may still exist buried under the pile of
construction debris and rubble that used to be it's hiding place.
Actually, I like to think this is the case -- and than a hundred plus
years from now someone will excavate the site and discover the little
letterbox -- a time capsule of our hobby!

The box will be replaced, but no time line has been set.

****

On more positive news, two boxes previously announced have now been
planted!! "Walden", with a great stamp carved by Jacks, has been
placed at Walden Pond State Reservation in Concord, MA, and "I Can't
Carve", the letterbox dedicated to letterboxers who love the hobby but
have no carving ability whatsoever, has been placed in Cochituate
State Park in Natick, MA. Clues are now available on my website,
accessible at http://home.comcast.net/~scott.e.hall/letterbox.html or
via the links on the LbNA database or AQ.

Additionally, to promote more visitors, I've unmasked the mystery that
was my very first box, Red Horse. Up until now it's location has been
a mystery (although an easy one to solve). As much as I like mystery
boxes and puzzles, it seems as though many letterboxers pass over
these boxes in lieu of the straight-forward clues leaving the mystery
box to be rarely visited.

When Mary's Lamb is replaced it will remain a mystery box, but Red
Horse is no longer. I have recategorized it on LbNA and AQ and listed
it's location within the box's clues.

That's my update for now! In four weeks The Lady in Black will be
placed and I'm also working on my very first series!

And, of course, looking forward to Moose Hill Mach 3 coming up later
this month!
