Re what to pack for winter boxing....I have one word for you:

KLEENEX!!!!

I'm not kidding. I went boxing today and boy does the old nose run in cold
weather, LOL. I also carry an old ski pole, which helps me keep my balance
and get up and down trails safely.

Found the In My Heart and Good Luck boxes in Ashfield, MA today. Beautiful
spots, although the trails are snow/icy right now. I didn't mind but would
not have taken kids--too slippery for little ones. These are new boxes
that haven't had many visitors, so go find them!

Tried but failed to find Chip N Dale at Lake Fitzgerald Reservation in
Northampton, MA. I think the long flat rock I needed to see must have been
buried under snow. It was a great hike, very scenic, so I'll go back
another time.

I also bring energy bars, fruit, water, extra Ziplocks, and paper towels
whenever I set out, even if I think it's a quick hike (sometimes they turn
into long treks).

Bonnie
(who's at F94 and would like to hit F100 by New Year's Day. Any
suggestions for 5 or 6 boxes in one place that are doable right now in the
snow? I'm willing to drive down to Connecticut.)

Phyto, Isosceles and crew did some boxing today. Started out
with Brodie Park. Replaced all the bags in that box. They were
slimey. Moved on to Hermit's Grave. This isn't listed on the new
site anymore, but I'm happy to report that we placed some
greens in honor of this site for the box is very much alive! Did
some box/bag repair at that site too.

Finished the Nature of the Forest Series at the DEP in
Barkhamsted in the dark. This is alive and well but it was a
stretch for us finishing up at 5:15pm!!! Thank goodness for the
sliver of moonlight. It's dark at 4pm in Maine usually.

So, my suggestion (for winter boxing) would be to bring paper
towels to wipe out the inside of the box and EXTRA PLASTIC
ZIPLOC FREEZER BAGS.
