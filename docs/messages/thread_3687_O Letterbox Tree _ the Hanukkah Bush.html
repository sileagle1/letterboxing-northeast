From: cclightningbug | Date: 2007-11-23 22:39:20 UTC
We have received our first holiday stamps! They came all the way from
Seattle and are phenomenally carved by South Hill Hendersons and
Triple T. They represent Hanukkah traditions.

Since Hanukkah begins on December 5th this year, they will be
available from that date on in or under the Hanukkah Bush located
between the menorah and dreidel window ornaments and will remain in
place through January 1st. O Letterbox Tree clues will be posted on
December 10th and will remain letterbox-decorated through January 1st.

In case you missed the details in the original post:

2007 O Letterbox Tree & the Hanukkah Bush
The possibility of ocean-effect snow flurries are showing up in our
local weather forecasts, a sure sign that the time has come to think
about O Letterbox Tree & the Chanukah Bush once again. We decided to
give this year's series a theme, a way of getting to know each other
better: holiday traditions from our families or heritage. We will have
both our letterboxing Christmas tree decorated and our Chanukah bush
under the light-up dreidel and menorah ready on the weekend after
Thanksgiving.

From then through December 10th, we will gratefully accept
contributions of stamps from those who want to celebrate with our
corner of the country. Archimedes' Screw and I will supply a decorated
ornament/box in which we will hide your stamp. You do not need to make
a logbook. We will ask each finder to log in on cardstock which I will
scan and return to you in full color with your stamp.

If you live locally and wish to make your own container for your stamp
and put it on the tree yourself, it will be ready and waiting for you
from November 24th through December 10th. We will post the clues as a
series on the evening of December 10th. At that point we will consider
the series complete so visitors don't feel that they have to wait
until January 1st to come to the tree in order to collect them all.
For those who wish to contribute after that date, a hitchhiker,
perhaps one with a note asking it to remain with the tree until
January 1st, would be a delightful addition. We ask you to post the
hitchhiker on your own wherever you want and we will send them on
their way either in boxes we find during our winter boxing or with the
stamps and copies of the log that we return to the generous contributors.

We will retire the tree on the morning of January 2nd and give our
neighbors a break from letterboxing, (at least for a few months.)
Stamps should be mailed to:

Mary Nelson
40 Mockingbird Lane
West Yarmouth, MA 02673

Thank you in advance from the holiday revelers in the Northeast!
-Cape Cod Lightning Bug & Archimedes' Screw
