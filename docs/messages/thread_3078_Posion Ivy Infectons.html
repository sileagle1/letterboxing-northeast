Re: [LbNE] Posion Ivy Infectons

From: Team Safari | Date: 2006-07-19 22:51:54 UTC
This is kind of late in the process, but I'll tell you what I have found to help. Immediately after known exposure, Technu seems to do a great job of preventing an outbreak. They have a product line and sometimes they bundle in a bottle of Caligel (clear gel, not that thick pink junk) anti-itch, which seems to help an itch and dry blisters.

I have gotten horrific cases of PI. If I get an outbreak, it usually becomes systemic. Benadryl (oral) or similar antihistamine helps to help me sleep, but I drag butt. I find calamine lotion, oatmeal, and Cortaid to be totally ineffective for me. Things that have helped in the past:

Pre-emptive shot that works like most allergy shots. I think it's a sort of hair-of-the-dog before the exposure that minimizes reaction. Now that you know you are sensitive, you may want to get a shot around April. I think the timing is important. I haven't done this in a while because I haven't had any serious outbreak in a while.

Technu to clean any urushiol off anywhere that may have come in contact but hasn't erupted yet. I fell completely into a hedge of PI a couple of weeks before my wedding. When I got myself out of that bush (and ivy can grow enormous, especially by the water; it can climb, but doesn't need to in order to grow huge), I ran to the drug store and got Technu, and showered at a friend's house. The branches and leaves had poked in, under, around, and through my clothing. I ended up with only two small batches of rash that I managed to clear up fast on my forearms, and I think that the reason why the Technu couldn't stop the rash on my forearms was because the skin had been broken, letting the plant resin under the skin. Years ago, I would have suffered for weeks from such an exposure, and my skin would scar from it. This time, Technu saved the day. No PI or blotches on The Big Day.

When I have had that rash-on-rash, hideous bubbling, blistering, weeping reaction that crops out all over in places that it should not, the only OTC remedy that dried up the blisters for me was Ivy Dry. I think they have a whole product line now, but all they had then was a brown bottle of a tanic acid solution. It dries up the blisters, whereas calamine would dry up and flake in some places, but combined with that lymphatic (I think) fluid and make a sticky, oozy, and runny mess that would soak into bandages and clothing.

The pharmacist and doctor should be able to recommend things that patients have said worked for them. You can probably get lots of info on line, but you may have to do all your own trials to find out what works for you. I think a cortisone shot saves the day for many people with bad reactions, minimizing the blistering and shortening recovery time. Good luck! And think about asking for the Boo-boo box. I think there's a PLB for just PI, too.

Oh, and make sure you wash everthing that came in contact with the plants really, really well, or throw them away.

HTH
Mary

From: Team Safari | Date: 2006-07-20 04:28:03 UTC
I've never had steroid shots or prescription oral steroidal meds, nothing stronger than OTC allergy meds. My worst (and I mean horrible) cases of PI were when I was much younger. I'd have to be half dead before my parents considered taking any of us (7 kids) to the doctor. In my 20s, I'd have to be half dead before dragging myself to the Dr., unless it was a sign of something potentially dangerous (like breast lumps). I think medical coverage and money or lack thereof dictate what gets treatment for a lot of people; I know that was the case with me. In my family's case, besides my mom being raised a Christian Scientist, I think it was upbringing and mind-set of the Depression and WWII survivors; whatever we got, we treated it with whatever worked for our forebears with home remedies and "common sense," and we gave it time and toughed it out. That which does not kill you... We got vaccinations and such, of course, ...but poison ivy? That was an uncomfortable _learning experience_.

Fast forward to a new century. If Technu hadn't nipped it but for a few spots, I'd have gone and gotten help. I'm older, wiser, and carry a card. BCBS, that is. And a debit/credit card for whatever part of it I have to pay. ;-) Bring on the modern medicine! I'd use it if I needed to.

Mary

From: Pamela Smith | Date: 2006-07-20 12:04:50 UTC
It's probably fine once the oils are totally gone. The hot-shower
thing with oils still present is a horrid idea - think oils roiling
around in water and steam and getting into now-very-open-pores all
over your body. I've seen horror-story pictures of the results and
just thinking about them makes me shudder even now. Should you have a
strong stomach, google can probably point you at some pix.

My worst experience wasn't exactly mine. A woman I knew was out
hiking with friends and they pointedout some poison ivy. I don't know
if it had purple leaves or why she didn't think it was PI, but she
told them it wasn't PI. Well, a discussion ensued and she, being one
of those people who is always right (we all know at least one, lol)
decided to demonstrate that she was right by grabbing a handful of
leaves and rubbing them alllllllll over her face. I can't describe
how terribly itchy and painful that looked once her entire face
erupted in sores. Noone is always right and PI is an awful way to get
a life lesson.......

Careful out there, folks. :-)
pezpam
