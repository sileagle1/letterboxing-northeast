Rescuing Lost HH's

From: Steve and Heidi | Date: 2003-12-19 05:52:13 UTC
Sooooo it's my last midnight shift for the week and I'm sitting here
looking at Wanda and Pete's HH Tracking page and I say to
myself "self, there's a lot of hitchers out there that have not been
updated in a while". Makes you wonder if they are "stranded", if
someone hasn't posted their travels in a while, or if they got lost
somewhere.

Any solutions? Probably not. I think we'll be making a real effort to
visit some of the older and perhaps some of the....dare i
say.... "less popular" boxes in the area. I can't say "less popular"
meaning its a "bad" box, but what I mean is that there are many
series' and boxes out there that are just not as well travelled for
some reason. Example: I dropped off a HH at Jay's new one "White
Rock Quarry" in September. Being a fine carving and a new box, not to
mention I gave a huge hint, I expected someone to hop on it. Much to
my suprise, it was still there when I took B'Fly by that locale about
10 days ago.

Enough rambling, but I'd encourage everyone to take a few minutes to
revist boxes in your area once in a while so HH don't get stranded.
Also, post the travels once in a while. We've put 2 out and really
had no reports recently, and that becomes discouraging! If you
placed a hitcher somewhere and its dropped off the face of the earth,
perhaps stop back, check, and move it along??

Anxiously awaiting the comments...

-Steve (with Heidi and Madras snuggled like bugs in the rug back at
the ranch)

From: Janet | Date: 2003-12-19 12:49:14 UTC
Hi Steve,

My very first box ever is a hitchiker called "Man's Best Friend" that
I released in April of 2002. I carved the stamp with love and
released it in a special place. It disappeared in the summer of 2002
after being found by only two groups of people. The last known
people to see the HH were Latte Man & Curious Crane. They dropped it
off at Trish Kurdziel's Midnight on the Moon LB in Narragansett, RI.
Trish pulled a damaged log book from the box a while ago. I asked
her if she could figure out who got my HH. The log book was unclear
so it remains a mystery where my first letterbox went. In a year and
a half it has never turned up :( I don't expect I will ever hear
from it again. This has made me very reluctant to releasing
hitchhikers. I have posted several times asking if anyone has seen
this HH with no response.

Janet
