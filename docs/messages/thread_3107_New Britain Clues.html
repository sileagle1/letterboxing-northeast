State: Connecticut

County: Hartford

Nearest city: New Britain

Number of boxes: 1

Clues
Stamp by rtrw
New Britain needs a letterbox so here we go again.
This is an easy walk of about one half hour.

From Stanley Street (Rt. 71) turn onto Eddy Glover Blvd. near the university.
At the first stop sign turn into Stanley Quarter Park and park along the road
as soon as you're able.

As you turn into the park there's a large yellow block with a one way sign,
this is where you want to walk back to cross a stone bridge.
Over the bridge stay on the road to the left past a ball field and skate
board heaven. Take the dirt road to the left of these, crossing another stone
bridge. At the fork go left (or straight) on the road less traveled up a gradual
incline, ignoring all side trails, stay on this main path. At the top when you
can see a white house ahead of you, you will cross over a log on the trail (I
've had many notifications that this log is missing, but it used to be 105
steps before the cement block at the end of the trail). Looking ahead to the right
is a two trunked tree with the perfect letterbox hole at it's base where
Stanley Quarter Carousel will (hopefully) spend some time.
After carefully rehiding, go back the way you came and when you get near the
first stone bridge take a moment on the "big peoples" swings near the pond.

Hike Length: 2.5 miles
Clue Last Updated: November 4, 2005

Status: Stanley Quarter Ski Slope — active (Last found: July 15, 2005)
Stanley Mansion — active (Last found: May 21, 2005)

This hike may not be suitable for young kids and dogs due to the high amount
of broken glass and other hazards. Poison ivy is also fruitful on this trip.
Please wear long pants and be mindful of it!

Stanley Quarter Park is on Stanley Street at the intersection of Eddy Glover
Boulevard.

Walk towards the skate park and take the dirt trail to the left of the park.
Go straight over the stone bridge and when you have to make a choice, go
north. When you get to an open area with a hill up to your left, go left on the
overgrown path. As you go up, you may notice poles with wheels on them to your
left. That was the old ski tow for the old ski slope you are currently on. When
you get to the top, you will find the old parking area. Go SW through the
parking area to another trail. Find a two-sister tree on the right, with one
having a blue band on it. Find the Stanley Quarter Ski Slope Letterbox behind the
tree.

Go back the way you came and go until you are at the bottom of the ski slope.
Go left onto the main trail. At the next intersection, go right over the
wooden bridge with metal rails. At the T, go left and just before the road, turn
right. As you go up the hill, go around, under or over (whichever way works for
you) the "X." Just after the X, take the left turn which takes you over the
road and to the concrete barriers. After the concrete barriers, take the first
right. Soon, you will come across some ruins to your right (does anyone know
exactly what this is?). Keep to the right at the jumble of trails. On your
right, notice the band of overgrown evergreens on the hill edge. These were
planted by the Stanley family to give them privacy. Soon on your left, you will come
to a second set of ruins. This is the old Stanley Mansion. Find the circle in
the ruins (poison ivy everywhere... and be careful in the ruins!). For those
of you who like trees (Butterfly didn't believe me when I told her), there's a
GINKO TREE just east of the NE side of the mansion wall! From the NE side of
the circle go eight steps at 300 degrees. The Stanley Mansion letterbox is in
the corner of the wall. PLEASE REHIDE VERY CAREFULLY!

Go back to the road and turn left down the hill. You will notice the Stanley
Quarter Park entrance to your right, and just inside, your waiting car. Have
fun and be careful!

I don't have a problem with this since my clues are not listed online,
but if they were then I would object to the re-publication of my clues
without my prior permission. This is one cardinal "rule" of
letterboxing, not publishing the stamp or printed clue without the
placers permission. I would be equally irritated if I found out that
a yahoo talk list group was publishing clues that I have specifically
taken offline and they were distributing an online version of them.
This is wrong and let this be a warning that those boxes will go
missing if that ever happens.

Just my 2 cents.

I finally got around to putting out the replacements for two of my series'
boxes that had gone missing: The 4th box of The Castle of Scovell and the
3rd box of Ol' John Joseph's Railroad series. The clues for the Castle box
are the same and the stamp is also the same. The clues for the Railroad box
are a little different, with a new location and a new stamp. For those who
have done this series already, it would be considered a new find. The name
of the new box is The Engine, and the new clues are as follows starting with
"come to a stream with no bridge", instead it will read:



Come to a bridge, which was newly constructed as an Eagle Scout project. Do
not cross the bridge. Look under it on the left side, and there you will
find your treasure.



I will be posting the new clues as soon as the site is up and running again.
For those who have done the Castle boxes, I hope you will return and get the
last piece of the castle.



Any questions, let me know. Thanks.
