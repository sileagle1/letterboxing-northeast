That happened to us, as well. Despite what the clues say about clues
for the other boxes being available in the office, the ranger actually
in the office had no idea what I was talking about. He did mention
that the man in charge of the Junior Ranger program (John, who posted
the clues) was outside with this year's class of Junior Rangers, and
John provided us with the laminated sheet containing the other clues.
Most we could not find, and one of the three we did locate we stumbled
across scattered through the woods. We brought it back with us because
we had no idea where it was originally hidden, but it was in such bad
shape that they just tossed it.

While I applaud the Junior Ranger program at this park and wish we had
something similar in NY, I would not recommend a special trip solely
to find this series of twelve boxes. I can provide more details to
potential searchers off-list, but out of respect for the Junior
Rangers who planted these boxes, that's all I'll say here.

The state park itself, however, is just lovely, and the quarry and
natural bridge are well worth the visit. The rangers were incredibly
helpful and even lent me the trail map off the wall when they couldn't
find a copy for me to take into the woods. If you're near North Adams
and looking for someplace beautiful to explore, I can't say enough
about how much we all enjoyed walking the trails and checking out the
quarry. We fell in love with the Berkshires during our drive (and many
letterboxing-inspired hikes) across MA, starting with this hike.

If you're looking for other family-friendly letterboxing adventures in
the area, my kids highly recommend the Scooby Doo and SpongeBob
series. Great stamps, beautiful hikes - what more could you ask for?
We're already planning our next road trip back to the area
specifically to find the many boxes we didn't have time for on this
particular trip. And if you find yourself driving on Rt. 2 past
Shelburne Falls as we did, be sure to walk across the Bridge of
Flowers (a location just screaming for a microbox) and then go eat at
Cafe Martin. The food was so amazing there that we changed our return
route just so we could stop in again on the drive home.
