Red Onion LB, Wethersfield Conn.?

From: connfederate | Date: 2006-03-31 02:05:01 UTC
I work in Wethersfield, Conn. and there are only a couple of boxes in
town, a veritable desert in Conn. 'box-dom...
I delved a little into local history and had this idea for placing a
letterbox to honour the "Wethersfield Red Onion". I carved a stamp,
put to-gether a log, boxed the contents and started looking for a
suitable hiding place.
Then I printed the clues for RTRW's Wintergreen Woods series. The
clue references "Christi, Jeff and Liam's" Red Onion LB (among
others), clues to which are not available on any resources I have
available. I am a fresh-fish (noob), having only started in
Nov. '05. Does anyone have information on when and where it was
planted? And is this 'box still viable or has it been
retired/abandoned/gone-missing? I'd hate to step on anyone's toes if
the original 'box is still out there or to duplicate one that was
recently retired.

The "sixtyfourthousanddollar" ($64,000) question is, envelope
please: do I plant it or send it out as a hitch-hiker?

This is the second time I've started working on an idea for a LB only
to find that it has been done...except this time I have a LB. Re-
inventing the wheel, LOL! :-D

Thanx for your suggestions.
Connfederate

Re: [LbNE] Red Onion LB, Wethersfield Conn.?

From: christi tilton | Date: 2006-03-31 02:33:00 UTC
Hello,
The series that had the red onion lb was pulled after someone at the Webb-Dean house found out that two of the boxes in the series were on their property. They asked that I remove them. This was the first series I planted and realized long after that I should have asked them before putting them on the grounds of the houses. The Red Onion box was planted down by the cove and for some reason we have still not gone and pulled that one, although with flooding it may or may not be there still. Feel free to plant your box. Our toes are not stepped on. In fact I'd be happy to have another box to look for in Wethersfield. Please let me know when you plant it so we can get out there and box.

Christi, Jeff and Liam
Wethersfield, CT

Re: Red Onion LB, Wethersfield Conn.?

From: connfederate | Date: 2006-04-04 01:10:26 UTC
Christi,
Thanx for e-mailing the clues to me. I was able to get out this
afternoon after work to look for the *ORIGINAL* Red Onion LB. I
found it!
The last log entry/stamp was dated 13.Feb.'05. It was in place,
well hidden, and everything was bagged inside the box and in GREAT
shape. There was a little condensation in the box and the stamp was
damp but the log was high and dry on top of the stamp. Nice stamp!
I duly stamped-in and recorded F109! :D
Just my humble opinion but I think you should re-list this 'box
on the LbNA and/or Atlas Quest sites. THANK YOU for the find.
Connfederate
