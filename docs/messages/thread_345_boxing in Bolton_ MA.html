boxing in Bolton, MA

Hi all,

Binx and I spent a wonderfully sunny day boxing in Bolton, MA...half
of which with Amymisha and Camera Obscura (hi guys!). We've decided
that Bolton must have more stone walls than just about any town in
New England we've yet been to!

Most of the boxes in this town were originally planted in 2000 and
the clues posted only in the town newspaper. More recently, the
boxes have been adopted by active letterboxers (thanks Seekyr,
Blaze, & Mobius!) and published to the LBNA site. Many of the clues
require some light puzzle-solving and/or knowledge of the town's
many wonderful conservation areas, so plan for some pre-boxing
research. Though we haven't yet solved all the clues, we've been
enjoying getting to know the wild parts of this pretty town.

These boxes are severely under-visited and need the love and ink of
more letterboxers. Go and check em out!

Solor

From: lb_seekyr | Date: 2004-03-10 01:25:57 UTC
We're glad to hear that you, Binx, Amymisha, and Camera Obscura are
all enjoying the Bolton series. Thanks so much for your help in
getting the Bolton Bull's Eye water tight again.

For those of you who would like to learn more about the Bolton Series
you can visit our web pages on it here:

http://www.gladehome.us/clues.html

If you are unfamiliar with Bolton and its conservation areas take a
look at this map for an overview of the area and links to the Bolton
Trail Guide descriptions of the areas.

http://www.gladehome.us/Bolton-MA-Trails.html

Nearby Harvard and Acton also have a few boxes, and Westborough and
Leominster (both of which have excellent letterbox series) are each
within a quick half hour drive from here as well. So there's plenty
of Boxing action to be had in the area.

Enjoy and keep visiting!

Cheers,
-Seekyr
