MA/RI boxes ... some found, some missing (?)

From: cpascott | Date: 2004-08-11 17:40:50 UTC
Anjabanja and I got 6 boxes over the weekend ... all found in good
shape. We were lucky enough to be the first finder for 2 of them,
raising our first finder count to 4!

Otis Smith Farm (Cumberland, RI)
Wandering Bird and Catch a Falling Star (Franklin, MA)
Old Landing (Marion, MA)
Pratt Farm and Oliver Mill (Middleborough, MA)

We attempted to hunt for some of the Scout Law Series, but weren't
very successful with the first one we went after ... the first one,
Trustworthy, is said to be in the "Veronica Geddes Bowen Wildlife
Preserve", "0.5 miles" on Angell Road (from Mendon Road). At 0.5
miles, however, we saw no trails or mention of a preserve, just
houses. We asked a couple of locals and they had not heard of the
preserve either. A check of our Gazetter yielded nothing
either. ??? We haven't contacted the placer yet. Has anyone found
this first box? If so, what did we miss?

We also went after the Diamond Hill letterboxes, but the Cumberland
Fest was going on at the park and there was no way to get to the
boxes -- even if we paid the admission to get into the fair.

Finally, the Almost Last but Not Least letterbox in Woonsocket, RI
appears MIA. We're contacting the placer to determine if is missing
or if we missed something, so it's status is not yet confirmed.

A lot of bad luck continued for us Saturday as we hunted boxes in
southern MA. We couldn't get near some of the Seaside Series boxes
as there were too many people around blocking the box or beacuse we
were unable to park (many areas required a resident sticker).

We did hunt the Dunseith Gardens and Island Wharf letterboxes, both
part of the series, and found both to be missing. Again, we have
not contacted the placers yet and could have simply missed them. We
did get one box from the series (The Seaside Series II, actually) --
Old Landing.

Work has been incredibly busy so I have not had time to update our
website with any stories of our finds yet, but I will soon. And,
lastly, because of other commitments and a lack of time, the
finishing touches on one of the two boxes we're placing has not been
completed -- and the placement of those two boxes remains delayed
despite my telling all of you that we will be placing them soon.
Well, that's true, we will be placing them soon -- and, I promise,
soon means before Labor Day. We'll let you know!

Regards to all ...

CPAScott

Hi All -
We have just recently discovered this LbNE Yahoo group. We have
been letterboxing for about a year now, but 'casually' : we don't go
out every weekend, we didn't carve our own stamps, we don't keep
close count of what we have found ( altho everything is stamped in
our logbook ), we forget to exchange stamps with people whom we have
introduced to letterboxing.....we just like the hiking so much and we
love discovering new places and finding boxes...there are so many
things we would never have seen, if we were not out there,
letterboxing!!
We tried to find Dunseith Gardens, in July and were not successful.
We tend to think this is because we don't look hard enough. Now we
don't feel too badly, since it seems to be actually missing. There
was no response to our attempt to contact the placer.
We are off to Ogunquit ME for three days at the end of this month
and look forward to some nearby letterboxes there. Any ideas?
