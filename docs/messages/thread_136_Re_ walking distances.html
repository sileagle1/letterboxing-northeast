From: Drew Family | Date: 2004-01-07 03:31:10 UTC
Hiya! Good question: like many others have said, we also use the
distances and times given by authors in their clues to match our own
time constraints. Since we often mountain bike our hikes, in our
early placing days we had lots of logbook comments along the lines
of "whoa! that was a LONG mile, guys." People still tell us that "if
you haven't found a Drewclan box you haven't gone far enough..." So
to give better distances we bought the very reasonably priced Topo!
map computer program from National Geographic. It's pretty cool: we
draw out our route and it gives nearly exact mileages and elevation
gains. Since most of our boxes are in Connecticut, with a high point
about 800' above sea level, elevation isn't much of a factor, but our
mileages should be pretty accurate. That's another option, especially
if you like fiddling with maps. For free, you might try the website
topozone dot com.

Jay in CT, off to gaze at the nearly full moon
