OT/Internet Safety

From: cavy_lovers4 | Date: 2004-03-30 03:14:54 UTC
Hello all,

Given recent discussions, I thought I would share a link to a site
with good, common sense internet safety information. Though directed
at parents, teens and kids, much of it makes sense for adults as
well. The link is "http://www.getnetwise.org/"

Our community has been fortunate to have had several forums for both
kids and parents on this topic. One of the best ideas I picked up
was to put the computer in the family room or wherever most people
hang out and set it up so the screen is visible to others in the
room. (Only drawback is when trying buy gifts over the internet...)

Other guidelines we try to follow are:
- Just as you stay away from strangers on the street, be
careful about strangers on the Internet.

- Never give your real last name, address, or telephone number
to anyone; if someone asks for this information (or for your
password) , don't respond. Log off and tell a trusted adult.

- Never agree to meet with someone you've talked with on the
Internet without parents with you. (For parents I guess we'd say
with a buddy).

- Don't let people online trick you into thinking of them as
real-life friends if you've never met them in person. Just the same,
don't let people goad you into online fights (tough one!)

- If you get suspicious e-mails, files, or pictures from
someone you don't know and trust, trash them just like any other
junk mail. You could have a lot to lose by trusting someone you've
never even met. The same goes for clicking links or URLs that look
suspicious - just don't do it.

- Do not fill out any form online that asks you for any
information about yourself or my family without asking parents
first. This includes forms for contests or registering at a site.
Also check to see if the sites have a privacy policy & if they
promise to keep your private information private. If they don't
promise to keep your private information private, don't give them
any private information. (Talk to your parents about what "private
information" is, such as credit card numbers.)

Parents need to work hard to get kids to internalize these rules.
Kids are so proficient at computers they may justifiably feel that
they know more than their parents do!


Hope this is helpful to someone!
Cavy Lovers
