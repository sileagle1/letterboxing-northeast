MA - at long last-announcing the Moose Hill Mach 3 gathering

From: kimcalcagno | Date: 2005-07-25 00:08:26 UTC
Come one, come all to the third annual letterboxing gathering at
Moose Hill Wildlife Sanctuary in Sharon, MA!
Find directions at
http://www.massaudubon.org/Nature_Connection/Sanctuaries/Moose_Hill/i
ndex.php

This event will take place on Sunday, August 21, 2005 beginning at
12 noon and lasting until round about 5 p.m. or so. Our past two
have been great fun and this year should be no different.

There are several permanent boxes on the sanctuary and many more
within close proximity at nearby parks.

Thanks to the generous folks at the sanctuary (doing me a favor yet
again, since I no longer work there), we will be allowed to use the
canopies and picnic tables in the field adjacent to the nature
center and parking lot. (I hope folks will consider a small donation
to the sanctuary as a way to say "thank you" for this courtesy.)

A few items of note:
The restrooms are in the nature center, which closes at 5 p.m.
Alas, as always, dogs are not allowed on the sanctuary.
Smoking and alcohol are also not permitted. (The town of Sharon has
a no public smoking statute)
We will have a hitchhiker hostel and will observe strict "bring one
to take one" rule as well as "no double, triple etc dipping" to
prevent the over-exploitation of hitchhikers.
CSCM and I will be hosting and will provide cold drinks and we
welcome folks to bring their favorite dish.

I will carve an event stamp but certainly welcome any other event
stamps or traveling event stamps to join us that day.

Please feel free to contact me if you have any questions, comments
or concerns....or if you'd like to RSVP.
We hope you'll be able to join us on this relatively short notice.

Cheers!
-Kim (Rustypuff)

report from Gillette Castle & Devil's Hopyard

From: Silent Doug | Date: 2005-07-25 03:02:59 UTC
Another banner day of letterboxing for Clueless and me today -- a
total of 36 finds (including one hitchhiker), a new personal best for
each of us. We hiked about 11 miles through Gillette Castle and
Devil's Hopyard State Parks, and here's what we found:

Sherlock Holmes #1-2
African Queen
As You Wish #1-5
Use Your Imagination #1-4
The Grinch in Connecticut
Eagle at the Lone Wolf Tree
Hopbox
Devil in the Hopyard
Devil's Hopyard #1
Waterfalls Two
Curious George and the Giant's Chair
A Series of Unfortunate Letterboxes #1-12
Flower Drum Song #2-3

Birthday Cake in Devil's Hopyard has been pulled, which we didn't
know, so we couldn't find that one. We did find the Happy Hitchhiker
and two bonus boxes which brought our day's total to 36.

We replaced baggies when needed (until we ran out!), but most
everything was in good shape.

We also replaced Devil's Hopyard #2 with a new box, stamp and log
(with the permission of Jay Drew), and refreshed #1 with a new log book.

Funny incident at the end of the day -- as we were pulling in to
Flower Drum Song #2, a van pulled in behind us, and Clueless thought
for sure they were following us. Turns out they were -- it was North
Star and Kreepi Kelli! We found Flower Drum Song #2 together and
exchanged new personal stamps and tips and inside info. Quite a fun
chance meeting!

SD
