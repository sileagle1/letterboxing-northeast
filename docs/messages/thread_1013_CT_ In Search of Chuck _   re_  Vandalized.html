Yet ANOTHER one of our boxes has been vandalized. Box #1 of In Search
of Chuck Straub has been torn apart. That make 4 or 5 in a matter
of 2 weeks. We realllllly need to make sure folks rehide better.

We're considering making all our boxes word of mouth... though even
our unpublished boxes get wrecked too. If people keep destroying
boxes, then placers will stop planting, and this hobby's going to
wind up failing...or maybe we're just not very adept at hiding..who
knows....

Sadly, shaking our heads in disbelief...

-Steve, Heidi and Madras

From: uneksia | Date: 2004-07-21 16:22:18 UTC
i think it is so sad when i hear of boxes being destroyed. i live in new
hampshire and out of the 51 boxes i have found only one was destroyed and or
missing. interesting to say that was at a university campus in virginia.
perhaps it is simply a matter of numbers. the more boxes out there the more
likely they are to be found if not hidden or rehidden well. kinda like cars,
the more the numbers increase so do careless drivers and therefore more
accidents. it seems the responsibility might be in who and how we teach
newbies" of which i am one. most people learn of this hobby through word of
mouth. it is important that we just not tell them but show them. go out with
them, have them experience the more finesse art of hiding and rehiding.
just a thought.
smile
uneksia
