Opinion please

From: rifamily | Date: 2009-04-25 10:47:20 UTC
I have gotten an email that has bothered me a bit. I think I know what most
people would say, but I'd like to hear it anyway.

I have a box (Read a <http://www.atlasquest.com/showinfo.html?gBoxId=71963>
book II) located in a state that I will not be returning to. I do have some
boxing friends in the area that would assist me if any maintenance is
needed.

It is a microbox located in a library. When I placed the box I had a small
log book inside. Very small. I was in the area for six months so I was
able to replace it when it filled up quickly. But when I left the area I
removed the logbook as it just wouldn't make sense to try to maintain the
size book that would fit in the microbox. I put the lack of a logbook into
my clues so no one would be confused.

Now I have received an email from the library saying this:
Hi. The library does know about the lettebox, but we have a question: The
lack of a logbook is a little sad for some of our visitors. A big part of
the fun with our other letterbox (directions on AtlasQuest) is looking
through the logbook to see who else has been here.

My question is this. I'm the library's Community Relations Coordinator. I
see you live out of the area. Is there a way we could work together to add a
logbook to your letterbox in our children's section?

I am having mixed feelings. On one hand, that's very nice and helpful of
them to offer. On the other hand, part of letterboxing is stealth. I don't
think I want people walking up and asking for the log book. Plus, not every
box is the same. There ARE boxes without log books. Sure, people like
looking through the log book and adding their own stamp, that IS part of the
fun, I agree, but that doesn't mean it is a requirement.

Additionally, I wonder HOW they found out about the box. I mean, I know
this happens, but I do wonder. Perhaps a librarian, or friend of one is a
boxer and told them about it. Or perhaps a would be finder went and asked
questions..... OR cried to a librarian "poor me, there is no log book, isn't
that sad, boo hoo". A situation, which, obviously, bothers me. I mean, too
bad, some boxes simply don't have logs, especially mircoboxes.

When I planted the box I knew there is another (really wonderful) box it the
library that was planted with the library's assistance. I didn't want mine
to be "known" or else I would have approached the library about it.

I'm sure people will have differencing opinions, and of course I can proceed
how I want, it is my box. My gut is saying to pull it. It is no longer a
secret and the library is telling me how to "run" my box.

Am I taking the low road here? Would the high road be making a beautiful
log and sending it to them, saying Thanks, Librarian for helping me out! I
know that is a very positive approach. But I feel like my box is tainted
some how, it's just not feeling happy to me. Which is odd because I am
usually very go with the flow and have a Pollyanna out look.

Anyway, what do you all think?

RIFamily

From: LundyandVickster@aol.com | Date: 2009-04-25 11:18:24 UTC
RIFamily,

Well you will probably get a variety of opinions here but you did ask for
opinions so here is mine:

You say that lots of boxes do not have logbooks. I have found a few boxes
and I do not see many (if any) letterboxes without logbooks. Some boxes are
part of a series and some of the components of the series have their
logbooks in the first or last box, but I don't know if I ever found a single
stand alone box without a logbook. You seem to have wanted a logbook in this
box when you were regularly maintaining it so why shouldn't that continue?

I think you have a great opportunity here. Someone (a responsible someone)
has offered to maintain this box. Was your real intent of this box to see
if you could keep it a secret from the Librarian? Now that they are onto the
box it doesn't need to be "hidden" from the Librarian so you could
actually arrange to have it swapped out for a larger box and logbook. I think
"pulling your box" because they know about it is a little drastic. For all
intents and purposes you have abandoned this box. The library could "pull it"
for you by throwing it away and then turn around and get rid of the other
nice box you mentioned.

As for people walking up to the Librarian and asking for the logbook, you
could advise people not to do that in the clues.

Larry

From: zess the treehuggers | Date: 2009-04-28 13:02:58 UTC
As with the others...since you asked...

While I understand your frustration, and you can do what you like with your
box, I agree with the general sentiment here. Although I can't hold a candle
to Larry's 3,000+ letterbox finds, I have never found a box that
*intentionallly* did not have a logbook, unless it was part of a series.
That's not to say there aren't any without logbooks, just that they are
extremely rare (at least in my experience), and finders can be puzzled by
it. If they found the box in the library, they might assume it was a library
box, and so asked the librarian to clarify why the logbook was missing, or
to try to be helpful and let the librarian know that it was gone. I think
assuming the best of people when you don't know the actual circumstances,
which it sounds like you usually do, may help you feel better about this
situation (?)

Diane's comments reminded me that I have found abandoned boxes with missing
or full logbooks and they were *littered* with random pieces of scrap paper
that finders stamped and left in the box. And I mean *littered*, it was
awful to see these boxes crammed with all those paper scraps. Stamping in to
the box's logbook is part of the game; finders expect it, and may improvise
if they don't find it.

That the librarian kindly offered to help you maintain the box is a nice,
feel-good kinda thing. I know people have had unauthorized boxes thrown away
w/unpleasant emails sent to the planter. I can't see how this is the
library tying to tell you how to run your box. It looks to me like they were
making an objective observation (the patrons enjoy the logbooks, there is no
logbook in the box) and offering to help since you can't maintain your box.
If it was me, I would have assumed you wanted a logbook in there, especially
since the box started with one. Perhaps you can send several small-enough
logbooks to them so they can keep the log in the box, preventing the need
for finders to ask the librarian about a logbook? Another option, rather
than pulling it, might be to transfer ownership to a local boxer, so people
can still enjoy finding it but you can still divest yourself of it?

As you pointed out, it's up to you, it's your box. If it is going to
irritate you that it is no longer a secret from the library staff, then pull
it or transfer ownership. No point in leaving it there and monitoring the
finds if it raises your ire every time you think about it! It's easy for us
to describe our opinions--we have no emotional attachment to or expectations
for that particular box--what's important is *your* opinion.

~zess~

Now I have received an email from the library saying this:
Hi. The library does know about the lettebox, but we have a question: The

think I want people walking up and asking for the log book. Plus, not every
box is the same. There ARE boxes without log books. Sure, people like
looking through the log book and adding their own stamp, that IS part of the
