MA box report

From: Silent Doug | Date: 2004-08-30 14:34:47 UTC
I finally made it to MA and did some boxing on Saturday. Here's a report on
the 20 I found:

I started off with Night Song in Needham, and then collected the Noanet
series (all 3) in Dover. Next, I took a tour of Rocky Woods/Fork Factory
Brook and found all 4 of Cavy Lovers' boxes there. (Cavy Lovers -- I
stamped the last page of the Star Flower log; folks can start stamping on
the backs of earlier pages, but you might want to replace it.)

Then I found 6 in Bird Park in Walpole: Loose Goose, Spring Leaf, Crows at
Bird Park, Weeping Willow, Cheeseburger Challenge and Happy Birthday, Baby!
(The driving directions for many of these are off -- the turn at the T is
not onto Old Post Road, but Pleasant St. -- at least, that's the street
sign that you see. The street apparently changes names at this intersection.)

Finally, I wrapped up the day with the 5 at Moose Hill Audubon Sanctuary:
Fertile Frond, Trillium and the Moose Hill series (all 3). Two of the Moose
Hill series boxes were left exposed and not properly restored (logs in
baggies, etc.) by a group of day campers who had visited the boxes in the
prior week or so. I put them all back properly.

Very cool -- as dusk approached at the end of my hiking in Moose Hill, I
heard a coyote call and then several others responded from a distance. Then
I spotted one on the road about 75 yards in front of me.

I also picked up The Raven hitchhiker at the Cavorting Cavy box and moved
it along on Sunday, and dropped off another hitchhiker along the way.

Thanks to all you Mass boxers!

Doug

CT box report

From: Silent Doug | Date: 2004-08-30 14:43:15 UTC
Following up on my day in MA, I went boxing on Sunday 8/29 and found the
following:

I started with the DEP American Legion State Forest and DEP People's State
Forest in Barkhamstead, and found them without a problem.

Then I met up with Jennifer and we took off together. In and around
Litchfield, we found the DEP Topsmead State Forest, The Twins Series (both,
lots of water in the first one but the log was dry), Corrina Lynn and Tammy
Ann.

Then we found Tom's Snobbery Series (all 4) and the DEP Mattatuck State
Forest in Watertown, and ended the day with the DEP Paugussett State Forest
and Upper Paugussett boxes in Newtown. We picked up the Lone Wolf
Hitchhiker in the Upper Paugussett box, too. The DEP box in Paugussett was
the 5th DEP box for us both.

We dropped off a hitchhiker along the way and found some bonus boxes, too,
making for a productive few hours of letterboxing!
