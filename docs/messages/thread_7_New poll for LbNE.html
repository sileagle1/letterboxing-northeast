From: Just Beth | Date: 2004-01-05 17:42:59 UTC
I felt the need to throw in my 2 cents because I think I was the party
guilty of suggesting a HH exchange at gatherings. The origional intent of
the HH exchange was sort of like a grab bag. You bring a HH, toss it in a
basket and take another in exchange to hide where ever your letterboxing
travels bring you. The intent was not as a stamping frenzy but it was a way
for HH's to travel to different locations and even different States. One of
my HH's that began in Connecticut was brought out to California and released
via a gathering. I think it's a fun exchange and a great way to sent HH's in
new directions

Now that I've cleared the air and my conscience I will bid you all well.




~Beth

Re: [LbNE] Re: New poll for LbNE (please vote again!)

From: HANNAHKAT@aol.com | Date: 2004-01-05 17:50:30 UTC
Please don't change the poll offerings again, but I found that my feeling was
different from the choices....my feeling is that I would rather find HH's in
boxes, but that I feel compelled to stamp into them at gatherings, so I don't
miss out on them. So, my beliefs and my actions don't jibe, but I think others
may feel that way too.

I wouldn't like to see all the pages of one of my HH's taken up at one place,
at one gathering, since it limits the places it can travel to before it gets
full. I'd much rather see that it has traveled far and wide and gets filled
with the people who carry it on.

I would certainly honor the requests of a placer not to bring a HH to a
gathering if it was so stated in the log (or, in this case, requested on the list).

It's a mixed issue for me about stamping into them at events, but a clear one
when it comes to my own boxes and when following the wishes of a placer. If I
know a placer doesn't want it done, I wouldn't do it. (Of course it means one
more thing to keep track of)

Sigh!
-Kim (Rustypuff)
