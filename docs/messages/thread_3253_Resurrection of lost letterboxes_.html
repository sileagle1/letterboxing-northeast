Resurrection of lost letterboxes!

From: cpascott | Date: 2006-10-09 13:56:00 UTC
Hey all ...

Had a nice Saturday 'boxing with Lundy and Choi up in Andover. Our
main purpose was to collect the Stairway to Heaven series in Ward
Reservation, but also (for me) to pick up the other boxes along the way.

After doing well with our walk up Boston Hill, checking on one of
Choi's boxes, and assisting in the planting of another, we headed over
to Holt Hill and the final part of our hunt.

Although Lundy and Choi already had found Tung 'n' Gruve's Due North, I
had not, so while Lundy went off to pull the last Stairway to Heaven
series box, I hunted Due North ... and came back instead with an
orphaned box!

The box was a mess, only sopping wet paper and no logbook. A very
rudimentary stamp found in the box seemed odd and there was no
reference to the box's name or placer.

Upon deciphering what we could from the papers we found, we discovered
that Team Randalstik had happened upon the box in the same manner I
had -- while looking for Due North. The stamp turned out to be one
carved by the youngest member of the Randalstik team -- aged 5 (which
explained the carving)!

When I first found the box, Choi and Lundy had thought I might of found
another orphaned box -- Girl Scout Troop #3703 -- but it was not. Sure
enough, however, based on Lundy's memory, we also found the remains of
that box as well -- far off of where the clues directed us.

There we sat upon the solistice stones atop of Holt Hill with four
letterboxes -- the fourth Stairway to Heaven series box, Due North, and
two orphans! Of course, with all the boxes out at the same time, we
had to be careful to remember what was what!

We ran into another letterboxer on the hill -- someone new to the hobby
we guessed -- but he didn't stick around long enough to chat or
exchange with any of us.

After logging into Due North and Stairway to Heaven, we contemplated
what to do with our two orphans. We had plenty of fresh bags to
repackage the boxes, and Choi had one extra logbook. We decided to
spruce up and replant the unnamed orphan -- which Choi
rechristened "Flying Spider's Orphan" after the little carver who had
left his contribution in the box! We replanted it in a slightly
different location to move it away from Due North and Choi officially
adopted it (Team Randalstick -- if you would like to adopt it instead,
please let Choi know as I'm certain he will turn it over to you).

As for the Girl Scout Troop, we couldn't spruce it up quite as much as
we had no more extra logbooks, but we did hide it in a better location
and revised the clues accordingly. That box is currently listed as
adopted by me, although I may pass it off to Choi as it will be easier
for him to maintain.

So -- look for two old but new letterboxes out there atop the hills of
Andover!

And thanks to Lundy and Choi for hunting with me!


CPAScott

Team Randalstik is honored that Choi would adopt the box that Flying
