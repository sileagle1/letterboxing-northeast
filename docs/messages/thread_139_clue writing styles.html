clue writing styles

From: funhog1 | Date: 2004-01-07 22:27:05 UTC
As the current discussion shows, everyone has different approaches toward clue
writing. I don't often add a time or distance element to my clues, hoping to make
them a bit more cryptic. However, if I consider some or all of a hike not suitable for
some because of difficulty or possible danger, I ALWAYS say so in my clue.

The element of the unknown is one of the things I like most about this hobby. I don't
want to see the stamp before I open the box. If the clue writer makes me look
carefully at the surroundings as I pass by, I love it but I don't want to be told about
them in advance. If I have to Google parts of a clue, I'm in Hog Heaven. If I have no
idea how long a hike is, c'est la vie. If I have no idea how long a hunt might take I
would probably not try for it on a day that had time constraints. I actually wish there
were more Dartmoor style clues to be found on this side of the pond. These tend to
be nothing more than a set of map coordinates and compass headings to landmarks.
How you get there is up to you.

I have taken a number of kids on letterboxing hikes and I would usually choose a hike
with known perameters if I were going with them. In that case, a clue indicating
length and difficulty would be useful but I'm not in the least bit averse to doing the
research myself to learn about a trail. For me, that's part of the fun.

Fortunately, there are so many different types of people involved in this hobby that
there are a myriad of clue writing styles out there. The possibility of creating a
uniform style is nil. The Mapsurfers of our world will continue to create clues that
take months to decipher. The Green Turtle will take us up impossibly steep gradients.
Kindergartners will take us to the swing set in the local park. There's something for
everybody. Hooray!

Out where I live, there is such a thing called hunting season.
Whenever I place a box in an area where I know that hunting is
permitted from Nov 1 to Feb 1, I *always* start my clues with "the
fashion statement for today is blaze orange". In fact, I wear blaze
orange year round because there always one out there ;-)

I have learned from experience, however, to find a box before taking
my children on an unknown trek. This is kind of fun - I get to box
it myself, then I have the joy of watching them figure out the clues
and finding the box (or giving random nudges when they are off
course).

If I go with an adult letterboxing partner, then all bets are off!
