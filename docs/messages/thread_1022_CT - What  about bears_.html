From: rscarpen | Date: 2004-07-23 15:21:56 UTC
> There are bears in CT (and other places as well). What do you do
> as a letterboxer if you come across one?

I generally stand stupidly with my mouth gaping open. If you see a
bear--unless your in Shenandoah NP or something--seeing a bear is
real treat. I hiked the entire Appalachian Trail from Georgia to
Maine and one saw bears once--in the Shenandoahs.

Bears are VERY shy animals. You could walk by hundreds of them and
not even know they are there. They hear or smell you coming from a
long way off, and generally make a point of steering clear of
people. If you are *lucky* enough to see one, just don't do
something stupid like chase it and you should be okay. =)

And if you see cubs nearby, stand WAY back.

-- Ryan

Re: CT - What about bears?

From: swampyankeesunshine | Date: 2004-07-24 10:39:19 UTC
Generally speaking black bears (the ones we have in this area) will
avoid you. Their senses are much more acute than yours, specially
their sense of smell. You may spook one if you approch up wind, but
bears are more likely to run from something then turn to see what it
was. If you do run into an aggresive bear your best chance of
survival is to back away and give ground. Don't turn and run and all
the advice you hear about laying down and playing dead applies to
brown bears (not in this area) not black. A brown bear is much more
likely to be aggressive and consider you a threat, if you play dead
you're no longer a threat and it may just go away. If a black bear
is aggressive (which is very rare) he most likely considers you a
food sorce and you should defend yourself or give ground immediatly.
