Great CT Boxin' Day

From: Sara | Date: 2006-04-09 23:20:35 UTC
We set out this morning around 9 am and headed North from West Haven,
CT. We returned just before 7 with 9 stamps!

Here's what we accomplished today-

DEP Naugatuck State Forest
DEP Topsmead State Forest
Lil' Banditos' Hermansita
- We didn't look for the second box (Fernando) we had already
found too many ticks on our legs and didn't wish to see more!
DEP Mattatuck State Forest
DEP Nepaug State Forest
Cunning Kids' Nepaug Series
- Funny, as we located the 3rd box, we saw a father and two small
children coming up the trail, so we pretended to be doing something
else, hoping they would pass and we could get the LB unseen. Instead
they stopped, and the kids were obviously watching us. After a few
mins of both us and them trying to look like nothing was going on,
they asked if we were LBoxers and turns out it was the Cunning Kids
themselves! They were so happy to see us searching for their
letterboxes. It was really neat, and it was good to meet them and
their "cunning" dad and dog as well!
DEP Paugnut State Forest

So now, needless to say, we are exhausted.. to think we had left with
clclues to over 20 boxes (just in case)... we could barely walk back
to the car from the 9th ! Ha ha ha.. All in all, beautiful weather..
and even more beautiful parks! (If you've never been to Paugnut & its
Burr Pond.. its a must see!)

*TeamHappilyEverAfter*

From: Fisher & family | Date: 2006-04-10 06:26:40 UTC
The first time I saw another boxer I was with a bunch of other NEW boxers, (see that as 6 screaming kids running down a hill in a cemetery) showing no respect for where we were. I saw a lone man taking the same steps that were directed in the clues. I so wanted to go and ask for an exchange but didn't want to put a "lone wolf" in the middle of such chaos I had with me but I did watch him and wish I got that exchange. I went back to the box on one of the following days to see who it was and he didn't seem to stamp in. I don't know why... So I don't know who he was...mystery.

My next experience was with Veronica and Tony (Tony was at work ;). But we walked past each other. And I had to be brave enough to go back and say something like are those clue sheets in your hand. After that it was fun.

My next time was at Mansfield Monster Mash. The highlight of boxing for us. Walk in the woods on any day during then and make an exchange... Giggling as we each pretend that it's natural to be walking in the woods with papers in hand. Laughing when they are still looking and you've already stamped in. "should we leave this out for you?"

Be brave, look for papers in their hands or ink on there fingers. A simple leading question like whatcha up to might help you know if they are a lboxer or just an ordinary hiker.

:) Fisher in CT
