New Single Day Record

From: cpascott | Date: 2006-04-03 15:36:52 UTC
Hey all!

Wow ... spent a whole day and evening in East Lyme this past Saturday
with Pointer Party and Brandy You're a Fine Girl. I think this April
1 was "Letterboxing Fools Day" instead as that's what we were!

Brandy and I started early -- 6:30 am -- at the Veterans Memorial Park
in Niantic, CT picking up Stump the Ump and Take Me Out to the
Ballgame. We headed up the street to pick up Jay Drew's Save the
Hills (Laurel Tract) box as well before finding a place for breakfast
(the guy walking his dog said there were no diners around, so we ended
up at McD's only to find a diner about 1/4 mile up the road ... @#$%!).

Pointer Party joined in at 8:00 when we all met at Rocky Neck State
Park (aka Dartmoor US) for a full day of scrambling to pick up as many
of the boxes in that park as possible without backtracking too much
Including mystery and word-of-mouth boxes that we knew of, we were
aware of over 50 boxes in the park covered on 20-some sets of clues!
A little hard to coordinate without backtracking, let me tell you! I
had plotted most of the East Lyme boxes on my Gazeteer before-hand,
and Brandy plotted the boxes within Rocky Neck, so we had a general
idea where things where before we started. Out-of-order was the call
of the day, especially for those large series such as
Woodland-Marshland-Water (8 boxes) and Denizens of Rocky Neck (14 boxes)!

It was a pleasure to discover that all but a couple of the boxes were
there, in place, and in good shape. There was one mystery box we
could not find, and we found the third box in the American Heart Walk
series missing. Three Shells by the Seashore was a bit confusing as
well as we found one box in a location that was not in the clues, and
an unrelated box (with no name or placer information) in a spot where
one of the Three Shells boxes was supposed to go! Thus, we all have
the "Unknown" box by the "Unknown" placer ticking up our F-count by one!

After having a letterboxer's picnic there at the park we decided that
after 5 hours or so we were getting bored with Rocky Neck so we headed
out to pick up a couple other boxes in the area -- and ended up
spending nearly 2 hours hunting for a single mystery box that we had
cracked over lunch (kudos to Brandy and Mike from the Pointer Party
for figuring out the clues!!) We needed a little push to get us over
the final hump, but we got it! Which box was it you say? We'll never
tell!!!! :-P

As darkness was falling we found ourselves at the Book Barn -- a very
interesting and odd sort of book store on Route 156 -- and a must stop
for anyone driving through the area (letterboxers included -- there
are 4 published boxes there!)

Pointer Party headed home, but Brandy and I weren't quite done yet --
so back to Rocky Neck we went! Sneaking in through the hiker's
parking lot (technically tresspassing as it was after dark!) we picked
up a few more of the Denizens series, Wanda and Pete's Two Cats and
some others before finally calling it a day at about 9:00 that night
-- 14½ hours after we first started!

It wasn't really a 'boxing marathon as we need much endurance for the
jaunt -- we barely left the 2 sq. miles of Rocky Neck!

When all was said and done, between published boxes, mysteries, a
couple of word-of-mouths and hitchhikers, 54 boxes was my count -- a
new single day record outside of an event!

I'm going to hit that F1000 sooner than I think!

I think we left about 16 or so boxes behind in Rocky Neck, so we'll
have to head back there another time, but probably not the day we head
to Hartman Park in nearby Lyme -- which claims 50 or so boxes on its
own. Geez, two mini-Dartmoors right near each other! I'm going to
wear out my new hiking boots quickly!

The following is the list of boxes found in good condition on our
hunt. It represents only some of the boxes found that day (no
mystery, WOM or bonus boxes are listed here):

* C&H's Stump the Ump (aka The Umpire)
* McDuck's Take Me Out to the Ballgame
* Drew Clan's East Lyme Land Conservation Trust Series - Laurel Hill
Tract (aka Save the Hills)
* Wanderlust's American Heart Walk Series - Boxes #1 and #2 (#3 was no
where to be found)
* Music Woman's Dolphin in the River
* Lauralei's Denizens of Rocky Neck Series:
* The Seagull (box 1)
* Horseshoe Crab (box 2)
* Ribbed Mussel (box 3)
* The Frog (box 4)
* The Skunk (box 5)
* Snowy Egret (box 9)
* The Squireel (box 10)
* The Fox (box 11)
* The Osprey (box 12)
* The Most Precious Citizens of Rocky Neck (box 14)
* (note: we did not hunt boxes 6 - 8 or 13)
* Lulu's Three Shells by the Seashore Series (This series needs some
maintenance as things are out of place. The placer has been notified.)
* Sadie & Russ' Think Right
* Music Woman's Happy 75th Birthday Butterfly
* Butterfly's Tree Tor
* Karen's Red Dragon (This box is in place, even though the website
says it is missing. We found it in a location other than what the
clues said and did our best to replant it where it should have been.
The placer has been notified.)
* Butterfly's Marshland-Woodland-Water Series:
* Rocky Neck (box 1)
* The Damsel (box 2)
* Fritillary (box 4)
* (note: we did not hunt boxes 5 - 8)
* Columbian Bugs' Fruit of the Dune Series (first 2 boxes; the 3 was
listed as missing by the placer so we did not hunt for it)
* Gal Pals' Wind Beneath Her Wings
* Sewsobizzy's Relax and Read
* Sewsobizzy's Book Barn Series
* Pudding Beth's Boo Box
* AM/PM's Tea Party Series - Cream and Sugar (this is the only box in
this series we hunted)
* Wanda and Pete's Two Cats
* Swamp Yankee & Sunshine's Butterfly's Hat

We also found three hitchhikers:
* Fish or Man's Flying Disk (currently with me)
* Celtic Quinn's Fear Itself Hitchhiker Series - Felinophobia
(currently with me)
* Chunna's JLB Angel (currently with Brandy You're a Fine Girl)


CPAScott
