Re: [LbNE] new to letterboxing

From: Melissa Ray | Date: 2009-02-17 00:00:27 UTC
Hi dinosaurmom3,

I have been letterboxing with my son (now 8) for three years. It is
definitely a disappointment when that box isn't there. Make sure when
you check the clue online, that someone has found the box very
recently - that greatly increases your chance that it will be there.
Also, pick boxes that are planted off the beaten path. They are less
likely to disappear than boxes hidden in an obvious hollow tree, or
someplace easily found by non-boxers passing by. Sometimes you really
have to persevere and double-check that you are closely following the
directions. The box could be under your nose. It's hard to keep at
it, with the kids getting impatient while you look - but it's actually
a good lesson for them in sticking to a task.

It's definitely worth the effort to keep trying it with them. The
look on my son's face when he got his 50th box was priceless. Our log
book contains the story of each day that we went boxing, and now it's
a real treasure of memories.

redcloak
Sleepy Hollow, NY

From: von der Insel | Date: 2009-02-18 16:02:38 UTC
Yes, it is becoming quite common (unfortunaltely) for boxes to go missing.
On both sites there is a way of seeing when the last letterboxer found that box.
Try to go for boxes that have recently been found - at least for the near future - so the boys don't get discouraged.
It's hard to convince you children that the hike is enough!
Also include areas that have something else to offer that the boys will enjoy - like a good romp in a nearby playground or searching for shells and rocks on a beach.
Before going out (if you plan ahead) try contacting the placer about the box. If they don't get back to you, don't go after the box.
Good Luck and I hope we meet on the trail sometime.
Diane
the von der Insels

I'm sorry to hear that CPAScott's website, with the cool name huntingtupperware.com, has gone down. Hopefully he or one of his friends will pick up on this note and the clues will get back online.

But this brings up another topic: automatic letterbox clue backups. When we post our clues to letterboxing.org, we have the option to archive the clues at http://groups.yahoo.com/group/lbox-announce/

I think that is a great place to store our backups. But if we only put a link to our personal website in the clues then the lbox-announce archive won't help a bit when our websites go down.

So I suggest that we boxers who keep clues on personal websites also cut and paste the actual clues into the letterboxing.org Add A Box page. Then our text will be stored both at LbNA and on the yahoogroup archive. Poof! Free backups! This helped Margaret and I quite a bit earlier this year when our own website went down unexpectedly.

Just a suggestion, Jay in CT
