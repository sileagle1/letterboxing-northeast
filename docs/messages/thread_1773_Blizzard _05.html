Blizzard '05

From: Erin Boucher | Date: 2005-01-23 18:06:11 UTC
Well, seeing as how Rhode Island and Massachusetts have declared states of
emergency due to what they're calling Blizzard '05, I would say that the
same would be true for letterboxes in the New England area as well. When all
of this snow is over, most letterboxes in the area will be under at least a
foot of snow. That means that, when spring thaw finally arrives, there will
be LOTS of water and any boxes in low-lying areas or any that are close to
streams/rivers/etc. will be in danger of being lost. Any damaged and/or
non-waterproof boxes will likely get soaked right through. Also, the winds
are quite strong, (sometimes reaching tropical storm strength!) so trees are
likely to be blown around a bit resulting in lots of fallen branches. All of
this chaos may result in damaged or lost boxes so I personally will be
contacting every placer of boxes that I find/can't find for the next few
weeks to let them know what kind of shape their boxes are in, for better or
for worse. I think that if everyone who ventures out in the next few weeks
contacts the placer to let them know how their box is doing one way or the
other, there will be a lot less confusion when spring arrives. I mean,
haven't we all followed the clues to a box only to find that it was missing
but not reported as such on the LbNA website? Rather than let that happen,
we should band together and make sure that placers know the condition of
their boxes even if they are not able to check on them themselves. Better
communication equals less confusion, right?

Keep warm and keep boxing,
Mystique
