A Walk In The Woods Can Help Ledyard
To The Editor Of The Day:

This summer, there has been attention given to Al Gore's
documentary, "An Inconvenient Truth," which concerns looming
environmental catastrophes. Whether one is confronted by such
disturbing issues at the cinema or closer to home while perhaps
driving down a once-shady roadway now devoid of overhead canopies,
classic roadside stonewalls gone, and new construction as far as the
eye can see, I find it easier to deal with in "small doses." You've
heard the phrase: "Think globally, act locally."
Wish you could be involved in the growth of your community but don't
have time to commit to a municipal commission? Wish you really had a
voice and a chance to use it efficiently? Wish you could be at the
front end of the process, perhaps involving such things as local
land-use proposals, instead of hearing about them after the
decisions have been made?

Take heart. Whether you cherish trees, prefer pavement or are a
consumer in need of clean drinking water, here is a chance to get
involved. Take a walk in Ledyard Â— free. Join the Inland Wetlands
Commission at 6 p.m. Aug 8 or the Planning Commission at 6:30 p.m.
Aug. 16. Meet at the end of Fawn Drive, off Whalehead Road. Once you
see and hear about this land-use proposal within the watershed of a
public water supply, you can forget about it or ask questions, voice
concerns, or sing the praises of the proposal at public hearings on
Aug. 15 and Aug 17. Discussions are encouraged and sometimes
decisions are made by those who show up.

If you enjoy the process, you might want to continue to find ways to
participate in your community, become engaged in "realistic
remedies" and benefit the entire community.

Editor's note: The writer is a member of Ledyard's Conservation
Commission.



Anne T. Roberts-Pierson
Gales Ferry
