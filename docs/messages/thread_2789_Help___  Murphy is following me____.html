From: Warrior Woman | Date: 2006-03-29 04:13:31 UTC
Oh, good! Mr. Murphy has moved on!

My 12 year old son (Dune Dude) had a half day off from school about a
week ago, so we decided to go out and grab a box or two. This is a
weekday (Wednesday I think), during the day, around 12 noon. We head
to the Bellingham/Franklin area.

We went to find Sand Dollars and Sense (which, ironically for you,
had a HH in it). It looks like practically a drive-by, in the middle
of a commercial/residential area.

Now this location is a nice spot, not a hunting area at all. But do
I trust that everyone follows the rules about hunting? No way. I
don my blaze orange, and make my son do the same. Don't we look
ridiculous walking around a fishing pond with blaze orange on!

We find the box, stamp in, start chasing each other around the field -
and gunfire erupts. Having had a lot of firearms training, I *know*
this is not hunting rifle fire. This is someone shooting a semi-
automatic at a target.

Fortunately, my son also knows the rules about firearms. He drops to
the ground (as did I). The semi keeps firing in bursts. During a
lull, I screamed out "CEASE FIRE! CEASE FIRE!". Fortunately,
whoever was doing target practice in their back yard with a Glock (or
whatever) heard me and stopped firing.

Needless to say, this scared the living s**t out of my son, who now
REFUSES to go letterboxing with me, after being my trusty companion
for two years now. I can't say I blame him, and now I am a little
skittish myself.

It was a Murphy's law day for us - BIG time!

However ....

I'm looking forward to finding your next Millis box (I think I have
the first four already) - whenever you *do* find your clues and post
them.

And you bet I'll be wearing blaze orange, too.

Warrior Woman
