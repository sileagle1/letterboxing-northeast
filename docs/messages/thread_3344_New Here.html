From: zess devine | Date: 2006-11-28 06:30:19 UTC
Hi Carrie,

I live just outside of Worcester, and just started LBing 3 months ago with
my husband & two children (ages "just turned 4" &" 5-1/2"). I'd love to swap
tips off-list for "good" & "bad" hunts for kids in this age group. I thought
I'd first respond on the list in case there are others nearby who might also
be interested in this kind of info sharing for our area.

I will say that among our first trips out were two "the lazy letterboxer"
series in Waushacum Ponds Rail Trail in Sterling, MA (Dave's Faves and Build
your own Burger), and I recommend both.

First, (and these are in no particular order) it is a Rail Trail, which
allows for taking along a wagon or stroller, in case your daughter gets
tired. The first time I had to hike any distance carrying my 35 lb son, I
started to attend to this option, unless I knew it was a short hike. I also
tend to take water & snacks & a few other kid things (e.g., a change of
clothes has been needed) in addition to the usual LB stuff & camera, so a
wagon is handy.

Second, there is a lot of "bang for your buck" since these two series are
both multiple boxes (6 boxes and 7 boxes). This is nice b/c if you cannot
find a box or two, no big deal. Also, my kids like to take turns with the
"find," so I lean toward plants with at least 2 boxes.

Third, the clues are easy for the kids to follow (which is the coolest kind
of LB for them) with perhaps a bit of subtle guidance from the helpless
adults who can "never" find those Tupperware boxes anywhere!

Fourth, they are FUN, hand-carved stamps for the kids to find...engaging for
them in a way that some of the more abstract adult-oriented stamps are not.

Fifth, it is a beautiful reservoir with a fun little bridge that just calls
to be played, as if an instrument, via a variety of stomping techniques.

Sixth, there is no hunting allowed.

All in all, they were definitely fun times! We did them in two separate
trips.

In addition to these family outings, I try to go at least once a week by
myself to explore some new areas. This is part "therapy," part adventure,
sometimes scouting (for good places to take the kids later), and all
wonderful. In these journeys I have found a few local LBs that I really
enjoyed, but I would definitely NOT recommend when you are out with the
young-uns.

We sometimes call them "treasure hunts" in addition to "letterboxing," b/c
what is cooler for a kid than a treasure hunt? However, I also think it is
really fun to try to incorporate some learning opportunities into the hikes.
Not too much, or they can't take it all in & will tune me out. I am repetive
as much as possible. For example, teaching them the words "trail blaze" and
having them search for them. Then adding that these help us stay on the
trail and know where to go next. And using the terminology & activity on
each hike that has trail blazes. Also about what a reservoir is, or the
difference between oak leaves and maple leaves, or what is "bushwhacking"
and why do we not want to do this, or anything else we come across that I
have a clue about. And I'll ask them about what we've talked about on other
journeys, e.g., "Who can find the kiosk? What is a kiosk for?" I'm starting
with some very basic instruction on using a compass. I do these kinds of
things b/c I don't want it to be just about finding the "treasure." I want
them to engage in the place, to enjoy the walks for what else they hold
(kids LOVE to learn new things), and, most especially, to try to nurture a
sense of stewardship toward the land.

One of my first "mistakes" was to try to "squeeze" an LB trip into a certain
time-frame with my 5-year-old. I thought one hour was plenty of time, but I
didn't count on the 20 minutes she took to enjoy playing with milkweed. I
actually had to move her along to stand a chance of finding the box,
thinking she'd be upset if we didn't find it. In the end, the box was
missing. She was unphased--she just wanted to get back to the field of
milkweed to play with it some more! Ding-ding-ding went the bells in my
head...lesson learned from daughter.

I'd love to hear about the LBs you've done & your thoughts about them.
Please feel free to contact me off-list to exchange info re: Worcester
County kid-letterboxing.

~zess~
