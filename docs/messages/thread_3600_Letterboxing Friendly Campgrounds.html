I camp by boat. My favorite camp sites are on Selden Island (or
Selden Neck as the state calls it). You will need a boat or canoe to
get out there. The camp sites are rustic, the only faccilities are
outhouses. There are a couple letterboxes and a bunch of geocashes
out there. Call 860-526-2336 (Gelette's Castle) to reserve a site.
The cost is $4 per person per night. You can only reserve the camp
site for one night (unless you give them a differance name for each
night).

Re: Letterboxing Friendly Campgrounds

From: swampyankeesunshine | Date: 2007-08-06 13:34:21 UTC
Sorry, Connecticut in the town of Lyme, across the CT River from the
town of Deep River. On the map of Connecticut find Rt. 148 about
half way between I-95 and Rt. 82. Rt. 148 crosses the CT river via a
ferry. Right next to (north of) the ferry slip is a boat launch.
It's sandy and labeled by the state as "carry in" only, ie, for
canoes and kayaks only but with a 4x4 you can launch a small boat (I
launch my 14' Lund and my 17' Whaler all the time). Selden Neck is
about 3/4 of a mile down stream on the east side of the river and
extends for about another 1-1/2 miles south the west side of the
island is the CT river, the east side is Selden Creek which is only
about 40'or 50' feet wide except for the northeast end which is
Selden Cove which is a couple acres big but very shallow.

From: quinny75@aol.com | Date: 2007-08-08 14:16:18 UTC
Hi we have two favorite campgrounds to share. The very best is Burton
Island State Park on Lake Champlain. You can stay in a tent or a lean-to and can
only get there by boat. There are a fair number of boxes in the Lake
Champlain area and even a few on Burton Island! You can take your own boat (any
size-canoe, rowboat, sailboat, motor boat- we took a 13 foot Boston Whaler) or
there is a ferry that will transport you and your gear.

Another favorite of ours is in the White Mountains (NH)-Crawford Notch
Campground and General Store is close to all of the mountains and trails in the
White Mtn. National Forest, near North Conway and tons of boxes! We go up
every year for Columbus Day weekend and have barely put a dent in our pile of
letterboxing clues. Great location, something for everyone from kids to adults
and hikes for all ability levels.

Hope these suggestions help!

Melissa, Dan & Max

Thank you Queen B for the fun hunt! Although we only occassionally get

fair itself. As a added bonus, being "locals" to the area, we were
able to hop over to Donutz' tag sale and purchase a few goodies as well
as stamp her special event stamp.
