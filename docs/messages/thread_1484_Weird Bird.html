From: birder579 | Date: 2004-10-24 13:09:04 UTC
Yes it looked like a ruffed grouse to me also. Katie and I saw it
when we met at that box. These birds can be very territorial. I
saw one at Devil's Den that walked all around me, and pecked at my
shoe. I guess it was trying to drive me away.

Usually if you walk near them they will fly away in a startling
burst of energy, enough to scare you.
The Bird Stamper

Re: Weird Bird

From: birder579 | Date: 2004-10-26 23:08:35 UTC
Most of the time birds fly for their lives when people come near.
That's why birders need to use binoculars to see them. In both
these cases, however, the Ruffed Grouse walked up to within a few
feet of me as I was sitting on the ground.

The only other bird who is so bold is the Chickadee. I have had
Black-capped Chickadees fly to my hand looking for seeds. Groups of
them will perch on nearby branches when a person comes close. They
are inquisitive.
The Bird Stamper
