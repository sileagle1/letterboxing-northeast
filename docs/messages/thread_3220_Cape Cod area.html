From: doublesaj@hughes.net | Date: 2006-09-23 18:22:47 UTC
I know we're an odd choice to recommend boxes in Cape Cod because we live in
California but we just returned from a lovely vacation out there and have a few boxes we think you should not miss.

"Rocky's Box" in Yarmouthport is very sweet and TeamNewHampshire carves wonderful stamps. We were sorry we didn't have more time to find their boxes. "Peace Day", and "A Very Gorey Letterbox" are highly recommended for their intricate carvings as are other boxes by Aschimedes Screw. (We saw the images from her "Alice in Orleans" series and marveled but did not have time to go for them, sadly). There are other boxes in the same area as the "Gorey" box so look for their clues too. "Crafter's Landing" (in store) is another good one especially if you need supplies or are a rubber stamper!

We did get one of the baseball series (CCBL West Box #1) too.

Have a lovely trip!

From: cpascott | Date: 2006-09-28 17:14:46 UTC
The CCBL series is still out there as far as I know. It belongs to
Cape Cod Letterboxer, and there's a bonus once you collect all the
stamps. They are very well carved.

The gathering on the Cape is this weekend, so I can tell you what
some good boxes are in the area around the gathering. I'm hoping to
pluck as many of the Yarmouth, East Dennis, and Barnstable boxes as
possible, but with a gathering in the middle of the day, I don't
know how many I'll get. My printout sheet shows nearly 90 boxes in
the town of Yarmouth alone, including mysteries. Yowzers! Cape Cod
is becoming the next Connecticut!

Apparently, no trip to the Cape is complete with hunting
the "Phantom" ...

"Clue" is another big one.

Best,
CPAScott

From: archimedesscrew17 | Date: 2006-09-28 22:28:02 UTC
Oh, I missed this post earlier. Actually, the Cape Cod Baseball
League series was a collaboration between Cape Cod Letterboxer and
Mumma and Bunny Boy. I carved the bonus box. They are still out
there, but do require a lot of driving between boxes. For less
driving and more boxing, come to Yarmouth. It is where a bunch of
letterboxers live and have been planting. We're pretty good about
updating our clues, so if they are listed as available they probably are.

Enjoy your time on the Cape.

-Screw
