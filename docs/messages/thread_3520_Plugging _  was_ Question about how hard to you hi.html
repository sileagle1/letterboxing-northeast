Plugging : was: Question about how hard to you hide boxes

From: vitlaur@aol.com | Date: 2007-06-08 20:36:53 UTC
Hi folks,

I have planted a few of the plugged in boxes that Mary has written about. I
just hollow out a spot in the pine needles with enough room for the box, plug
it in and cap it with a marking stone. No digging required. I have had
several boxes in place this way for over 5 years. They have fared more or less the
same as my other boxes.

I went out to check on one the other day and found that a newt had taken up
residence under the box.

Several well established, well known boxers in my area have left boxes this
way and it has worked quite well. If you write the clues well and mark the
final spot, it is not an issue. I do not know of any letterboxes in this area
that were buried. It is my understanding that sometimes geocaches are buried.

All said, I do prefer to to tuck my boxes under overhanging rocks, because I
feel that this gives the most protection.

On on,
Laurette
