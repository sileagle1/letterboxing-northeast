Only three people have the authority to delete a given post: The
person who made it, or a moderator (of which there are two--
TeamGreenDragon and myself). Moderators also have the special
priveledge of looking up who deleted a given post, and I can tell
you this:

Posts 934, 935, 942, 943, and 946 were deleted by the person that
posted them. I don't know why they deleted them or if they'd rather
stay annonyous so nobody pesters them about why they deleted their
posts, so I'm not saying anything about who the posts belonged to.
(Except you, Phyto, since I thought it highly amusing that you were
confused when one of the posts in question was your own, and you--or
someone using your account--deleted it. *smile*)

Which, I might add, people deleting their own posts is remarkably
common. Far more posts get deleted by the person who posted them
than by moderators. (In fact, as far as I know, only those last
three posts mentioned were EVER deleted by a moderator on this talk
list.)

The last three posts were deleted by TeamGreenDragon, presumably
because he considered them spam, in bad taste, vulgar, or whatever.
I didn't see the posts before they were deleted and don't know the
contents. (Even the all-powerful moderators can't resurrect a
deleted post, proving once and for all that there is no such thing
as an 'all-powerful' moderator. *wink*)

But--here's the thing--some people have e-mails sent for every post
to this talk list, and an e-mail cannot be recalled. So even though
the post will no longer show up on the talk list, it is still
resting in some people's e-mail box, and they'll reply to the post
not realizing that the post they're replying to has already been
deleted and anyone not getting the e-mails will be highly confused
about what is being replied to.

So in a nutshell, there's a new Letterboxing Connecticut group that
someone was promoting, but the posts relating to it were deleted.
(Knowing Brian, I'm sure it was for a good reason, even if I don't
know what it is.)

And there's been no mass-deletion of lots of posts by the
moderators. Just three. Which, out of nearly 1,000 posts, isn't
too bad at all!

Speaking of which, WOW! Almost a thousand posts and 150 members on
this board! You guys rock! =) You've all done a great job making
this a successful and terrific talk list that requires *almost* no
intervention on the part moderators. Thanks for all your efforts!

From: Steve and Heidi | Date: 2004-03-25 12:58:25 UTC
Interesting comment. We actually PREFER digest form. Frankly, with
the high traffic of the big list it became a pain in the rear to wade
through all the "stuff" that got posted and the "re:re:re:re:re:"
posts. Email 'digest' simply gives you a once-a-day listing of what
gets slung. Much easier to scroll down and selectively view the
topics and comments. And digest form preserves all the posts before
they get deleted too!

The individual e-mail function would likely cause a major mailbox jam
given the traffic of the lists also. We reserve it for lists where
you might get only 1 or 2 messages a day, such as my National
Collegiate EMS list.
