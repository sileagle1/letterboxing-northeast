I know that many boxers in our area enjoy making their own log books,
many using Coptic Stitch to sew them together.I came across the
following workshop in New Haven, CT that I thought some of you might
be interested in.

On Sunday, February 3,from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m., the Creative Arts
Workshop is hosting a class called "Coptic Sewing with Drum Boards" at
it's facility at 80 Audubon Street in New Haven. The cost for
non-members is $85. I signed up but do not know yet if there are any
additional charges for materials. The instructor will send additional
information after sign-up. Basic tools such as bone folder, scissors,
etc., are probably requried.

Here is the course description:
"Coptic sewing is a chain-like stitch that is exposed on the spine of
a book. It is very versatile and can be used for journals, sketchbooks
and artists books. In this workshop, students will learn one type of
Coptic sewing. First we will practice by sewing signatures of a soft
cover exhibition catalogue. Then we will create a small hardcover
blank book, with drum board covers, which lies perfectly flat when
open. In addition, we will review other possibilities for these sewing
and board techniques, including their use in certain book repairs. A
list of tools and materials needed will be sent to students upon
registration. Previous handcraft experience is recommended.
Instructor: Susan Klein studied bookbinding at CAW and Center for Book
Arts in NY. She worked as a bookbinder in the Preservation Department
of Yale University Library for almost ten years, repairing books in
the circulating collections. She has also studied and taught weaving,
knitting, drawing, printmaking, and origami, and has exhibited at CAW
and other craft galleries in CT and NY. Cost: $85; members: $77"

You may view this and other courses
at:http://www.creativeartsworkshop.org/html/classes.html

Hope to see some boxer-bookmakers there! Might be a lovely way to
while away a cold winter Sunday and chase the doldrums away.

Alafair
