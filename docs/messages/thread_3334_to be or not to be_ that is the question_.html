Ironically, I went for this box yesterday; I had no idea of the park¹s
reputation and didn¹t know about the article in the newspaper. I showed up
alone mid-afternoon and noticed about eight cars parked there, half of which
had men sitting in them. I noticed more men wandering around the paths,
figured out what was going on, and felt uncomfortable being there, like I
was intruding on an event I wasn¹t invited to (and didn¹t want to attend).

and thought perhaps I¹d walk in from the other direction and try not to
bother anyone. As I made my way toward the trail two different men took
notice of me and began heading my way. Not wanting any kind of
confrontation, I headed back to my car and gave up.

kids out to find this box. Frankly, I don¹t care what these guys do there,
but I don¹t want to be involved, and I certainly wouldn¹t want my kids
seeing any of it. I just don¹t think a known ³anonymous sex park² is a place
many people, especially families, will feel comfortable letterboxing.

From: alwayschaos | Date: 2006-11-21 23:14:54 UTC
When I first moved to my area I had a similar park--gorgeous, with
great trails. Saw lots of people running, walking their dogs, etc.
located behind a nursing home. Planted a small series there.

Little did I know that not only did this park have a draw for people
to engage in anonymous sex, it also drew the druggies and people with
nothing better to do than create mischief and mayhem.

My boxes were quickly muggled (you needed to have the clues to know
where they were- they weren't visible from the trail). The stamps
were stolen, boxes filled with leaves or thrown aside.

At one box I found a bag with a pair of jeans in it, a newspaper, a
tag from a dog and a picture of a guy showing what he was most proud
of (if you get my drift). Now my husband didn't believe me, but Miss
Prisspott found the same thing earlier.

I've also found used condoms and "toys" (ahem), along with drug
paraphenalia. At that point it's becoming a public health issue and
has nothing to do with people's personal sexual choices which have
never been my concern nor have I had the least interest in.

I pulled the boxes, pulled the clues.

Another new letterboxing family in my area planted a box in the same
park recently. After a short period it was "adopted" by a group of
kids who apparently gather there to do drugs. This box is well off
the trail and well hidden. You wouldn't know it was there without the
clues.

Frankly, there is too much of an opportunity at this point of coming
upon something I don't care to see in this park, so I no longer
visit. Too bad, it could be a great park.

I'd pull them if I were you. It's just to risky for someone to come
upon
