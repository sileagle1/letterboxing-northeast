Journey Into the Imagination Series Update

From: Amy | Date: 2004-10-04 22:13:27 UTC
"Journey Into the Imagination" Series at Shenipsit SF, Stafford and
Somers, CT.
October 4rth.
Update by Hawkwind and Mienu:
"Where the Sidewalk Ends" and "Berenstain Bears" will need new log
books soon.
Poor Pinocchio was left out in the open without his lid! His logbook
was completely soaked and ruined. We have it and will try to replace
it in a couple weeks.
(I will be in Maine for the weekend, so I am not sure when I can get
back to replace the log book.)
We found the "Witch Craft" H.H.(left by the Bigelow Hollow Staff) at
the "Very Hungry Caterpillar" After some coaxing on "Witch Crafts"
part Hawkwind was convinced to bring her along on one of her trips
north or northwest.
"The Lorax" is in need of new Marvy Markers. We found "The Bunny Hop"
H.H. at "Is Your Momma a Llama?" and after realizing that she has been
waiting patiently for quiet a while for a lift Hawkwind and Mienu
remembered a lovely spot in the north east that she will like. She is
safely residing with Hawkwind until her move in the near future.
Poor Eeyore from "Winnie the Pooh!" The forestry workers have been all
around his house! But he is safe and so is his house! Poor "Misty" on
the other hand has had part of her trail along the lookout wash out
and the going is very tricky, but she is still resting safely with the
rest of the ponies.
"Where The Wild Things Are" will need a new green stamp and there is a
large tree across the trail on the way to our visit with "Ferdinand",
but he is also safe. We enjoyed our "Green Eggs and Ham" and were
very tickled to find "Horton Hears a Who" or actually he heard us!
"Stega Nona" has also had some forestry work going on in her area and
other than the need for an orange ink pad she is safe and happy.
We were absolutely taken with "The Cheshire Cat", he had the most
wonderful logbook we have ever seen!
Poor, poor "Alexander and the Horrible, Terrible Rotten Day"! His area
has been trampled a bit by the workers also and the tree with 4 blue
spots on the way to "The Secret Garden" is missing, again a causualty
of the forest workers.
The lamp post to "The Lion, The Witch and The Wardrobe" was safe and
sound and burning brightly.It was rather strange to see a "Fish Out of
Water"..but he was happy as could be so we wandered on to visit "Mrs
Rumphius" and her beautiful flowers. There has also been some forestry
work done in her area but she is also safe. Lastly we stopped to rest
with "The Nights of The Round Table" the night on guard will need to
have his armour polished with some new gold ink, but he is doing well.
(Will be sending out a SAS postcard to the guardian of the box.)
We replaced quite a few bags that were worn out as well as a couple
'blotters'. There is a need for new log books soon as well as more
bags and ink pads.
All in all a wonderful day of LBing!
