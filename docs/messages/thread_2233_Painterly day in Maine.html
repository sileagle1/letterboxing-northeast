Painterly day in Maine

From: Phyto | Date: 2005-07-18 10:45:21 UTC
Phyto and Isosceles endured thousands of mosquitoes (literally) at the
Laudholm Painterly Day at the Wells Reserve on Sunday. Kudos to
Oceanwytch and Maine Kokopellian who were the only ones able to find
us, get a one-day-only stamp and....clues to the "We be Jammin'"
series of boxes!

On our way through the Southern Maine area, we checked on the original
Secret Garden box.
http://home.gwi.net/~moxie1/Clues/SecretGardenMystery.html
This box is still alive and secluded from the hustle and bustle of
busy summer tourists. A special bonus awaits those that visit this
oasis...

Finally, on the way back home after a day of painting on the trail, we
checked on some seashore boxes. Glad to see some folks like Sadie and
Russ, Butterfly, Nautilus, Culex and others have managed to find these
little ocean jems. These are wonderful spots for a lazy summer drive
and seashore exploration.

We also checked on "Higher Education"....in place since July 16th of
2003. Every summer since the placement of this box, Phyto has taught
the coastal marine ecology course at UNE. This box is in fantastic
shape and so many visitors from alumni at the University to locals...

Hope to see some friendly faces in September!
Phyto

report from Penwood & Northwest Parks

From: Silent Doug | Date: 2005-07-18 16:58:21 UTC
Clueless and I braved the rain, humidity and mosquitos yesterday to
make a sweep through Northwest Park in Windsor. We think we found all
the active boxes there (except for the box in the museum, which was
closed). Most were in good shape, though we did replace baggies in a
couple of boxes. Here's what we found:

* Inland Lobster
* Celestial Orbs (2)
* America's Pride
* Slow Poke
* Northwest Park
* My Favorite Stinky Thing
* Northwest Park Series (4)
* Hooded Merganser (*excellent* stamp by Team Green Dragon!)
* The Birds (7)
* Perfect Sunday Afternoon (3)

Earlier in the day, we logged The Gondoliers in Gilbert & Sullivan #3
in Simsbury (it was completely iced in when we found the rest of the
series in March, so we had to pay a second visit to the woods here).
We picked up The World's Stinkiest Hitchhiker and found two other
bonuses boxes, too, so I think we ended up with 26 finds for the day.

Last Sunday, we made a sweep of Penwood State Park, and found all the
boxes in that park, too. With bonuses, we logged 19 in total for the day:
* Circle of Lives (4)
* Is It? (2)
* Penwood State Park
* Lake Louise
* Measure Up
* Whatt? & Whoo?
* Sneaky, Beaky & Leaky
* Valley Girls

Thanks to rubaduc, rtrw and everyone else who planted boxes in these parks!

SD
