a problem.

In my most humble opinion: Most psychologically sound people don't have a problem with x-posting, and if it does irritate them for whatever reason, they have figured out how to use the delete key or ignore it. Normal people deal without making it a big freakin' issue _to everyone else_ who just wants to find out about all things letterboxing. But some posters apologize because they know there are snipers out there... and who knows who will catch the bullet. Will it be them who get a barrage, or some poor soul who makes a reply...

I'll weigh in with, "It has its place, and it doesn't bother me, but I hate to cause anyone bad feelings if they feel they have been ambushed because they are part the thread that gets attacked because I x-posted it."

It didn't used to be that way on LbNA. It used to be a kinder, gentler place about, oh, 3 years or more ago, I'd guess. Let's see... I started reading on the cusp of 2000-2001... posting more 2002-2003... started getting suckier... hey, wait a minute... <s>

Which came first, the chicken or the egg? Did anyone get real cheesed before the splintering? Did the groups splinter off more because of attitude or region or "specialty"? Enquiring minds want to know.

Maybe not. It's probably a door I don't want to get blamed for opening.

Please don't change a thing, Alan, except maybe post more when life doesn't interfere with letterboxing. You say you crosspost occasionally because you want to reach a wider LBing audience. If your post could be of interest to even _one_ person reading a group, why should anyone get nuts that you posted to that group? It's not like you are selling Cialis. Others may totally disagree. I can't wait to read their reasons. If anyone goes purple on you, just keep repeating, "it's them, not me..." or "it's her fault, she encouraged me..."

Actually, I always post the apology 'cause back when I was
crossposting about our first WNY gathering, I got chewed out for it
by folks on and offlist. But really, it's become a necessity. On the
big board, my messages get lost in the fighting or, worse yet, don't
get seen because people like me are AVOIDING the fighting (and those
are the folks I WANT at our gatherings!).

So, I basically crosspost any major Western NY boxing stuff (usually
gatherings and calls for donations) to the Great Lakes, the Mid-
Atlantic, the CNY, wth WNY and this board. We've had visitors from
all areas at our gatherings, so I know the bulk of you are within
driving distance (AND you don't fight!).

Seriously, if I'm bugging anyone, I apologize, but I'm just hoping to
meet and greet new boxers to our area any way I can.
