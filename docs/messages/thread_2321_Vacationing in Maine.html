I love to do mystery boxes but when you are not local to the area a
small mystery often becomes a huge one! I've planted a couple myself
that were not difficult for locals but for folks from out of
state/province they were very diffcult. I've looked at a few of the
Maine mystery boxes and hope to try a few next week when I visit - if
I have time to do all the research needed before we hit the road!
When your holiday time is limited one tends to pick the boxes you
think you can successfully find in the time you have available, print
those clues out and take them along with you. Sometimes I travel with
my laptop, sometimes not. But if I haven't solved the mystery - at
least the starting location - before we leave home - I will definitely
not be able to find the box. I have decided to have all of my future
mystery boxes include a identified starting point or a very very easy
clue to the start. After that the clues can be more cryptic and for
everyone, local and visiting, it's pretty much a level playing field.
Not sure if this still counts as a mystery box but the mystery can be
any part of the clue/s I think...?
Norasta

From: J. BRIDGMAN | Date: 2005-09-01 01:01:23 UTC
This must be the time of year to visit Maine. We were also there
last week. At Laudholm Farms, we needed two boxs, one of which was
Otter in the Pines. When we saw that the trail was posted as closed,
we were disappointed at first. A tractor operator happened to be
driving into the construction site (which is the reason the trail is
closed) and said he didn't mind if we walked the trail. And so we
did, sucessfully finding the box. We also noticed by studying the
trail map that the box could be reached by another route. In fact we
returned using the alternate way.

Other boxes we visited include:
Fiddlehead
The Hound - Sherlock Holmes #2
Cooks Brook
Mourning Dove
Box of Rocks
Togue
Picket Fence
Snow Falls Snowflake
Porcupine Den
Transportation HH Hostel
Majestic Moose
Harbor Lights
Keep in Touch 1,2 &3
Waddle We Do Without You
EPSF
Clean Water for All
And Justice for All
First Do No Harm

We returned home and found that paplela had put out several more
boxes in the York area which we had not seen before we left. What a
busy placer she is!

Hitch hikers encountered:
Golden Slipper in Worldwide ME - Athens
Itty Bitty Tear in The Hound in Saco
M.S. Escher at the HH Hostel in Wells
New Jersey - handed to me by CT TrAAveler

Did not find:
The Way Life Should Be in Kittery. Pretty sure we were in the right
place, however.

We also found time to play in the ocean at York Beach where the water
was almost WARM two days in a row and enjoy a family picnic.

Now it is back to the scheduled life.
