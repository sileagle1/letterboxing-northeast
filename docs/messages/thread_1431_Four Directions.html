From: T Prindle | Date: 2004-10-10 23:07:45 UTC
Thanks to everyone who've made The Four Directions series possible.
We'd like to thank Koyote who conceived the idea and the locations
along the Mohawk Trail., and introducing someone who's grown up in
that area to new incredible places. Thanks to the entirty of
TheEndGang for their patience in getting this series off the
ground! This part of Connecticut, a portion of the old AT, exhibits
such rugged beauty and floral uniqueness that it's simply not to be
overlooked. Nearly every section of the trail offers some breath
taking vistas, seasonal fog permitting! I'd stress to take your
time with this series, and even venture further down these trails
with time permitting... PowWow Dancer and I are planning to do the
Coltsfoot Mountain section this november.

The gathering for the release of this series was just perfect, and we
thank all those that attended, and hope those that could not attend
will explore the series at their leisure down the road. A big
thankyou to Mark for doing outstanding organizing, website work and
graphics layout for Koyote and us. It was a great gather to chat and
hike together and learn a little more about what everyone's interests
are. I thourougly enjoyed my time with Butterfly and I hope we can
get out and box and talk plants and trees together sometime! I
have to thank Sojourner for all the time contributed, not only in
helping with planting, but also with the last minute under pressure
stamp carving requests, your carvings are excuisite. (We really
liked Molly!). We cant believe Phynstar and Rubaduc did two
sections!! WOW power boxers!! You go girls!! *smile. We hope we can
keep up with you guys! Rubaduc you missed the event stamp log!! (Can
I stamp you in with our upcoming new carving?) We really enjoyed
our conversations again with Wanda and Pete, and hope the old AT
brings back some good memmories too. Finally, but certainly not
lastly, we enjoyed doing Koyote's Chief Joseph box with Sadie and
Russ. I *have to thank my sister the LeafLady for her support and
all around sister-stuff. (She thanks Music Woman for finding her
compas).

The frybread actually worked!! The dough was pure 'Cherokee gold' if
I might coin a phrase. Contributed by longtime friends Mikki
Aganstada (Eastern Band Cherokee) and Sherman Paul (Maliseet) who've
been cooking frybread as Native American Cuisine for some 25
years... We were smiled upon and my coleman stove reached the
perfect 350 - 375 degree frying temperature and the rest was Frybread
Power history!! What better way to send people off to the Four
Directions! Thank you guys for enjoying my little tokens of
appreciation for your support!

Rush Gatherer and PowWow Dancer
The Dawnlanders
(F400 at the very end of TFD gather!)
