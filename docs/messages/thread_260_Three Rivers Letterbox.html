From: J. BRIDGMAN | Date: 2004-02-22 21:54:57 UTC
Had a great day Saturday, hunting for boxes in SE CT.
Wanted to comment about box #1 on the Heritage Walk in
Norwich. We found the box, but it was frozen in the
ground. Only had a trowel to dig with so did not
succeed in removing it. It was placed in it's
hidyspot upside down. If it had been right side up we
probably could have gotten the lid off and accessed
the contents. So we'll just have to try that walk
again when the ground has thawed. ;-) If anyone is
going for that one, take a strong ice pick or ice axe
or something! Nice walk anyhow.

In 2 days we found the following in good shape:
Hurricane, Fish Tale, Happy Musical Note, Jordan Mill,
Sara's First Birthday, Letter from a Traitor,
Captain's Walk, Thames River Lights (except for #1),
Avery Light, Olde Mystic Village, Big Sugar Maple,
Patriot, Indian and Fountain (except for #1) with #'s
2-4 in need of attention but didn't have replacement
books. Also found 4 of the 6 in the gazebo series.
No. 1 had only a nickel in the box and #6 seemed to be
missing.

Great cure for cabin fever!
