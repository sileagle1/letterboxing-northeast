From: Chuck and Laura Lubelczyk | Date: 2004-03-03 04:02:42 UTC
Hello all-
Forgive my silence on the subject of accommodations for the upcoming
gathering that Phyto and I are hosting, but I've been away for work
(hence having to miss the POE gathering). I have a few thoughts/comments
on accommodations.
First, the deal Mark has found sounds great. I've never been there
myself, but it sounds nice. It is however, not one, but two exits away
from Wells which is exit 2. It's still probably only about 25 minutes
away though.
Second, as some of you have discovered, many of the Wells, Kennebunk and
Ogunquit hotels are closed until April or May. There are a few I know of
that are open. Beach Farm Inn is a nice place that includes breakfast. I
think you can get a room for around $70. Juniper Hill Inn in Ogunquit is
also nice and I believe the rooms start at $60. Village By the Sea is
very posh. They have one or two bedroom suites for 4 people that range
from $75 to $115. I think in the two bedroom ones you can sleep 6 people
b/c of a pullout but there might be an additional charge. There is also
Quinney's Bed and Breakfast which is right down the road and is $65 with
a full breakfast. I don't know if they allow pets, but it's a mini farm
with a feed and seed store, so I'm assuming they do.
Just over the border of Wells in Sanford is the Super 8 motel. The AAA
rate for 2 people is $58 (single is $51). They allow pets, but there is
an additional charge.
I think that most, if not all, of these places are listed on the Wells
Chamber of Commerce website at www.wellschamber.org but if you have any
trouble finding any of them, I'd be happy to send you the phone number,
just contact me off list.
Also, as Phyto mentioned, unfortunately, pets are not allowed on Wells
Reserve property (that includes being left in the car). I would offer up
my house, but it's a half an hour away and I'm afraid my six cats might
be petrified by a dog! I'll check with a friend who lives right down the
road and see if he would mind if we maybe leashed up a dog for a bit at
his place.
Nautilus
