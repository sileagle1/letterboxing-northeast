Questing

From: Team Randalstik | Date: 2006-08-31 19:16:33 UTC
Being members, we have received a couple of mailings from The
Trustees of Reservations. In their most recent "Special Places"
newsletter, they have an article about Questing. Basically a VERY
EDUCATIONAL letterboxing experience. They have a virtual one at:

www.vitalcommunities.org/valleyquest/virtualquest.htm

plus more info about questing, which I have heard of but thought it
was an early version of letterboxing in the US. I guess not.

Anyhoo, in the newsletter is a quest in RAVENSWOOD PARK in
Gloucester, MA; MENEMSHA HILLS RESERVATION, Chilmark, Martha's
Vineyard; THE TRUSTEES OFFICE at 193 High St, Holyoke, MA; and
BARTHOLOMEW'S COBBLE, Sheffield, MA. All the clues are available
either on the bulletin board at a kisosk (Ravenswood, Menemsha), at
the office (Holyoke), or at the visitor's center (Sheffield). The
hikes range from 1.5 hours to 3 hours. At the end I THINK is a hand-
carved stamp.

Also, they have one planned at APPLETON FARMS, Hamilton, MA, (my
GREEN DOGS letterbox is there as well . . . I hope they don't get rid
of it). I don't know when that will be ready.

I'll have to try the 2 hour Gloucester one sometime.

Check out their web site for a bunch of other quests in the VT and NH
areas.

thought you all would like to know . . . if you didn't already . . .
Anybody did any Questing before?

Team Randalstik

Re: [LbNE] Re: Questing

From: John Chapman | Date: 2006-09-03 02:53:20 UTC
Questing is usually a reference to Valley Quest from Vital Communities and is an educational effort to use activities similar to letterboxing to educate people about community history, nature, culture or places. It was started around 1987 after David Sobel discovered letterboxing while on sabbatical in England and decided to use the idea for educational purposes. Their quests are in the Upper Connecticut River Valley region of Vermont and New Hampshire.
http://www.vitalcommunities.org/ValleyQuest/ValleyQuest.htm
