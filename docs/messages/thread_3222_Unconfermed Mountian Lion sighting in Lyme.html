From: Kim Calcagno | Date: 2006-09-26 12:01:14 UTC
One bit of caution on this topic....
There is a guy here in Rhode Island, but he travels around southern NE, who
tries very hard to travel in field biologist circles who has been claiming
for years that there are mountain lions here. In the field people recognize
him as being 'full of baloney' but he does often convince non-professionals
that he is for real and knows what he is talking about. He claims he's seem
them many times and has seen tracks etc...but it a bit funny that no one has
ever been with him during a sighting. He has no photos of the animal or of
the tracks etc.

I don't know if this sighting is associated with this guy, but there is no
confirmed record of mountain lions, and there are very few areas that could
support one here in southern NE due to the sheer acreage of open space they
need to support a single animal's territory.

-Kim (Rustypuff) (wildlife refuge manager here in RI)

RE: [LbNE] Unconfermed Mountian Lion sighting in Lyme

From: hannahkat@cox.net | Date: 2006-09-26 17:51:46 UTC
Cool!

If it's a wild cat, it's likely to be a bobcat, although it would be extremely unusual on the north shore area of MA. In MA they are usually sighted in the Berkshire mountains as a single animal needs an area of approx 6000 acres as a territory. They can take relatively large prey, but generally wouldn't bother with something the size of a human, even a small child.

Lynx are native to extreme northern NH and VT and central to northern Maine (and Canada). If it was a lynx, it would be very far from where it is supposed to be.

Another possibility is the accidental or purposeful release of a big cat like an ocelot or margay or even a cougar that someone was keeping illegally. If someone was keeping a big cat in captivity and it became too much to handle, they may have dumped it. It has happened before.

Mountain lions would be the size of a large dog, say a german shepard, have a very long tail and would have a buff/gray to rusty colored coat. Bobcat are slightly larger than a red fox, have a short 'bobbed' tail and have a buff colored, spotted coat.

In any case, the sighting of any of those cats would be very special, and I am jealous!

;-)
-Kim
