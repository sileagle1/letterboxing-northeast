A Letter to the Lords of Trade
From the Colonial Governor of New York
Dated 8 May 1698

Dear Sirs,

You had tasked my predecessor, Governor Fletcher, to eliminate the Pirates that plague the shores of New England but I find that those Pirates that have been given the greatest disturbance in the East Indies and the Red Sea, have either been fitted out from New York or Rhode Island.

I have heard that there will be a meeting of these villainous outlaws in November in Rhode Island. Our efforts to limit their plundering and hanging those Outlaws that we can apprehend have been bearing Fruit. My spies have informed me that several of these Pirates are planning to rendezvous to recruit more hands.

I will endeavour to gather more information and keep you informed of my Efforts to eradicate this scourge to our Colonies.

Your humble servant,
Richard Coote
Earl of Bellomont
Colonial Governor of New York
