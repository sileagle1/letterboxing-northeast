How or why does a letterbox get into the "Basement"? There's an old box
at Westwoods in Guilford that we found a few days ago... Alchemy Series
#3: Oil and Wax. We were the first to find it in two years. I wrote to
the planter but so far have had no reply. Is it possible to bring it
back to "life"?
Rubaduc

RE: [LbNE] Guilford, CT.

From: Debbie Kotlarek | Date: 2008-08-30 20:29:57 UTC
Usually you can contact the webmasters at the LBNA site. If they can't
contact the placer in a reasonable amount of time, they will let you adopt
it if you want. This is what happened with a box we found in Hawaii several
years ago. The box is now in the active part of letterboxing.org with my
name. All I did was edit the clues to have a prefix to say that I adopted
the box and would be happy to turn it back over to the original planter if
they reappeared. I left the original planter's clues "as is" and noted that
they were the actual creators of the box.

Wisconsin Hiker

From: Debbie Kotlarek | Date: 2008-08-30 22:54:13 UTC
Yeah, I know how you feel. However my Hawaii box has done amazingly well
for 4 years with my very remote "parenting". Unfortunately we haven't been
back there to check on it (I wish!), but have received NUMEROUS emails about
it. People have asked if it needed anything before they took vacation
trips, reported on its status, and when it needed a new box I just noted it
in the clues and shortly after that the next visitors were able to bring a
new box and replaced the logbook. It also has survived a fire and people
sent me a photo of the area after the fire. So... it turns out that I've
really enjoyed having this adopted box so far away!
