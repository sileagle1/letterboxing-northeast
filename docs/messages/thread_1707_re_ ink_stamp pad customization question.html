From: rospa10 | Date: 2004-12-31 02:02:55 UTC
May I offer an alternative suggestion? I now stamp my sig. image onto
plain white labels which can be easily affixed into logbooks while
letterboxing. Sometimes the logbook is too small, so I do bring my
stamp(s) and inkpad so as not to be caught short. This saves time,
plus ensures the letterbox placer gets a good image of my sig. this
tip was passed on to me ages ago. It is especially useful when
attending gatherings. I have not yet heard any complaints about a
stamped label being used v.s. directly samping into the logbook.
Happy winter letterboxing!
Catlover/teacher/gardener

From: May Day Lillies | Date: 2004-12-31 02:51:25 UTC
We do the same thing now for when we are out in the winter. We still carry our stamps around and on a nice day we dont mind staying out a little longer to stamp in. On the cold days it is a big time saver. We even do some verticle and some horizontal depending on how the log book is. A great place to get the stickers is the post office, you get priority mail ones for free there and just cut that part off. You better hury if you want those though, they are changing it so that it says priority mail accross the whole thing, our post office just changed over this week, others weeks ago. All depends on how quickly they use up the old ones.
