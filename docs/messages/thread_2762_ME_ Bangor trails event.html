ME: Bangor trails event

From: Phyto | Date: 2006-03-19 15:08:50 UTC
There will be a meeting at the Bangor Public Library this coming
Wednesday evening about the prospects of creating an interconnected
public trail system similar to the existing Portland Trails system. I
encourage any of you living in the Bangor area to attend this. While
checking on my Prentiss Woods boxes, I noticed a poster announcing
this meeting at the trail kiosk.

For those of you in Southern Maine, you're probably familiar with
Portland Trails and you can thank the initiative of those folks as
well as Saco Trails for their dedicated land acquisition efforts that
make your letterboxing adventures possible. I think this is a strong
community effort to unify the existing trails in this area, and we
will be attending as local voices in the Bangor area. The trail and
land infrastructure already exists up here, it's just a matter of
putting folks in charge. Please make your voice heard! -phyto


Bangor Trails Event

Wednesday, March 22nd at 7:00 pm


The Bangor Land Trust [BLT] and Keep Bangor Beautiful [KBB] will
present "Building a Community Trail System." The speaker, Alix
Hopkins, is one of the founders of Portland's well-known city-wide
trail system, and the author of Groundswell: Stories of Saving Places,
Finding Community. The sponsoring organizations feel that Bangor has
the beginnings of and potential for an outstanding system of trails
for walking, biking and recreation. They are seeking community input,
and Ms. Hopkins' presentation will be followed by discussion, and an
opportunity to contribute ideas.

Bangor Public Library
http://www.bpl.lib.me.us/
