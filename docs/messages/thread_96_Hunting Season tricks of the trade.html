From: Carolyn Stearns | Date: 2003-12-30 02:01:46 UTC
Like most letterboxers the whole hunter thing worries me too! here
are some of my ideas. Well a lot of preserves have boxes and no
hunting that doesn't mean no poaching so I wear orange pretty much
all fall and winter. I think it is goose and black powder season
now. I got my orange sweatshirts and vest at Walmart but they sell
out eary in the season and don't seem to replace when its gone as it
is a short selling season for that. If you are in the vicinity
Danielson Surplus in Danielson , Ct had some in the fall a call may
let you know how the stock is before you drive out or Dicks sporting
goods Buckland Mall in Manchester and there happen to be boxes in
both directions. Then I have two bells on my back pack all through
the fall. I enjoy the jingle as I walk the trail and certainly any
hunter will know that only Santa's deer jingle all the way. Sounds
funny but I believe it helps . The last thing that will help you
would be my son... no really he is so loud in the woods everything in
a mile radius has heard him coming including the hunters who would
know all the wildlife have been scared off a 100 acre area. well
have fun and be safe as always. carol - Leader of the Pack

Here's some links so that you may check out what seasons are
currently open in your area. As a former CT DEP Employee (emergency
dispatcher), I will mention that RI has the right idea with hunting -
if you're going in the woods from October through February, wear your
orange!!!!!

One thing to mention: Hats are better seen than vests when walking
through brushy cover. If you can afford an orange vest and an
orange ball cap, that is ideal. I personally wear a vest and an
orange "fisherman" hat with a wide brim. Heidi wears an orange
ballcap. We have a vest for Maddie and she also has a small cowbell
that you see used for bird dogs usually!

Steve, Heidi, Madras
