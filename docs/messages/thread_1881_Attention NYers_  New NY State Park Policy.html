From: birder579 | Date: 2005-03-18 15:46:31 UTC
I think we have to look on this as a positive development. If we
don't, we risk reenforcing all the bad things that could be said
about letterboxers.

Principally the state is saying that geocaching (and letterboxing
eventually) can be done on public land as long as a series of rules
are followed. We can debate the rules and ask for modifications
that may be applicable to letterboxing that are different from
geocaching, but I have no problems being welcomed to an area as long
as I follow rules. These rules do not have the effect of preventing
geocaching (and eventually letterboxing), unlike the National Park
Service. Having rules like these in place will prevent a problem
with one letterbox from having a backlash against all letterboxers.

Being willing to follow the rules of a park will show that we are
responsible people who want to enjoy the park and not cause any harm
to the environment. This will enhance the image of our communities
with the park managers. If this process of permits succeeds, it can
be used as a model for other lands owned by other organizations.

This is something we need to see succeed.
The Bird Stamper
