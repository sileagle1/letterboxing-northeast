Happy Spring!!

As we all know, warm weather brings our lovely tick friends out to play. If
you are like me, though, this fact may not be foremost on your mind as you
set out for a hike. On this, the first day of Spring, I flicked TWELVE ticks
off my (fortunately light-colored) pants while I was out hiking. Granted, I
was hiking our newest trail, that is flagged but not yet built (and
therefore lots of bushwhacking), but it was truly a wake-up call! Just
thought I'd send out a little reminder.

From: Melissa Ray | Date: 2008-03-21 18:05:18 UTC
Hi all,

My son got Lymes Disease summer before last. We had a hard time
protecting him from insects because he has very sensitive skin, and
even reacted to the natrapel and other "gentle" pesticide
treatments. Finally I found Buzz-Off clothing, which uses a
synthetic version of something in chrysanthamums to repell insects.
My son and I wear our "letterboxing clothes" every time we go out,
and never get bitten my mosquitos or followed home by ticks. My
husband thought the clothing was silly, until the day we all went out
(him wearing his major DDT containing substance) - he got bitten and
we didn't. It's a great relief not to worry about the ticks any more.

Too bad they don't make socks for dogs!

Red Cloak
