Common Sense for hitch hikers at Gatherings?

From: cavy_lovers4 | Date: 2004-09-22 13:28:53 UTC
Hi all,
Just want to suggest that folks apply common sense before
bringing a HH to a gathering -- if the logbook is likely to be
filled up by the gathering -- maybe it should be planted at the
gathering instead of put on a table.

Of course this suggestion reflects my personal bias. I have tried
to keep our HH packages/boxes small so they more easily fit into a
host box. This usually meant a small logbook. I would like my HHs
to travel to several boxes before getting a full logbook.

For the Monster Mash I would also like to suggest (not dictate)
that folks consider putting the HH in one of the many boxes set out
for the event. That way lots of people can find them and perhaps the
log will collect not just several personal stamps, but several
stamps for the MM boxes as well -- that would be a treat for the
person who placed the HH!

Just my thoughts - ignore if they upset you!
Cavy Lovers

Re: Common Sense for hitch hikers at Gatherings?

From: Steve and Heidi | Date: 2004-09-23 00:40:58 UTC
Just an opinion on all this hitchhiker at gathering debate.

A couple years ago when only 40 people came to a gathering and it
could be discretely left out for others to happen upon, well, that
was fun!

Nowadays there's over 100 people going to gatherings and you could
easily fill up a pack of note cards with one event.

It's ultimately OK to bring them to gathering and I don't think we
should scare people off. Perhaps discretion is paramount? If a few
folks go out in a group to box together, bring it out then!

Just our 2 cents....

-Steve and Heidi
